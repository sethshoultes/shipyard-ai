# Board Review: Shonda Rhimes
**Product:** Shipyard Client Portal
**Lens:** Narrative Arc & Retention
**Date:** 2026-04-15

---

## Story Arc: 3/10

**No "aha moment" journey.**

Landing page → Signup → Empty dashboard.
User arrives, creates account, sees... nothing.
No first project wizard. No guided intake flow. No "here's what happens next."

Dashboard says: "Projects will appear here when you submit a project intake."
Where's the intake? Not linked. Not obvious.

**Missing beats:**
- No onboarding narrative ("Welcome! Let's build your first site.")
- No status progression story (user can't visualize the journey)
- No emotional payoff (no celebration when site goes live)
- Empty states feel abandoned, not anticipatory

Signup-to-value gap is a story black hole.

---

## Retention Hooks: 2/10

**Nothing brings users back.**

After signup, what makes them return tomorrow?
- Email notifications exist (in code) but user never experiences them in demo
- No project status updates visible
- No "your site is 60% complete" progress bars
- No daily/weekly engagement triggers
- Dashboard is static, not dynamic

**What's missing:**
- Status change notifications ("Now in BUILD phase!")
- Cliffhanger moments ("Staging ready for your review")
- Weekly digests for retainer clients
- Analytics dashboard (traffic, conversions) post-launch
- "Recommended next steps" panel

Users log in once, see emptiness, never return.

---

## Content Strategy: 1/10

**No flywheel. No momentum.**

Where's the content that feeds engagement?
- No blog/changelog/updates section
- No case studies or project showcase
- No educational content ("How your site gets built")
- No social proof or testimonials
- No "recently launched" gallery

Retainer tier exists in database but offers no ongoing content value:
- No monthly recap emails
- No token usage insights
- No "this month's improvements" summary
- No community or peer visibility

Portal is transactional, not relational.
One-and-done, not ongoing relationship.

---

## Emotional Cliffhangers: 1/10

**Zero curiosity drivers.**

What makes a user wonder "what's happening with my project?"
Currently: nothing visible.

**No narrative tension:**
- No real-time build progress (just placeholder text)
- No staging preview links to explore
- No "your designer is working on the homepage" updates
- No countdown to launch
- No behind-the-scenes AI agent activity log

Dashboard should be a story unfolding.
Instead it's a waiting room with no magazines.

**Missed opportunities:**
- "Wave 2/4 in progress" → what's in Wave 3?
- "Staging ready in 6 hours" → check back soon
- "Your site got 127 visitors today" → daily ritual
- "Recommended: add testimonials section" → future project hook

---

## What Great Retention Looks Like

**Act I: Signup → First Project**
- Guided intake wizard (feels like co-creation)
- Immediate confirmation: "Your site is in the BUILD queue"
- Email: "Meet the AI agents working on your project"

**Act II: Build → Launch**
- Status updates as story beats ("Planning complete. Build started.")
- Staging preview: "Want to see it early?"
- Countdown: "Going live in 18 hours"

**Act III: Post-Launch**
- Daily digest: visitor count, form submissions
- Monthly: "Your site is growing. Here's what's working."
- Recommendations panel: "Add a blog? Integrate Stripe?"

**Ongoing:**
- Retainer clients get weekly recaps
- Token budget gamification ("You saved 50K tokens this month!")
- Changelog: "New AI capabilities unlocked"

---

## Score: **2/10**

**One-line justification:**
Portal handles transactions but tells no story—no onboarding arc, no retention hooks, no reason to return tomorrow.

---

## Critical Fixes

1. **Add intake flow CTA on dashboard** (immediately after signup)
2. **Build live status timeline** (visual progress, not just text)
3. **Email notifications for every status change** (create narrative momentum)
4. **Post-launch analytics dashboard** (daily engagement ritual)
5. **Recommendations engine** ("What's next for your site?")
6. **Weekly retainer digest** ("This week's updates + token usage")

Without these, portal is a filing cabinet.
Not a relationship. Not a story worth following.
