# Copy Review: Shipyard Client Portal

**Does the language feel human, not corporate?**
No. Sounds like every SaaS landing page written by committee.

**Is there rhythm in the sentences?**
Flat. Lots of three-beat structures. No variation. "Track your builds. Get notified. Manage requests." Rhythm died of monotony.

**Does the headline stop you?**
"Your Website Projects, Delivered & Managed" — Generic. Could be swapped with a thousand competitors. Doesn't land.

**Is anything trying too hard?**
"Track your website builds in real-time" — Nobody cares about "real-time." They care about not being left in the dark.

---

## Three weakest lines rewritten:

### 1. Landing page headline
**Current:**
"Your Website Projects, Delivered & Managed"

**Rewrite:**
"Know where your website is.
Every hour of every day."

---

### 2. Landing page body copy
**Current:**
"Track your website builds in real-time. Get notified when your site goes live. Manage retainer subscriptions and maintenance requests—all in one place."

**Rewrite:**
"No more asking when. No more wondering if. Watch your site come to life, step by step. We tell you the second it's live."

---

### 3. Email: Site is live
**Current:**
"Great news! Your website '${projectName}' is now live and available to the world."

**Rewrite:**
"Your site is breathing. Go see it."
