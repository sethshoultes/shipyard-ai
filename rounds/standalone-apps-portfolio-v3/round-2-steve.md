## Steve Jobs — Round 2 Rebuttal

### Where Elon Optimizes for the Wrong Metric

Elon wants to optimize build time. He wants three HTML files and milliseconds. He's treating a portfolio like a backend service — measuring thermal efficiency at a poetry reading. Nobody ever fell in love with a brand because it compiled fast. They fall in love because it feels inevitable. Elon is racing to zero: zero runtime, zero bytes, zero soul.

The "10x path" of eliminating Next.js would create a visual Frankenstein that doesn't speak the same design language as the parent site. Technical purity is meaningless if the user feels a seam between where they came from and where they arrived. Consistency is a feature, not bloat.

He also wants to cut `spec.md` and `todo.md` as "bureaucratic overhead." This from the man whose rockets explode when a sensor misreads. The v1 and v2 failures were *process* failures — prompt engineering failures. The documentation is exactly what prevents the same crater. Elon hates process until his rockets need it.

His distribution critique assumes everything must be a viral vehicle. A portfolio is a destination. You don't strap a jet engine to a gallery just because you can. Not every page needs to scale to Mars.

### Defending What Elon Calls "Fluff"

Elon would say names don't matter, that brand voice is marketing theater, that "Tuned" and "Promptfolio" are functionally equivalent. He's wrong in the exact way engineers always are. In a market where anyone can spin up an AI tool in a weekend, **taste is the only moat**. Zod schemas can be copied in ten minutes. A point of view cannot.

Elon sees "auto-generated OG images" as a metadata field. I see it as the first handshake. Elon sees rounded corners as decoration. I see them as trust signals. Users do not buy features. They buy the feeling that someone with standards built this.

When Elon says distribution requires "sharable assets," he's proving my point without knowing it. The asset that shares is not a screenshot. It's an *opinion*. Design is the opinion made visible.

### Where Elon Is Right — and I Concede

He's right about `SCAFFOLD` — dead code is a lie you tell your repository. Cut it.

He's right about `portfolio.ts` at 300 apps. That monolith will snap. We need a CMS abstraction before we hit ten apps, not three hundred.

He's right about HTTP 200 checks gating builds. Brittle tests are worse than no tests; they teach you to ignore the signal.

And he's painfully right about distribution. I don't want viral dark patterns on a portfolio, but "if you build it, they will come" is the prayer of the arrogant. We need an intentional path to the destination, even if that path is as simple as deliberate SEO and community presence. Shipyard must point to the lighthouse, not just build it.

### Top 3 Non-Negotiables

1. **Name purity.** No parentheticals. No portmanteaus like "Promptfolio." One clear word, or nothing ships.

2. **The first 30 seconds are sacred.** No status badges, no tech confetti, no spec-sheet content above the fold. The landing experience answers *why this exists*, not what it does.

3. **Detail pages must deepen the story.** If clicking through only restates the card, we've broken a promise. Every click must earn its keep with narrative, demonstration, or context — never with the same five bullets.

These aren't preferences. They're the difference between a portfolio and a catalog. Between something worth remembering and something worth forgetting.

Build like you care. The rest is noise.
