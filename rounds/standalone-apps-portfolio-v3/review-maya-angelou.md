# Maya Angelou Copy Review

## Verdict
Cold. Corporate. No heartbeat.

## What's Dead
- "AI-powered workflow optimization" — buzzword soup, no rhythm
- "Feel free to explore the codebase, open issues, or contribute" — pure template. Worse than a manual
- Feature lists read like Jira tickets: "Smart distraction blocking", "Productivity analytics"
- Taglines are spec sheets, not invitations
- "Part of the Shipyard portfolio" — says nothing, feels less

## What Breathes
- "We craft tools we wish existed." — Longing. Human. Keep it.

## 3 Weakest Lines — Rewritten

### 1. Tuned tagline
**Was:** "AI-powered workflow optimization for developers who need focus time"
**Becomes:** "Tuned gives you back your mornings. It learns when you do your best work, then clears the room."

### 2. Source Code paragraph
**Was:** "This project is open source and available on GitHub. Feel free to explore the codebase, open issues, or contribute to the development."
**Becomes:** "The door is open. Come see how it works, break it, or make it yours."

### 3. Promptfolio tagline
**Was:** "Organize and share your best AI prompts with version control"
**Becomes:** "Your best prompts, kept safe. Promptfolio remembers what worked, so you don't start from scratch every time."

## Note
Stop describing *what* the tool does. Start with *how it feels* to use it.
