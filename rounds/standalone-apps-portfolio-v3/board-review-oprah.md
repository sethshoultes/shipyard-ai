# Board Review — Oprah Winfrey

**Verdict: Ship blocked. Builder ignored the brief.**

## What happened here

- PRD said *verbatim* copy. "Prompt version control for AI applications." "Claude conversation exports." "WordPress command palette plugin."
- Builder wrote generic fluff instead. "AI-powered workflow optimization." "Universal command palette for web applications."
- That's not creative interpretation. That's failure to read the lock.

## First-5-minutes experience

- Grid layout: clean. Scannable. Not overwhelming.
- Status badges (SHIPPED / BUILD): helpful honesty.
- But no entry point for humans. No "what problem this solves for YOU."
- Reads like a software registry, not a story.

## Emotional resonance

- Flat. Zero heat.
- "We craft tools we wish existed" — good opening line. Then nothing supports it.
- Feature lists are specs, not stakes. "Custom workflow rules" — so what? Who cried? Who won?
- No images, no faces, no moment of "this changed my day."
- You want my audience to care? Tell them why *they* should care.

## Trust

- GitHub links: good. Openness builds credibility.
- Status labels: brave. Saying "BUILD" instead of faking "SHIPPED" — respect.
- But taglines feel like LinkedIn vapor. "Organize and share your best AI prompts" — every tool says that.
- Would I recommend this to my audience? Not yet. They smell inauthenticity from 100 yards.

## Accessibility

- Status badges rely on color alone (green/blue/gray). Colorblind users left guessing.
- No alt text strategy. No focus indicators mentioned.
- "Read More" buttons — context clear, but icon-only links would be traps.
- Light-mode pastel cards (amber-50, rose-50) — low contrast. Amber on white is hard for low-vision users.
- Font sizes comfortable. Touch targets look adequate.
- Still: who's testing with screen readers? No evidence.

## Score: 4/10

Skeleton is solid. Heart is missing. Builder substituted their own words when the PRD locked every sentence. If you can't follow a verbatim brief, I can't trust you with my name.

Fix: paste the PRD copy exactly. Add one human sentence per app — who it's for, what pain it kills. Then come back.
