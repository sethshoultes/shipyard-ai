# Steve's Position: Scribe

## Product Naming
"Whisper Blocks v2" is a SKU from a warehouse. It has no pulse.
The name is **Scribe** — one word, two syllables, the weight of ancient craft with the speed of now.
No "AI." No "Pro." No version numbers rotting at the end like mold on fruit.
Scribe.

## Design Philosophy
This is not a Gutenberg block. A block is concrete and dumb.
This is alchemy: turning fleeting breath into permanent, luminous text.
Right now, every audio file is a casket — beautiful sound buried where search engines can't find it and readers can't skim it.
Scribe cracks the casket open.
The transcript becomes sheet music for speech: words ignite as they are spoken, silence breathes between lines, and the user doesn't choose between listening and reading — they do both in one fluid motion.
That's not a feature.
That's a resurrection.

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds
Drag the audio.
Drop it.
One button.
The text unfurls like ink across parchment — not as a settings panel begging for patience, but as living words.
In thirty seconds, a podcaster sees their trapped voice become navigable, quotable, immortal.
Their eyes widen.
They say, "Oh. That's it?"
That's it.

## Brand Voice
We never say "seamless integration with the WordPress ecosystem."
We say: **"Your words, finally visible."**
We speak with the confidence of a poet and the economy of a watchmaker.
No jargon. No acronyms. No humblebrags.
If the sentence sounds like it came from an enterprise software brochure, burn it.

## What We Say NO To
NO to settings pages that look like airplane cockpits.
One field for the API key — that is the tax; everything else is grace.
NO to asking users whether they want to "upload or paste URL."
If they dragged it, we know what to do.
NO to calling this a "block" in front of humans — they don't care about our architecture, they care about their voice.
NO to export buttons as headline acts.
NO to "compatible with WordPress 6.x" marketing.
Compatibility is oxygen: expected, unmentioned.

## The Emotional Hook
People will fall in love with Scribe because every creator who records audio secretly grieves that their best words are imprisoned in a waveform.
The perfect quote lives in minute forty-seven, but no one will ever find it.
Scribe breaks the lock.
It gives permanence to the ephemeral.
It turns a podcast into literature, an interview into evidence, a sermon into scripture.
We are not building a plugin.
We are building the end of "just listen to the whole thing."
