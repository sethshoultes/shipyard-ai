# Board Review: Shipyard Maintenance Subscription
**Reviewer:** Oprah Winfrey
**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Product:** Shipyard Care (formerly "Maintenance")

---

## First-5-Minutes Experience

**Verdict: Overwhelmed, not welcomed.**

New user lands confused:
- Token pricing requires mental math (15K per PRD? How many PRDs is 100K?)
- "Incident-only reporting" feels reactive, not protective
- Three email templates but no human warmth in activation flow
- Referral system front-loaded before trust established
- No onboarding that shows you "what good looks like"

First impression: technical subscription, not emotional partnership.

Missing: "Here's what happens after you sign up. Here's your first check-in. Here's how we'll communicate."

---

## Emotional Resonance

**Verdict: Branding promises care. Execution delivers monitoring.**

Copy says "We've got this" but architecture says "You'll track token budgets."

**What works:**
- Welcome email tone: calm, confident
- Tagline: "No dashboards. No anxiety. Just care."
- Incident report structure: clear, resolved-focused

**What doesn't:**
- Tokens create cognitive load (what do I get for $500?)
- Incident-only reporting feels distant (where's the relationship?)
- No monthly "everything's great" touchpoint
- Referral system prioritized over customer delight

I'd recommend this to founders who want infrastructure, not founders who want peace of mind.

Gap: Care implies proactive. Product feels reactive.

---

## Trust

**Verdict: Would not recommend — yet.**

**Red flags:**
- Token overage model invites surprise billing anxiety
- Priority queue risks two-tier service perception (free users left behind?)
- No SLA for incident response time
- Referral credits cap at 50% (feels restrictive for loyal customers)
- "We'll fix it before you notice" promise not technically verifiable

**Trust builders:**
- Stripe integration (legitimate billing)
- Database architecture solid (can scale)
- Email templates professional

Would need proof:
- Show me 3 months of incident resolution times
- Show me free tier median wait stayed under 3 days
- Show me customer testimonials about "peace of mind"

Board question Warren would ask: "What happens when token estimation is wrong?"
No answer in PRD.

---

## Accessibility

**Who's left out:**

1. **Non-technical founders**
   - Token terminology assumes AI fluency
   - No plain-English translation ("100K tokens = roughly 6-7 updates/month")

2. **Budget-conscious customers**
   - $500/month barrier for post-launch support
   - No fractional tier ($200 for 1 revision/month?)

3. **Visual learners**
   - All text emails, no dashboard/status page
   - No visual token usage meter

4. **Anxious customers**
   - Incident-only communication increases worry between emails
   - No "everything's healthy" reassurance rhythm

5. **Non-English speakers**
   - No localization mentioned

6. **Customers without shipped projects**
   - Can't subscribe before launch (locks out pre-planning)

---

## Score: **6/10**

**Justification:**
Strong infrastructure, weak emotional delivery — solves business problem, not customer anxiety.

---

## What Would Make This a 9/10

**Immediate fixes:**
1. Replace token language with outcome language ("~6 revisions/month")
2. Add monthly health email even when nothing's wrong
3. Create onboarding checklist (visual, human, reassuring)
4. Remove referral CTA from welcome email (build trust first)
5. Add response-time SLA to incident reports (24 hours? 48 hours?)

**Long-term:**
1. Visual dashboard (even if simple) for accessibility
2. Fractional tier for budget accessibility
3. Customer stories section on marketing page
4. "How we protected you this month" monthly report (not just incidents)
5. Token-to-outcome translator ("You have enough for 3 feature adds")

**The gap:**
Product named "Care" must feel caring from minute one.
Right now it feels like "Monitoring with Better Branding."

---

**Recommendation:** Launch after addressing emotional onboarding gap. Founders will churn if first month feels transactional.
