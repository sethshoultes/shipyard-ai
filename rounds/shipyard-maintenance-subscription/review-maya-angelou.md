# Maya Angelou Review: Shipyard Care Copy

## Verdict
**Mostly human. A few lines pretend too hard.**

---

## Human vs. Corporate

**Human:**
- "You're all set. Your site is now under care."
- "We've got this."
- "If something breaks, we'll fix it before you notice."
- "No dashboards. No anxiety. Just care."

**Corporate creep:**
- "Get priority processing with dedicated capacity" — sounds like AWS
- "Submit updates anytime through your existing flow" — what flow?
- "For every new subscriber you refer, you'll earn $100/month credit" — fine print voice

---

## Rhythm

**Works:**
- "No dashboards. No anxiety. Just care." — three beats, clean landing
- "We've got this." — mic drop, done
- "Your site is back to normal. No action needed on your end." — symmetry

**Flat:**
- "Submit updates anytime through your existing flow" — too many syllables, no pulse
- "Receive incident reports only when needed (not noise)" — parenthetical kills momentum
- "Everything is still running smoothly, but you'll want to know where you stand." — meandering

---

## Headlines

**Welcome email:** "Welcome to Shipyard Care"
- Stops you? No. Expected.
- Better? "Your site is under care now."

**Incident report:** "[Shipyard Care] Issue detected and resolved"
- Stops you? Maybe. But bracket kills it.
- Better? "Fixed before you noticed."

**Token warning:** "[Shipyard Care] Token balance update"
- Stops you? No. Sounds like a receipt.
- Better? "Heads up: Running low this month."

---

## Trying Too Hard

Lines that want applause:

1. **"Share Shipyard Care with colleagues"**
   - Trying: recruitment language
   - Says: we need your help
   - Feels: desperate

2. **"No setup required. No configuration steps. Just peace of mind."**
   - Trying: rhythmic trinity
   - Says: we read marketing books
   - Feels: rehearsed

3. **"Your options:"**
   - Trying: empowerment framing
   - Says: you have choices!
   - Feels: customer service script

---

## 3 Weakest Lines Rewritten

### 1. Original:
> "For every new subscriber you refer, you'll earn $100/month credit on your subscription."

**Rewrite:**
> "Refer someone. We'll credit you $100/month."

*Why: Direct. No "earn," no "on your subscription." Just the deal.*

---

### 2. Original:
> "Submit updates anytime through your existing flow"

**Rewrite:**
> "Send updates the same way you always do."

*Why: "Existing flow" is jargon. People don't think in flows.*

---

### 3. Original:
> "Just a heads up — your token balance is running low this month. Everything is still running smoothly, but you'll want to know where you stand."

**Rewrite:**
> "You're running low on tokens this month. Everything's fine. Just wanted you to know."

*Why: Say it once. "Everything's fine" > "still running smoothly." Shorter breath.*

---

## Final Note

Brand voice ("We've got this") works when short.

Falls apart when explaining.

Trust brevity.
