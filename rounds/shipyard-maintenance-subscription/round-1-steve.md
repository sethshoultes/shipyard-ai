# Steve Jobs: Design & Brand Position
## Shipyard Maintenance Subscription

---

## Product Naming: **"Shipyard Care"**

Not "Maintenance." Not "Maintenance Plus." **Care.**

Maintenance is what you do to a lawnmower. Care is what you give to something you love. We're not fixing broken things — we're nurturing living products. One word. Emotional. Human.

The tiers? **Care** and **Care Pro.** Simple. Clear. No "Plus" nonsense.

---

## Design Philosophy: Invisible Excellence

This product must be **felt, not seen.** The best maintenance is the kind you never think about — until the one day something breaks and it's already fixed.

Like the Genius Bar. You don't celebrate going there. You celebrate that when you need it, it *just works.* That's the bar here.

The health report isn't a dashboard. It's not a spreadsheet. It's **a quarterly checkup from your doctor.** Three sentences: "Your site is healthy." Or: "We found two issues. We fixed one. The other needs your input." Done.

Insanely great = **invisible until essential.**

---

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

You open the email. Subject line: **"Your site is healthy."**

Three lines. Green checkmark. One button: "View Report."

Click it. Clean page. No jargon. No clutter. Your site's name at the top. Three metrics — uptime, speed, links. All green, or one red with a plain-English explanation: "We found a broken link on your About page. We'll fix it this week."

**That's it.** No login. No dashboard bloat. No "enterprise platform" nonsense.

If something's wrong, we tell you. If it's fine, we leave you alone. Respect for the user's time is the ultimate luxury.

---

## Brand Voice: The Trusted Co-Pilot

We don't upsell. We don't panic. We don't use words like "optimize" or "leverage."

We speak like a pilot during turbulence: **calm, confident, in control.**

"We've got this." "Your site's running smoothly." "We spotted an issue and fixed it."

Never: "You have 3 critical vulnerabilities!" Never: "Upgrade now to unlock premium insights!"

**We're the friend who changes your oil without being asked.** No drama. No guilt. Just care.

---

## What to Say NO To

### NO to dashboards.
Dashboards are where products go to die. Every SaaS company thinks users want "visibility." They don't. They want **results.** Emails, not logins.

### NO to tiered feature gates.
"Unlock advanced monitoring with Pro!" is a scam. If it's important, everyone gets it. We tier on **volume** (rounds per month), not **access to basic dignity.**

### NO to "revision rounds."
That phrase is garbage. It's procurement language. Call them **"updates."** Normal humans don't think in "rounds." They think: "I need to change my pricing page."

### NO to SLAs in marketing.
"48-hour start time" is for enterprise contracts, not humans. Say: **"We'll start within 2 days."** Or better: "We respond fast."

### NO to "founding subscriber discounts."
Desperation pricing. If it's worth $500, charge $500. If it's not, don't launch it.

---

## The Emotional Hook: Peace of Mind

People don't buy maintenance. They buy **the ability to stop worrying.**

The hook isn't "priority queue access." It's this:

**"Your site won't break. And if it does, we'll fix it before you notice."**

That's the iPhone moment. That's why people pay $500/month. Not for "2 revision rounds." For the *feeling* that someone's watching.

We're selling sleep. We're selling the confidence to ignore your website because you know it's in good hands.

---

## Final Word

This product lives or dies on **trust and invisibility.**

If users think about us every day, we failed. If they forget we exist until something breaks — and we've already fixed it — we won.

Make it simple. Make it human. Make it care.

**Anything else is just another subscription box they'll cancel in three months.**
