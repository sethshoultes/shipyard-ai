# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
## Positions: Deploy Verification

### Product Naming: PROOF
One word. Five letters. Hard consonant. Memorable.
We don't "run post-deploy verification steps" — we deliver Proof.
When you ship, you get Proof your customers can see it.
Anything longer than one syllable is a committee decision.
Proof is a promise.

### Design Philosophy: The Invisible Seatbelt
What makes this insanely great? Invisibility.
When it works, you don't see it.
When it fails, it's impossible to ignore.
It's the seatbelt warning on a plane — silent until it saves your life.
We are not building a dashboard.
We are building a moment of absolute certainty.
Green means your users see it. Red means stop, fix it, now.
No charts. No noise. Just truth.

### User Experience: One Word in 30 Seconds
You hit deploy.
Thirty seconds later, one word appears: "Verified."
Below it, your domain and a timestamp. That's it.
If it fails, the screen turns red and speaks in English:
"shipyard.company is serving a 404. Your DNS points to the wrong place."
No stack traces. No log vomit.
The first 30 seconds should feel like closing a door and hearing the lock click —
secure, final, done.

### Brand Voice: The Genius at the Bar
Confident. Direct. Almost parental.
Like the best Apple Genius: "We checked. You're good."
Or: "Wait — something's wrong. Here's exactly what to fix."
No passive voice. No "an error was encountered."
We speak like a human who cares.
We say "Your domain is broken" because euphemisms are cowardly.

### What to Say NO To: The DevOps Trap
NO to "verification dashboards."
NO to "configurable alert thresholds."
NO to retry policies exposed as knobs.
NO to cron syntax.
NO to Slack/Discord/PagerDuty integrations as the primary experience.
If it fails, we tell YOU directly.
We are not building a monitoring platform —
we are building a moment of truth.
Say NO to feature creep disguised as flexibility.
Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication,
and that means removing everything that lets a customer 404 in silence.

### Emotional Hook: Dignity at Launch
Nobody wants to be the person who shipped a 404 to their boss,
their customer, their mother.
This protects your dignity at the moment of maximum exposure — the launch.
It's the friend who taps your shoulder and says "your fly is down"
before you walk on stage.
That's not a feature. That's love.
People will love Proof because it lets them sleep.
Because launching should feel like flying, not falling.
