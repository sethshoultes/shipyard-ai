# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

**"WP Showcase" is a tragedy.** It sounds like a committee chose it. We need one word that captures the moment a plugin author finally sees their work presented the way it deserves. I want **"Stage."** One word. A noun and a verb. It implies performance, spotlight, the moment the curtain rises. Not "WP Stage." Just **Stage.** Say it out loud. It breathes.

## Design Philosophy

Most WordPress plugins look like they were designed in a parking garage under fluorescent lights. We are building the **BMW of demo pages** — precision engineering wrapped in visceral beauty. Every animation must feel physical, like touching mercury. The demo site is not an *accessory* to the plugin. It **IS** the plugin. The first impression is the product. If it doesn't make the author gasp, we have failed.

## The First 30 Seconds

You install Stage. You type one slug. And suddenly — your plugin, which has lived in the gray purgatory of wp-admin, is standing on a white gallery floor under a single perfect spotlight. No onboarding wizard. No "choose your template." No configuration drudgery. It is the feeling of turning on the original iPhone and realizing every other phone you have ever used was broken.

## Brand Voice

We speak like a confident friend who happens to be a world-class designer. No "leverage our robust solutions." No "seamlessly integrate." We say: *"Your plugin is beautiful. Now your demo page is too."* Short sentences. One-syllable words where possible. We let the product speak, and we get out of the way.

## What We Say NO To

- **NO** admin panels with fifty toggles. If it needs a toggle, we chose wrong.
- **NO** "powered by Stage" badges polluting the author's stage. This is their moment, not ours.
- **NO** beige. No off-gray. No WordPress-admin blue bleeding into our canvas.
- **NO** feature bloat to chase enterprise contracts. One thing, perfect.
- **NO** iframes that feel like peering through a dirty window. The sandbox must feel native, or it must die.

## The Emotional Hook

Every plugin developer — even the ones who swear they "only care about backend architecture" — has a secret ache to show their work with pride. Stage lets a solo developer in Krakow stand shoulder-to-shoulder with Apple on the same morning. It gives them **dignity**. It makes them feel like the artist they actually are. That is not a feature. That is the whole game.
