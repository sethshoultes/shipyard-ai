# Board Review — Shonda Rhimes
**Project:** shipyard-revenue-deployment
**Verdict:** Infrastructure without a pulse.

---

## Story Arc
- No arc. Signup → portal is a plumbing diagram, not a journey.
- No protagonist transformation. Customer starts as "lead," ends as "lead with badge."
- Missing: inciting incident, rising action, climax. The "aha moment" is a green SSL badge. That's not an aha moment. That's a receipt.

## Retention Hooks
- Health checks every 6 hours = data, not drama.
- Day-7 email asks "How's traffic?" then goes silent for months.
- No reason to return tomorrow. Portal is a read-only report card.
- Tokenized portal = no identity, no progress, no streak, no investment.

## Content Flywheel
- None. Zero.
- No case studies. No "before/after" stories. No user voice.
- Lifecycle emails are all reactive, never additive. Nothing to share, forward, or discuss.
- PRD has a content strategy the way a vending machine has a kitchen.

## Emotional Cliffhangers
- Welcome email: "Here's your URL." End scene. Roll credits.
- Day-7: "Any tweaks needed?" No stakes. No consequence for ignoring it.
- Health badges: red means panic, but no narrative rescue. Just... red.
- Nothing makes the user wonder what Chapter 2 holds.

## What's Missing (The Fix)
- **Serialized onboarding:** Week 1 = setup mystery. Week 2 = first win. Week 3 = unlock hidden feature.
- **Character stakes:** "Your site is live" → "Your site is live. 3 things will break in 30 days if ignored. Here's #1."
- **Content engine:** Every shipped site becomes a case study. Every case study becomes an email. Every email becomes a lead.
- **Progression:** Tier names (Keep/Grow/Scale) are good. But portal needs to show trajectory, not just status.

## Score
**3/10.**
Solid plumbing. Zero plot. You built the stage but forgot the play.
