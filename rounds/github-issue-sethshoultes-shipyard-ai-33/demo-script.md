# Forge Demo Script

**Runtime:** 2 minutes
**Tone:** Human, conversational, urgent-then-relieved

---

## SCENE 1: THE PROBLEM (0:00 - 0:25)

[SCREEN: A website contact page showing a broken, ugly form. Missing fields. Misaligned labels. The submit button is gray and sad.]

**NARRATOR:**
You know this form.

[SCREEN: Zoom in on the form. It's labeled "Contact Us" but looks like it was built in 2009.]

**NARRATOR:**
You've been meaning to fix it for six months. Your designer mocked something up in Figma. Your developer said they'd "get to it." Your marketing team keeps asking when the new lead capture form will be ready.

[SCREEN: Email inbox. Subject lines pile up: "RE: Form update?" / "Following up on form redesign" / "Can we get this done before the launch?"]

**NARRATOR:**
And here's the thing nobody wants to say out loud: building a form shouldn't be this hard. It's a form. Name, email, message. Maybe a phone number. But somehow it requires a designer, a developer, a backend engineer, and three meetings.

[SCREEN: Calendar with meetings blocked: "Form Requirements Review" / "Form Technical Spec" / "Form Styling Sync"]

**NARRATOR:**
What if it didn't?

---

## SCENE 2: THE SOLUTION (0:25 - 1:25)

[SCREEN: Clean EmDash admin dashboard. Cursor clicks "Plugins" → "Forge" → "Create New Form"]

**NARRATOR:**
This is Forge. It's a form builder. But not the kind where you drag boxes around and pray they line up.

[SCREEN: Empty form editor. A single input field with placeholder text: "Ask something..."]

**NARRATOR:**
You just... ask.

[SCREEN: User types: "What's your email?"]

**NARRATOR:**
Watch this.

[SCREEN: User hits Enter. The field transforms: "Email" label appears, email validation icon, placeholder shows "your@email.com". A subtle green badge reads "High confidence."]

**NARRATOR:**
Forge understood. It created an email field. With validation. With the right placeholder. Because you asked for an email—and it listened.

[SCREEN: User types: "What's your phone number?"]

**NARRATOR:**
Let's keep going.

[SCREEN: Phone field appears. Type "tel", placeholder "(555) 123-4567".]

[SCREEN: User types: "Tell us about your project"]

**NARRATOR:**
One more.

[SCREEN: A textarea field appears. "Message" label. Placeholder: "Enter your message here..."]

**NARRATOR:**
Three fields. Fifteen seconds. No meetings.

[SCREEN: User clicks "Theme" section. Color picker appears.]

**NARRATOR:**
Pick your brand color.

[SCREEN: User selects a deep blue. The form preview on the right updates instantly—submit button turns blue, focus states glow blue.]

**NARRATOR:**
Drop in a logo if you want.

[SCREEN: User pastes a logo URL. Logo appears above the form.]

**NARRATOR:**
And publish.

[SCREEN: User clicks "Publish." A toast notification: "Form published. Live at /contact"]

---

## SCENE 3: THE WOW MOMENT (1:25 - 2:00)

[SCREEN: The live form on the website. Clean. Modern. Beautiful. A user fills it out: "Sarah Chen" / "sarah@acme.co" / "(415) 555-0123" / "We'd love to discuss a partnership."]

**NARRATOR:**
Here's where it gets good.

[SCREEN: User clicks "Send Message." Success message appears: "Thank you! We'll get back to you soon."]

[SCREEN: Cut to: Admin's email inbox. New email arrives. Subject: "New submission: Contact Form"]

**NARRATOR:**
The moment someone submits—

[SCREEN: Open the email. It's gorgeous. Branded header with the logo and blue gradient. Clean table showing each field and response. Timestamp. Submission ID.]

**NARRATOR:**
—you get this. Not a plain text dump. Not a JSON blob. A beautiful, branded notification that looks like you hired someone to design it.

[SCREEN: Back to Forge admin. Click "Submissions." Table shows the new entry. Click "Export CSV."]

**NARRATOR:**
And when your marketing team asks for the data?

[SCREEN: CSV downloads. Opens in a spreadsheet. Clean columns: Submission ID, Submitted At, Name, Email, Phone, Message.]

**NARRATOR:**
One click.

[SCREEN: Return to the live form on the website. Camera slowly pulls back to show it embedded perfectly on the page.]

**NARRATOR:**
This is Forge. Forms that build themselves. Notifications that look like you care. Data that's ready when you need it.

[SCREEN: Forge logo. Tagline: "Just ask."]

**NARRATOR:**
Just ask.
