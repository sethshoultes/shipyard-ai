# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

"LocalGenius" is a forgettable compound noun that sounds like a Yelp knockoff from 2003. It has the emotional resonance of a tax form. The name is **Sous**. One word. French, intimate, culinary. A sous-chef makes the chef look brilliant without ever stepping into the spotlight. That is this product: it works while you sleep, it never takes credit, and it never calls in sick. Sous IS the product. The widget, the voice, the soul — everything. LocalGenius dies today.

## Design Philosophy

This is not software. It is a concierge. Every pixel should feel like a perfectly pressed white shirt. The design philosophy is **inevitability** — the user should never ask "how do I..." because the answer was already there, smiling, waiting. The widget must be so light, so fast, so invisible that visitors forget it is code. Under 20KB? That is the ceiling, not the goal. It should feel like breathing.

## User Experience

The first 30 seconds must be magic. A restaurant owner installs the plugin, and before they can panic, Sous has already read their website, guessed their business, and pre-loaded their FAQs. It feels like someone walked into their restaurant and already knows the menu. Magic. Not setup. Not configuration. Magic. If they have to think, we have failed.

## Brand Voice

Sous speaks like the best maître d' in the city: warm, brief, confident. No "AI-powered insights." No "leverage." No "we're excited to announce." Sous says, "Your reservations are handled. Your parking question? Already answered." Period. We are not a tech company selling to businesses. We are the business's best employee.

## What to Say NO To

NO to a dashboard with 47 metrics. NO to "AI" in any customer-facing copy. NO to a settings panel that lets them change the border radius. NO to the engagement system. NO to the mobile app. NO to features that impress engineers and terrify restaurant owners. Every feature we ship is a burden disguised as a gift. Remove the border-radius picker. Remove the "custom greeting" field. Sous already knows what to say.

## The Emotional Hook

Every small business owner is terrified they will miss the one question that costs them a customer. Sous is the first good night's sleep they have had since they opened. It is pride without the work. It is having a five-star front-of-house staff at fast-food prices. People will not love this because it is a chatbot. They will love it because it makes them feel like the owner they always wanted to be: present, attentive, un-fuck-withable.
