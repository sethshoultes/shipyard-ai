# Sous — 2-Minute Demo Script

NARRATOR
It's 7:45. Dinner service starts in fifteen minutes. Maria's phone buzzes. Again. "What are your hours?" She thumbs past it. Buzz. "Do you take reservations?" She's got flour on her hands, a line out the door, and she's still the FAQ.

[SCREEN: Black. Phone notification stack growing: "Hours?", "Parking?", "Vegan options?"]

NARRATOR
Every local business owner is their own call center. Maria didn't open a restaurant to type "Yes, we have a parking lot" forty times a week.

[SCREEN: WordPress admin. Plugin page. Cursor clicks "Install" on Sous.]

NARRATOR
She installs Sous. That's it. Plugin reads her homepage. H1 says "Trattoria Maria." Meta says "Italian restaurant, downtown." Sous guesses: restaurant. Proposes five questions. Maria hits "Yes, that's me." Then "Activate."

[SCREEN: Onboarding view. Business type detected: "restaurant." Five proposed FAQs listed. "Yes, that's me" button clicks. "Activate" button clicks.]

NARRATOR
Twelve seconds. No coding. No "train your AI." No configuring intents or entities or whatever the hell an entity is.

[SCREEN: Maria's website. A chat bubble fades up in the bottom-right corner.]

NARRATOR
Now: customer on her site asks, "What are your hours?"

[SCREEN: Widget opens. User types: "What are your hours?" Hits enter.]

NARRATOR
Answer comes back before the typing dots finish their second bounce. Because Sous cached that. Pre-loaded it. Knew a restaurant gets asked hours forty times a week.

[SCREEN: Assistant bubble appears: "We're open Tue–Sun, 5 PM to 10 PM."]

NARRATOR
But here's the thing. Customer asks something weird. "Do you host rehearsal dinners?"

[SCREEN: User types: "Do you host rehearsal dinners?"]

NARRATOR
That's not in the cache. Sous doesn't shrug. It routes to the model. Two-second timeout. If it's slow, it falls back graceful. Captures email. Maria follows up later.

[SCREEN: Typing indicator pulses. Then answer appears.]

NARRATOR
It answers. Of course it answers.

[SCREEN: Dashboard. One toggle: "Widget active." One number: "12 questions answered this week." One button: "Upgrade billing."]

NARRATOR
And Maria's dashboard? One toggle. One number. One button. She's not running NASA. She just wants to know it's working.

[SCREEN: Close-up on the number. "12."]

NARRATOR
Twelve conversations she didn't have while the salmon was burning.

[SCREEN: Maria's phone. She flips it face-down on the counter. Fade to black.]

NARRATOR
Sous. Your business already knows the answers. We just put them where your customers are.
