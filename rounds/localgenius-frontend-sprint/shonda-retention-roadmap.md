# Shonda Retention Roadmap

*What keeps users coming back. Written like a showrunner's season bible.*

---

## The Retention Thesis

Users do not churn because a button is the wrong shade of slate. They churn because the story ends.

Sous is not a utility. It is a character in the owner's daily drama: the trusted employee who never calls in sick, never snaps at a customer, and never forgets the specials. Retention is not about features. It is about *relationship*. If the owner checks the dashboard and feels relief, pride, or curiosity, they stay. If they feel nothing — or worse, if they feel embarrassed by a canned answer — they uninstall before the season finale.

The first 30 seconds is the pilot. v1.1 is season two. It has to raise the stakes.

---

## The Emotional Arcs (Why They Stay)

### Arc 1: The Relief Hook
Every week, the owner must feel that Sous carried weight they did not have to lift. The "one number" is the cold open. It must land with impact.

- **v1.1 rewrite:** The number is no longer just "Questions answered." It is "Conversations you did not have." The dashboard whispers: *"You slept through 47 this week."* That is the hook.

### Arc 2: The Character Growth
A static concierge is a dead character. Sous must get smarter, warmer, and more *theirs* over time. The owner must notice the difference between week one and week six.

- **v1.1 engine:** Every answered question trains the voice. Every missed question is a cliffhanger: *"Someone asked something I didn't know. Want to teach me?"*

### Arc 3: The Season Premiere
Local businesses live by seasons: patio openings, holiday hours, prom season, tax-time appointments. A static FAQ bank dies in week three because the world changes and the widget does not.

- **v1.1 rhythm:** Seasonal answer packs drop like new episodes. The owner does not write them from scratch. Sous proposes: *"It's December. Want me to mention your holiday schedule?"*

### Arc 4: The Water-Cooler Moment
The owner must want to brag about Sous. Not because of a backlink — because the widget made them look brilliant to a customer who asked something weird at 11 PM.

- **v1.1 moment:** The invisible save. When the LLM fallback handles a rehearsal-dinner question the owner never anticipated, Sous sends a private note: *"Handled one you hadn't trained. Here's what I said."* Pride is a stronger retention drug than any analytics panel.

## v1.1 Feature Episodes

### Episode 1: Smart Misses (The Learning Loop)
**Premise:** Every KV cache miss is a writing prompt, not a failure.

- When three visitors ask the same unseeded question in a week, Sous surfaces it to the owner: *"Three people asked about rehearsal dinners. Want to add an answer?"*
- One-click approval. Sous drafts the reply in the owner's established voice.
- **Retention mechanic:** The owner becomes the co-writer. They are not configuring software; they are training a character.

### Episode 2: Voice Memory (The Ownership Arc)
**Premise:** Sous remembers how the owner speaks.

- Owner edits any answer once. Sous extracts tone, warmth, and brevity patterns.
- Future auto-drafts mimic that voice. A bakery owner who writes "We'd love to bake something special for you" will never see "Please submit your catering inquiry through the portal."
- **Retention mechanic:** The widget becomes irreplaceable because it sounds like *them*, not like a template.

### Episode 3: The Weekly Sous Report (The Recap)
**Premise:** One sentence. No dashboard. No noise.

- Every Monday at 8 AM, an email or in-app card: *"You slept through 47 questions this week. Your busiest night was Friday. Someone asked about gluten-free options 6 times — I answered perfectly every time."*
- **Retention mechanic:** Habit formation through narrative. The owner starts their week with a win.

### Episode 4: Seasonal Drops (The Premiere Event)
**Premise:** The FAQ bank must feel alive.

- Sous detects calendar events (holidays, local weather, known business cycles) and proposes answer updates.
- *"It's Memorial Day weekend. Want me to mention your Monday hours?"* One tap. The widget stays current without the owner becoming a content manager.
- **Retention mechanic:** Anticipation. Owners wait for the next helpful drop.

### Episode 5: The Quiet Save (The Invisible Hero)
**Premise:** LLM fallback is not a bug. It is a brag.

- When fallback succeeds on an unseeded question, Sous logs it privately.
- Weekly report includes one "surprise save": *"Someone asked if you host baby showers. I said yes and mentioned your private room. They left their email."*
- **Retention mechanic:** Trust in the unseen. The owner learns that Sous handles edge cases they never thought to script.

### Episode 6: Reactivation Hooks (The Comeback Story)
**Premise:** Paused widgets are not dead. They are dormant.

- If a widget is disabled for 7 days, Sous sends one gentle nudge: *"12 people visited your site this week. Your hours were the #1 question. Want me back?"*
- No guilt. No discount bribes. Just a fact that makes the owner feel the loss.
- **Retention mechanic:** FOMO without manipulation.

### Episode 7: Category Echo (The Ensemble Cast)
**Premise:** Nearby businesses teach each other, anonymized and safe.

- If three restaurants in the same zip code add "outdoor seating" answers, Sous proposes it to the fourth: *"Other restaurants near you are talking about patio season. Want to join the conversation?"*
- **Retention mechanic:** Network effect. The product gets better because others use it. Jensen's data flywheel, delivered as social proof.

## What Gets Cut (The B-Plots That Kill Momentum)

- **Badges and milestones.** A restaurant owner does not need a "100 Questions Answered" badge. They need sleep. Gamification is tone-deaf in this universe.
- **Benchmark percentiles.** "You answered more questions than 68% of businesses" is competitive anxiety, not relief.
- **Rich text editors.** If the owner is formatting HTML, the story has already jumped the shark.
- **Annual billing nudges.** Retention is earned monthly through value, not locked in through accounting.

---

## The Season Finale (90-Day Retention Gate)

At day 90, a retained user must be able to say three things:

1. *"Sous sounds like me now."* (Voice Memory + edits)
2. *"It handled something I never taught it."* (LLM fallback + Quiet Save)
3. *"I look forward to the Monday recap."* (Weekly Sous Report)

If all three are true, they do not churn. They renew. They tell the table next to them.
