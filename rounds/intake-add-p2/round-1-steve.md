# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

**Intake.**

One word. Verb and noun. It's what happens when you breathe. When you take something in. This system *inhales* issues from the chaos of GitHub and transforms them into actionable work. Don't call it "GitHub Issue Poller" or "Priority Label Handler." Those are descriptions, not names. Intake is a living thing.

## Design Philosophy

This is plumbing. And the best plumbing is invisible.

The insanely great thing here isn't what we're adding—it's that nobody should ever notice it working. The moment a developer labels something `p2`, it should flow through the system like water through pipes. No friction. No thought. No "why didn't my issue get picked up?"

**Great infrastructure disappears.** It just works. That's the design philosophy.

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

There is no first 30 seconds. That's the point.

The user labels an issue `p2`. They close their laptop. They go home. The next morning, there's a PRD waiting for them. They never saw Intake. They never configured Intake. They never wondered if Intake was running.

*That's* the experience. The absence of an experience. The removal of a gap they didn't even know existed.

## Brand Voice

Intake doesn't speak. It acts.

If it had to speak, it would say: "I got it." Not "I have received your request and am processing it according to the configured priority matrix." Just: "I got it."

Quiet confidence. Reliability so deep it doesn't need to announce itself.

## What to Say NO To

**No to configuration.** Don't ask users which priorities to watch. Watch all of them. p0, p1, p2. Done.

**No to dashboards.** Don't build a status page showing "Issues Polled: 47." Nobody cares. The work is the status.

**No to extensibility.** Don't make this a plugin architecture. Don't add p3 and p4 "just in case." Three priorities. That's discipline.

**No to refactoring.** The PRD says it plainly: "One targeted change. Do not refactor or reorganize." Listen to that wisdom. The best code change is the smallest one that solves the problem completely.

## The Emotional Hook

People will love this because *their work stops falling through the cracks.*

Every developer has felt that sting: "Wait, I filed that issue three weeks ago. Why hasn't anything happened?" That's a betrayal of trust. That's the system failing them.

Intake closes that gap. When you label something, it *matters*. The system sees you. The system responds. Your work becomes real the moment you declare its priority.

That's not just automation. That's respect for the craftsperson's intent.
