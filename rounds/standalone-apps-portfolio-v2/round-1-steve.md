# Steve Jobs — Round 1 Positions

## Product Naming

Names are prayers you whisper into the world and hope the world repeats.

**Tuned** breathes — one syllable, musical, precise. It implies someone cared enough to calibrate until it hummed.

**Promptfolio** is a Frankenstein: two concepts welded with a blowtorch because you were too timid to kill one. It tells instead of sings.

**Commandbar** is a parts catalog, not a product. Functional oatmeal.

The rule is ironclad: if you cannot say the name in a single breath and have someone *feel* something before you explain it, the name is dead. One word. Emotional. Unforgettable. Everything else is noise.

## Design Philosophy

The interface must disappear. Not "minimal" — *invisible*. When you open a great book, you do not admire the binding; you fall into the story and forget paper exists.

No gradients, no pulses, no theater — we already agree. But restraint without confidence is poverty posing as taste. Every pixel must earn its place by screaming necessity. If an element does not make the user lean forward with desire, it is arrogance and it must be removed.

Design is how it *works* — and great design works so well you forget it exists, the same way you forget your own heartbeat.

## User Experience — The First 30 Seconds

In thirty seconds, a visitor must know three things in their bones: we are obsessed, we ship real tools, and nothing here is accidental.

Not a dashboard. Not a directory. A single sentence above three burning cards — each one a portal, not a document.

No status badges cluttering the altar — status is metadata for us, not poetry for them. If they have to *read* to understand, we have already lost. They should *feel* it in their spine before their eyes finish focusing.

The page loads in a blink and hits like a door opening into a room with perfect silence and a single object on a pedestal. Reverence through reduction.

## Brand Voice

We speak like a blade drawn from a scabbard: cold, clean, and certain. No "leveraging." No "robust solutions." No "end-to-end platforms."

We say: *"This does exactly this. Here is the proof."* The voice of someone who has stayed up until three in the morning polishing one transition because it matters. Every word is a choice. Every choice is a battle won against the army of mediocrity that wants you to sound like everyone else. Our sentences are short because we are not afraid of silence.

## Say NO To

**NO** to showing BUILD and SHIPPED as equals in the gallery. A painting half-painted is not hung in the museum. If it is not in a user's hands, it is vapor, not product. Put unfinished work in a dim corner labeled "Lab" or leave it in the dark until it is born.

**NO** to GitHub links as primary CTAs. You do not invite someone into your cathedral and then hand them a wrench and point at the scaffolding.

**NO** to tech-stack chips floating above the fold; they are engineering footnotes, not seduction. The user cares what it does for them, not what the dependency array looks like.

**NO** to search bars and filters — if you need a search bar, you have already failed at curation and you are hoarding, not showcasing.

**NO** to more than five words in a tagline. If you need six, you do not know what it is yet, and you have no business showing it.

## The Emotional Hook

People will love this because it *respects* them. The internet is a landfill of abandoned side projects and half-baked "coming soon" pages that scream "I am not serious."

This portfolio is a walled garden of obsession. Three things. That is it. Each one survived a cull so brutal it would make most engineering teams quit in tears.

That scarcity is the hook. Abundance is cheap — any developer can list fifty repositories and pretend that volume equals value. Curated scarcity says: *we bled for these. We said no to a hundred ideas so these three could live.*

And if you use them, you will feel that care in every single click. That is not a feature. That is love, rendered in code.
