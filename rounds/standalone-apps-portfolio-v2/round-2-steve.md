# Round 2: Steve Jobs

## Where Elon Is Optimizing for the Wrong Metric

Elon, you measure this like a landing page. **Wrong metric.** A portfolio isn't about click-through rates; it's about whether the visitor *believes* the builder. You call SCAFFOLD "resume padding" and say "cut it." But SCAFFOLD isn't a promise — "coming soon" is the promise. SCAFFOLD is a fact. We label our scars. That builds trust no demo can manufacture. You're optimizing for velocity of output. I'm optimizing for quality of impression.

You also confuse restraint with laziness. "Restraint is just admitting most of the portfolio isn't worth showing." Wrong. Restraint is the hardest thing to ship. Any fool can add a gradient. It takes conviction to leave a wall white. You're already imagining 300 apps. That's not vision — that's a warehouse. Scale is the enemy of taste.

## Defending What You'll Attack

You'll call the gallery metaphor "aesthetic theater" for static HTML. You're wrong. Every pixel is a signal. If we can't make three cards feel inevitable, why should anyone trust us with three hundred thousand lines of code? Design isn't veneer. It's proof of care. When you remove the noise, the visitor has nothing left to judge but the work. That's the point.

You'll say we need search at scale. **Absolutely not.** If you need a search bar to find your best work, you haven't curated — you've accumulated. Search is an admission that you've built a junk drawer. We don't do catalogs. We do proof.

## Where Elon Is Right

He's right that seven deliverable files for trivial code is a broken pipeline, not a product. Kill the meta-work. `spec.md` is process theater.

He's right that "don't write TODO" as acceptance criteria means your agent is a slot machine, not a craftsman. Fix the tool; delete the guardrails.

He's right that this is not a growth engine. A portfolio doesn't bring users — it converts the ones who already arrived. Credibility infrastructure is just as critical as distribution. He brings them to the door; we close the sale.

## Top 3 Non-Negotiables

1. **Restraint is the feature.** Zero gradients. Zero pulses. No theater. The gallery wall is white because the work is the color.
2. **No search. No filters.** Curation beats collection. If you need search, we've built a database, not proof.
3. **Radical honesty, hierarchical real estate.** SCAFFOLD stays — it's a fact, not a fantasy. But the gallery wall is for shipped work only. Truth gets a label; perfection gets the spotlight.
