# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
## Emdash Theme Marketplace Review

---

### Product Naming

**Call it "Wardrobe."**

Not "Emdash Theme Marketplace." That's a description, not a name. Wardrobe is what this *is* — you're changing what your site wears. "Open your Wardrobe." "Install from Wardrobe." One word. Evocative. Human. The CLI becomes `npx wardrobe install ember`. That's poetry compared to the forgettable mouthful we have now.

---

### Design Philosophy

This product is about **transformation without destruction**. Your content stays. Your identity stays. Only the skin changes. That's the magic — and the marketplace must *feel* like magic.

Every theme card should be a window into a different world. Not a screenshot with a description underneath like some 2005 WordPress directory. Show me the theme *breathing*. Hover and it comes alive. Click and I'm *inside* it, with my own content, in under two seconds. The install command isn't hidden at the bottom — it's the only button that matters.

---

### User Experience — The First 30 Seconds

You land on Wardrobe. Five themes. No navigation. No "About" page. No FAQ cluttering the moment. Just five stunning previews arranged like album covers in a record store.

You hover on Ember — it pulses, the burnt orange bleeds outward. You click. Instantly, you see *your site* wearing Ember. Not a demo. YOUR content. YOUR images. That moment — seeing your own work transformed — that's the dopamine hit. That's when they're hooked.

One command appears: `npx wardrobe install ember`. Copy. Paste. Done. The whole experience is thirty seconds of "holy shit, that was easy."

---

### Brand Voice

Wardrobe speaks like a confident friend who works in fashion — not a salesperson, not a manual. Short sentences. Active verbs. Zero jargon.

- "Ember is bold. Editorial. For people with something to say."
- "Install in one command. Your content stays untouched."
- "Try it on. If it doesn't fit, try another."

No "seamlessly integrate your theming solution." No "leverage our robust ecosystem." Talk like a human or don't talk at all.

---

### What to Say NO To

- **No theme ratings or reviews.** Five curated themes don't need crowdsourced validation. Curation IS the quality signal.
- **No user accounts.** Why? You're installing via CLI. Accounts add friction for zero value.
- **No "Submit Your Theme" on day one.** Curate ruthlessly before opening the gates.
- **No filtering or search.** Five themes. You can see all five in one glance. Search is admitting you have too much.
- **No pricing tiers.** Free. All of it. Monetize later, if ever. Right now, adoption is the only metric.

---

### The Emotional Hook

People will love Wardrobe because it makes them feel **competent and creative in the same breath**.

Most tools make you choose: either you're a developer who builds from scratch, or you're a consumer who takes what you're given. Wardrobe says: "You built something meaningful. Now dress it up in thirty seconds and make it stunning."

That's not a feature. That's *dignity*. You made the content. We made it beautiful. Together, we made something worth sharing.

That feeling — "I can't believe I just did that" — that's why people will tell their friends.
