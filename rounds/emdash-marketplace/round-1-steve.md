# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
## Emdash Theme Marketplace: Design Vision

---

### Product Naming

**Call it "Wardrobe."**

Not "Emdash Theme Marketplace." That's a description, not a name. Wardrobe is what this *is* — you're changing what your site wears. One word. Evocative. Human.

`npx wardrobe install ember` — that's poetry. That's what people will tweet.

---

### Design Philosophy

This product is about **transformation without destruction**. Your content stays. Your identity stays. Only the skin changes. The marketplace must *feel* like that magic.

Every theme card should be a window into a different world. Not a screenshot with a description like some 2005 WordPress directory. Show me the theme *breathing*. Hover and it pulses. Click and I'm *inside* it, with my own content, in under two seconds.

The marketplace itself must be the sixth theme — stunning, opinionated, proof that we practice what we preach.

---

### The First 30 Seconds

You land. Five themes. No navigation. No "About" page. No FAQ cluttering the moment. Just five stunning previews arranged like album covers in a record store.

You hover on Ember — the burnt orange bleeds outward. You click. Instantly, you see *your site* wearing Ember. Not a demo. YOUR content. YOUR words, dressed in new clothes.

One command appears. Copy. Paste. Refresh your site. Gasp.

**That gasp is the product.**

---

### Brand Voice

Wardrobe speaks like a confident friend with impeccable taste — not a salesperson, not a manual. Short sentences. Active verbs. Zero jargon.

- "Ember is bold. Editorial. For people with something to say."
- "One command. Your content stays untouched."
- "Try it on. If it doesn't fit, try another."

No "seamlessly integrate." No "leverage our ecosystem." Talk like a human or don't talk at all.

---

### What to Say NO To

- **No user accounts.** Accounts are friction. Friction kills magic.
- **No ratings or reviews.** Five curated themes don't need democracy. Curation *is* the quality signal.
- **No "Submit Your Theme" at launch.** Curate ruthlessly before opening the gates.
- **No filtering or search.** Five themes. You see them all in one glance. Search is admitting defeat.
- **No customization panels.** Themes are opinions. Want to customize everything? Use code.

---

### The Emotional Hook

People don't buy themes. They buy **the feeling of having a beautiful website without becoming a developer.**

Every small business owner, every writer, every restaurant knows the shame of an ugly website. They've tried Squarespace and felt trapped. They've hired developers and felt robbed.

Wardrobe says: *You deserve this. One command. It's yours.*

That's not a feature. That's **dignity**.

---

*Ship the gasp.*
