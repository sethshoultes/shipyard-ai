# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

**RANK.**

Not "LocalGenius Benchmark Engine." That's a feature description, not a name. RANK is a verb and a noun. It's what you do. It's what you have. It's what you want more of. One syllable. Universal. When Maria checks her phone, she doesn't say "let me look at my competitive analytics dashboard" — she says "let me check my rank."

The word carries weight. Military. Sports. Google. Everyone understands rank. Everyone wants higher rank.

## Design Philosophy

This product should feel like a **scoreboard**, not a spreadsheet.

The best products create clarity from complexity. We're taking dozens of metrics, thousands of data points, and distilling them into one number that matters. That's the design philosophy: **ruthless simplification in service of motivation.**

The ranking widget should hit you like a punch. Big number. Your position. Direction of movement. That's it. No cognitive load. A five-year-old should understand if you're winning or losing.

Every chart, every email, every notification must answer one question: "Am I getting better?" If it doesn't answer that, delete it.

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

Maria opens the app. Before she scrolls, before she thinks, she sees: **#8 of 47**.

Her stomach tightens. She's competitive. She didn't know she was being ranked. Now she can't unknow it. She sees the arrow pointing up. Relief. Pride. Then she sees: "2 reviews away from #7."

Now she has a mission for her day.

That's the experience. **Instant context. Instant emotion. Instant action.** No tutorials. No onboarding flows. Just truth, delivered beautifully.

## Brand Voice

RANK speaks like a **coach**, not a consultant.

Short sentences. Direct. Slightly urgent. "You dropped 2 spots. Here's why. Here's how to fix it."

Never apologetic. Never buried in qualifiers. We don't say "You might consider potentially improving your response time." We say: "Respond faster. Top performers answer in 2 hours. You're at 8."

The tone is warm but competitive. We believe in you — and we're not going to let you settle.

## What to Say NO To

- **NO to public leaderboards.** This creates shame, not motivation. Keep rankings private. Competition with yourself, not public humiliation.
- **NO to complex weighting explanations.** Users don't need to understand the algorithm. They need to understand the outcome.
- **NO to seasonal adjustments in v1.** Perfect is the enemy of shipped. Adjust later.
- **NO to multi-location complexity.** One business, one rank. Period. Solve edge cases after you've proven the core.
- **NO to "insights" that don't drive action.** If a benchmark doesn't suggest a specific behavior change, kill it.

Simplicity requires saying no to good ideas so great ideas can breathe.

## The Emotional Hook

People will love this because **it transforms isolation into belonging**.

Every small business owner thinks they're alone. They have no idea if they're winning or losing. They're flying blind, hoping their instincts are right.

RANK gives them something they've never had: **a peer group**. Suddenly they're not just a Mexican restaurant in Austin — they're the #8 Mexican restaurant in Austin. They're part of a community. They have rivals they've never met. They have benchmarks that prove their hard work matters.

That's not analytics. That's identity.

This is the data moat the board demanded, wrapped in the human truth that competition brings out our best. Build this right, and every customer makes every other customer better. That's not a feature — that's a flywheel.
