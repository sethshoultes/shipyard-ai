# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
## Positions on the Shipyard Showcase

---

### Product Naming
One word. One.

**Poster.** Not "Poster Child," not "Poster Generator." Poster. WP-Agent becomes **Agent.** LocalGenius Lite becomes **Local.** AgentBench becomes **Bench.** PromptOps becomes **Ops.** AgentLog becomes **Log.**

If you need two words, you've already failed. The best names are adjectives turned into nouns. They feel inevitable, like they've always existed. Seven Emdash plugins? Give each a single word and let the collection breathe. A name should be a pocket-sized poem you can say in a breath. If you can't tattoo it, don't ship it.

---

### Design Philosophy
This isn't a website. It's a **museum.**

Each product is a sculpture on a pedestal. White space isn't empty—it's reverence. Jony used to ask: "What's the material?" Here, the material is **shipping.** The chrome is invisible. The product is everything.

When someone scrolls `/work`, they should feel the same hush as walking into a gallery where every piece matters. No gradients begging for attention. No shadows trying too hard. No borders drawing lines in the sand. Just the quiet arrogance of something perfectly placed. Typography so good you don't notice it—that's the goal. You notice bad typography like you notice bad breath. Good typography is invisible.

---

### The First 30 Seconds
They land. They see something they didn't think AI could build. They click. It works.

No explanation. No onboarding tour. No "learn more." It's like walking into an Apple Store and the iPhone is already in your hand, already on, already magical. Proof before promise. Show, don't tell—then get out of the way.

If they don't believe it in three seconds, you've lost them. If they do believe it, you don't need to say another word. The first thirty seconds are a handshake. Firm, warm, unforgettable. Make it count or go home.

---

### Brand Voice
We don't *explain.* We **reveal.**

Short sentences. Periods, not semicolons. We speak like someone who knows exactly what they're doing and doesn't need your approval. "We ship production AI software autonomously. Here's what shipped this week." That's it. That's the whole pitch.

Confidence is quiet. Insecurity is loud. We never say "leverage" or "synergy" or "AI-powered." Those words are the sound of someone compensating. We let the shipping speak. Every sentence should feel carved, not typed. If it sounds like a press release, delete it and start over.

---

### What We Say NO To
No carousels. No "as seen on." No testimonials. No social proof badges. No feature comparison tables. No "powered by" stickers.

No explaining how the sausage is made. No "coming soon"—if it didn't ship, it doesn't exist. No dropdown menus with seventeen options. No footer that looks like a phone book. No animated counters spinning up to impress. No team photos. No "our story."

Simplicity isn't just removing clutter. It's having the courage to leave something out. Real artists ship. Mediocre artists add one more feature and call it progress. We are not mediocre. We say no to a thousand good ideas so one great idea can live.

---

### The Emotional Hook
People don't love tools. They love **magic.**

They love the feeling that something impossible just happened while they watched. Every card on `/work` should make visitors feel like the future arrived early and they got a front-row seat. Not "impressive for AI." Just impressive. Full stop.

That's how you build a cult, not a customer base. They should leave the site angry—angry that they didn't think of it first, angry that they can't look away, angry that they now expect every other company to ship this cleanly. That anger is love in disguise. Make them feel something, or you've built a spreadsheet.
