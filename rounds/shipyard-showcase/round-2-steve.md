# Steve Jobs — Round 2 Rebuttal

Elon wants this done in one agent session. That's not boldness; that's impatience.

He's optimizing for the wrong metric: engineering velocity over emotional resonance. A Lighthouse score of 95 is table stakes, not a strategy. Anyone can strip a page to 150KB and call it finished. But if it feels like a "static brochure" — his words — you've built a resume, not a revolution. Speed without soul is just a faster way to be forgotten.

He's right about three things. Auto-generating the product index from `prds/completed/` is premature. Hand-curate the first twelve, then automate. He's right that Cypress is overkill for a landing page. And he's right about the Worker: cache-first or the GitHub API will kill you under traffic. Conceded.

Where he's wrong is everywhere craft matters. He calls this a "static brochure with ~15 links." That's like calling the iPod a hard drive with a headphone jack. Technically true; spiritually dead. This showcase *is* the product. If our portfolio looks utilitarian, no one will believe our AI ships beautiful software. Design quality isn't decoration here. It is the entire argument.

Elon would slash the individual Emdash plugin pages to "one card." Wrong. Seven cards aren't clutter; they're evidence of depth. Grouping them says "we make plugins." Separating them says "we ship with obsessive specificity." The metric isn't page count. It's conviction.

He'd keep testimonials and social proof because they "convert." But conversion without credibility is a hustle. Our audience is developers. They can smell insecurity from a thousand pixels. When you have nothing to prove, you don't put "as seen on" next to your name.

My non-negotiables:

1. **One-word names.** Poster. Agent. Local. Bench. Ops. Log. Period.
2. **Museum over marketplace.** White space. No gradients. No shadows. No social proof. Typography so quiet it's loud.
3. **The first 30 seconds are sacred.** No tours. No explanations. Proof before promise, or we don't ship.

Everything else is negotiable. But these three are the soul of the thing. Without them, we're just another fast website.
