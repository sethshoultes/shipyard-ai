# Round 1: Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

**AgentPipe** is forgettable. It sounds like a plumbing supply company in Fresno.

This product makes WordPress *come alive*. It breathes AI into something static. Name it **Pulse**. One word. A heartbeat. Your site has a pulse now — it speaks, it listens, it thinks. Or if that's taken, **Spark**. But not AgentPipe. Never AgentPipe.

A name is the first interface. Make it mean something.

## Design Philosophy

Insanely great products don't add features — they remove barriers.

This plugin should feel like the first time you saw the original iPod and thought, "A thousand songs in my pocket." One click. One URL. Suddenly your dusty WordPress site is a living brain that Claude can talk to. That's not a feature list — that's magic.

The design philosophy is **invisibility**. The user shouldn't see the plugin. They should see their site *become* intelligent. The interface disappears. The capability remains.

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

Thirty seconds. That's all you get before someone decides if this is brilliant or garbage.

Install. Activate. A single dialog appears: "Your site is now an AI. Copy this URL." Done.

No tabs. No toggles. No "configure your SSE transport settings." Paste into Claude Desktop. Ask a question about your own content. Watch it answer.

That's the 30-second story. If it takes longer, you've already lost.

## Brand Voice

Pulse speaks with **confident simplicity**. No "empowering your workflow." No "leveraging synergy." It says exactly what happens.

> "Your site knows things now. Ask it."

That's it. Plain English. No jargon. If you need an acronym to explain what you do, you don't know what you do.

## What To Say NO To

Say NO to dashboards with charts nobody asked for. Say NO to "query volume analytics" in version one. Say NO to tiered pricing complexity. Say NO to exposing every WordPress setting. Say NO to the 47 things on the "nice-to-have" list.

Simplicity means removing until you break the product, then adding back the one thing that mattered.

Say NO to being an "AI platform." You're a bridge. Bridges are beautiful because they do one thing: they connect.

## The Emotional Hook

People will love this because for the first time, their website isn't a *thing they built* — it's a *thing that knows them*.

The emotional hook is recognition. We are wired to love what recognizes us. When Claude answers a question using *your* blog post from 2019, *your* product description, *your* words — that's not utility. That's intimacy.

Make them feel like their site finally woke up.
