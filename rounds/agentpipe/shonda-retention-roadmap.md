# Shonda Retention Roadmap — AgentPipe v1.1
**Principle:** Users don't stay for features. They stay for the next episode.

---

## The Core Retention Engine: "What Happens When My Agent Queries My Site?"

The pilot must end on a cliffhanger that resolves into addiction.

### Pilot Beat Sheet (Onboarding)
1. **Cold Open:** Jordan installs plugin. One click. One URL copied.
2. **Inciting Incident:** Jordan pastes the MCP URL into their agent for the first time.
3. **Rising Action:** Agent asks a question Jordan never taught it. ("Your 2023 post on ceramic coatings outranks your 2025 product page—should I redirect it?")
4. **Climax:** Jordan realizes the agent is reading *live* site content, not static training data.
5. **Tag:** "Your site has 1,847 indexed posts. You've unlocked 100 AI queries today. Tomorrow: 100 more. Or go Pro."

**Retention rule:** If the agent doesn't surprise Jordan in the first five minutes, Jordan churns before the first billing cycle.

---

## Weekly Rhythm: The Digest as Narrative Engine

The weekly digest must feel like a season recap, not a spreadsheet.

| Week | Digest Theme | Emotional Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | "Your Agent's First Words" | What did it learn? What did it get wrong? Jordan corrects it—training begins. |
| Week 2 | "The Post You Forgot Was Ranking" | Surprise value from old content. Nostalgia + discovery. |
| Week 3 | "Someone Asked a Question You Never Answered" | Content gap alert. Jordan feels seen; the agent feels psychic. |
| Week 4 | "Your Site vs. The Internet" | Benchmark against anonymous aggregate of similar sites. Mild competitive tension. |

**v1.1 Upgrade:** Let Jordan *reply* to the digest. The reply becomes a queued agent query. The inbox becomes a conversation, not a broadcast.

---

## The Content Flywheel (Why Jordan Doesn't Leave)

Every site is a character. The more Jordan feeds it, the smarter the whole universe becomes.

- **v1.0:** Jordan's site answers Jordan's questions.
- **v1.1:** Jordan's site answers *other* agents' questions—anonymously, via opt-in federated queries.
- **v1.1 Hook:** "Your 'Ceramic Coating FAQ' just helped 14 other detailers answer their customers. You're now a source."

This is the network-effect cliffhanger: *What if my content becomes part of something bigger?*

**Retention mechanic:** A "Source Rank" score. Visible in the admin dashboard. Rises when other sites query your embeddings. Falls if you uninstall. Switching cost = abandoning your narrative authority.

## v1.1 Features: The Next Season

### 1. Setup Wizard Tension
- Current state: flat forms.
- v1.1: Three-arc setup wizard.
  - **Act I:** Plugin detects site type (blog, store, portfolio, community).
  - **Act II:** Agent asks one probing question based on the *actual homepage content*—not a template. ("I see you sell hand-poured candles. Should I treat 'scent notes' as a taxonomy or free text?")
  - **Act III:** Live preview. Jordan types a question; the agent answers from *just-ingested* content. The "aha" is manufactured in real time.

### 2. Emotional Cliffhangers in the Dashboard
- **"Your agent noticed something"** badge. Amber alert. Not an error—a *narrative hook*. Click to resolve.
- Examples:
  - "3 posts published this week were never indexed in the vector store."
  - "A visitor asked a question that matched 0% of your content. Create the post?"
- **v1.1 rule:** Every alert must end with a question mark or an ellipsis. Periods are for receipts, not retention.

### 3. The Annual Badge as Character Arc
- Shonda's note: Annual billing toggle is clean; badge visible in <3 seconds. Good.
- v1.1 enhancement: "Annual" is not a discount. It is a *season pass*.
  - Copy: "Unlock the full season."
  - Visual: A progress bar showing "Episodes watched: 11/52" (queries used / queries remaining).
  - When Jordan hits 40/52: "Binge more. Upgrade to Annual and unlock unlimited queries."

### 4. Content Calendar as Script Room
- v1.1: Collaborative content planner.
- Jordan and the agent co-write an editorial calendar.
- Agent proposes titles based on query gaps. Jordan drags to schedule.
- **Retention hook:** "Next week's post is already in your vector store. Want me to pre-write the meta description?"

### 5. The Churn-Reversal Episode
- When Jordan's usage drops 50% week-over-week, trigger a *narrative intervention*, not a discount code.
- Subject line: "Your agent misses your site."
- Body: One specific insight the agent generated *this week* that Jordan never viewed. ("You left this on the table.")
- CTA: "See what I found." (Links to the missed insight, not the billing page.)

## What Keeps Users Coming Back: The Three-Act Summary

| Act | Mechanic | Feeling |
|---|---|---|
| **Setup** | Live preview answering from real content | *I can't believe that worked.* |
| **Habit** | Weekly digest with reply-to-query | *My agent is learning.* |
| **Identity** | Source Rank + federated authority | *My site matters to the network.* |

---

*Retention is not a billing toggle. Billing toggles are props. Retention is the story the user sees themselves in. Give Jordan the moment the agent first answers from site content. That's your pilot. Everything after that is the next episode.*
