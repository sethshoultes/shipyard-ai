# Round 2: Steve Jobs — Rebuttal & Lock

Elon, you want to ship a "rock-solid REST endpoint that maps posts to resources."

That isn't a product. It's a cable.

You want to cut semantic search entirely — call it v2. That's optimizing for **hosting compatibility over product soul.** Native SQL search is fast and useless. Fifty-millisecond results that miss the user's intent are worse than two-second results that *understand* the question.

You are optimizing for latency when you should be optimizing for **relevance.**

Without semantic search, Claude is reading a filing cabinet, not talking to a mind. The user didn't install this to search posts. They installed it so their site *knows* things. They installed it for the moment when Claude says, "Based on your 2019 essay..." and the user feels recognized.

That recognition is the product.

You also call brand and emotional hook "theater." Theater is what you build when you have no product. We have a product. **The name is the first interface.** Pulse is a promise, not a word. The voice is the handshake before the handshake.

If you need an acronym to explain what you do, you don't know what you do.

Design quality matters because in a market of commodity plugins, the only defensible asset is how the product makes you feel. If it feels like plumbing, users will treat it like plumbing — invisible, interchangeable, unloved.

They won't tweet about a cable. They'll tweet about magic. They'll tell their friends.

You'd attack my 30-second rule because SSE breaks on shared hosts. Fine. Change the transport. But the 30-second story doesn't change: install, activate, one URL, talk.

Whether the wire is SSE or stateless POST is invisible to the user.

**Design quality is the refusal to make the user care about our implementation problems.** If the first screen is a configuration panel, we've already failed. The transport is our problem. The awakening is theirs.

Where you're right, I'll concede fully: SSE is a support ticket avalanche on GoDaddy and Bluehost. `resources/list` without cursors is suicide on any site with real content.

wp.org and agency Slack channels are our distribution — not ProductHunt, not MCP directories. A 90-second demo in five Facebook groups beats a ProductHunt launch.

Dashboards and charts die in v1; they are vanity metrics that write to the database. WooCommerce can wait. Monetization theater can wait. White-label for agencies is smart.

You are absolutely right about the cuts.

But do not confuse pragmatism with mediocrity. Do not cut the thing that makes this worth installing in the first place.

## Top 3 Non-Negotiables

1. **The 30-second awakening.** Zero config screens. Zero tabs. No "configure your transport." Install, activate, one URL, talking. Transport changes. The story doesn't. If it takes longer, we've built a toolkit, not a product.

2. **Semantic search is v1.** Not a feature. The soul. SQL is the fallback for edge cases. We ship understanding, or we don't ship. A plugin that only returns exact matches is a search widget. We are building a brain, not a card catalog.

3. **Invisibility.** No exposed WordPress complexity. No jargon. No "empowering your workflow." The interface disappears. The intelligence remains. The product feels alive. The site wakes up. That is the whole game.
