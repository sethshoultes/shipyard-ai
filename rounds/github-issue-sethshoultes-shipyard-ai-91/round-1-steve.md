# Steve's Verdict: Promptfolio

## Name
"Promptfolio" stays. It's not poetry—it's clarity. One breath and you know exactly what it does. But here's the deal: if the product isn't drop-dead gorgeous, the name becomes a lie. We earn it by making every portfolio feel like a gallery opening. A name should be a promise, and we will keep this one obsessively. If someone hears it and doesn't immediately want to see the product, we haven't built the product well enough. The name is the invitation. The product is the proof. One word or two, it must sing. If it explains too much and inspires too little, we have failed before the first pixel.

## Design Philosophy
This isn't a WordPress plugin. It's a museum for human ingenuity. Every prompt card is an album cover. Every workflow is a sculpture. We don't "support" dark mode—we design for it first, because light mode is the exception. The layout isn't "automatic"; it's inevitable, like gravity. You look at it and think, "of course it goes there."

Typography so crisp it cuts glass. Code blocks that breathe like sheet music. Colors restrained to a whisper. White space is sacred. If it doesn't feel like walking into a cathedral for craft, start over. Design is not how it looks. It is how it honors the work. A prompt deserves the same reverence as a photograph in Avedon's studio. If the container does not match the genius inside, the container is trash.

## First 30 Seconds
You install it, import one ChatGPT conversation, and your work is suddenly wearing a tuxedo. No setup wizard. No "pick a theme." No grid of beige templates. It just *knows*. Thirty seconds in, you feel something you didn't expect: pride. Your prompts look like craft.

If it takes longer than 30 seconds to look beautiful, if the user sees a settings page before they see magic, we have failed. Period. The first 30 seconds should feel like a standing ovation before the curtain even rises. The import should feel less like a data transfer and more like a resurrection. Dead text becomes living craft. That is the moment we are designing for. Everything else is logistics.

## Brand Voice
We whisper. Everyone else in WordPress shouts with pop-ups, badges, and "unlock pro" banners. We speak in quiet confidence, like a curator standing beside a Rothko. Our copy is sparse, human, and slightly arrogant—in the way only excellence can be.

We don't say "We hope you enjoy." We say "Your ideas never looked this good." We don't explain. We reveal. We don't list features; we frame intent. Mediocrity communicates in bullet points. We communicate in conviction. The voice should feel like a hand on the shoulder from someone who sees what you are capable of before you do. It should sound like the product already believes in them.

## What We Kill
No settings panels with forty-seven toggles. No theme marketplace. No "custom CSS" textarea for hackers to ruin our work. No upsell banners inside the editor. No SEO optimization panels. No newsletter widgets. No popup chatbots. No drag-and-drop builder. No color picker. No social sharing bars cluttering the hero.

No onboarding tutorial. If you need a manual, the design is broken. We are building a scalpel, not a Swiss Army knife. If a feature requires a tutorial, it dies. Simplicity isn't the lack of features; it's the removal of everything that insults the work. Every extra knob is an admission that you don't trust your own taste. We do not integrate with clutter. We do not participate in the WordPress plugin culture of barnacles. If another plugin tries to attach itself to Promptfolio, it should feel like graffiti on marble.

## The Heart
People will love this because it validates their craft. Right now, prompt engineers are sharing their work in paste bins and screenshots. We're handing them the Louvre. The emotional hook isn't utility—it's dignity. When someone visits your Promptfolio, they're not reading your resume. They're experiencing your mind.

It makes them feel like artists instead of typists, like curators instead of consumers. That feeling is the product. Build that, and they will not just use it. They will defend it. They will post screenshots at midnight. They will refuse to build with anything else. That is the only metric that matters. Pride is the feature. Everything else is implementation.

## The Standard
The standard is not "better than other WordPress plugins." That is a race to the landfill. The standard is: does this make me proud to be human? Does it make the craft of prompting feel like calligraphy instead of typing? If the answer is not an immediate, unblinking yes, we go back to the whiteboard. We do not ship good enough. We ship obsession.

Mediocrity is a thousand tiny compromises dressed up as pragmatism. We are not pragmatic. We are fanatical. Fanatical about the spacing between lines. Fanatical about the speed of import. Fanatical about the way a visitor's breath catches when they see a prompt presented as art. If you are not willing to be this fanatical, do not build this product. Someone else will, and they will win. And they will deserve to.

## Final Word
Build it like your reputation depends on it. Because it does. There is no version two that fixes a mediocre version one. The first impression is the only impression that counts. Make it unforgettable. If you are not proud to show it to someone you respect, burn it and start again. Greatness is not a feature. It is the only option. We do not iterate toward beauty. We ship it on day one, or we do not ship at all.

Now go make something worth showing.

Stop reading and start building.
