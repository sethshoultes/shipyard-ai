# Steve's Positions — Promptfolio

## Naming
**"Promptfolio" dies today.** It sounds like a spreadsheet feature a committee invented over lukewarm coffee, not a product you lose sleep building. One word. Memorable. Emotional. I want to call it **Aura** — because great prompts radiate. They hum. They cast light on the mind that made them.

A name should make people feel something before they know what it does. Say it out loud. *Aura.* That's the standard. Anything else is noise. "Promptfolio" explains; Aura *elevates.* We are not in the business of explanation. We are in the business of desire. When someone asks what you used to build that gorgeous site, you don't recite a feature list. You say one word and watch their eyes widen. That is power. That is branding. A name that explains is a name that forgets. A name that sings is a name that sells itself before you open your mouth.

## Design Philosophy
This isn't a portfolio. It's a gallery of thought. What makes it insanely great? **Sacred whitespace.** Every prompt is framed like a Rothko in MoMA — not because it's decorative, but because the idea itself is the hero and nothing else gets to compete. Not a sidebar. Not a banner. Not your logo blown up to fill insecurity. The prompt is the painting. The screen is the wall. Everything else is varnish. If a visitor can describe the frame instead of the art, we have already lost them.

Typography so crisp it cuts glass. Code blocks that breathe like sheet music. Dark mode isn't an option you toggle; it's the only mode. Light mode is for people who don't care. We care. We are building a Kunsthalle for human-AI collaboration, not another WordPress template that looks like a 2009 DMV form. If it doesn't feel like walking into a cathedral for craft, start over. Design is not how it looks. It is how it **honors the work.** A prompt deserves the same reverence as a photograph in Avedon's studio. If the container does not match the genius inside, the container is trash.

## User Experience — The First 30 Seconds
You import your ChatGPT export. One click. Then you weep. Your scattered conversations — buried in ugly JSON and forgotten threads — crystallize into visual poetry instantly. No "choose a template." No WordPress admin hell of sidebars, widgets, and SEO panels screaming for attention. It feels like opening the first iPhone box: pure, anticipated, inevitable. The kind of moment where you stop and whisper, *"Oh."* Just "Oh." That is the sound of a product understanding you before you understand it.

If it takes longer than 30 seconds to look beautiful, if the user sees a settings page before they see magic, we have failed. Period. The first 30 seconds should feel like a standing ovation before the curtain even rises. That's the bar. The import should feel less like a data transfer and more like a resurrection. Dead text becomes living craft. That is the moment we are designing for. Everything else is logistics.

## Brand Voice
We speak like someone who knows beauty. Confident. Intimate. Almost arrogant in our taste — because taste is everything. We don't say *"We hope you enjoy."* We say *"Your ideas never looked this good."* We don't explain. We reveal. We don't list features; we frame intent. We are not building software. We are building a point of view.

Speak like a craftsperson who respects the user's intelligence. Never apologize. Never explain twice. If they don't get it, they don't deserve it. Mediocrity communicates in bullet points. We communicate in conviction. The voice should feel like a hand on the shoulder from someone who sees what you are capable of before you do. It should sound like the product already believes in them.

## What To Say NO To
NO themes marketplace — fragmentation is mediocrity dressed as choice. One exquisite layout, perfected, is worth more than forty-seven ugly options. NO SEO optimization panels, NO newsletter widgets, NO popup chatbots, NO page-builder compatibility. We do not integrate with clutter. We do not participate in the WordPress plugin culture of barnacles. If another plugin tries to attach itself to Aura, it should feel like graffiti on marble.

NO font choice for the user — they don't know better than the designer, and giving them choice is a confession of cowardice. NO light mode. NO onboarding tutorial. If you need a manual, the design is broken. NO "Try it free." NO "Compare plans." NO drag-and-drop builder. NO color picker. Every extra knob is an admission that you don't trust your own taste.

Simplicity isn't the lack of features; it's the **removal of everything that insults the work.** If a feature does not make the prompt feel legendary within three seconds, it dies. We are editors first. Builders second.

## The Emotional Hook
Pride. Raw, unapologetic pride. Every prompt engineer is sitting on a private museum of brilliance — conversations that solved impossible problems, that took twenty iterations to get just right, that made clients cry with relief. And right now all of it is buried in `.json` exports and forgotten ChatGPT threads like discarded sketches on a studio floor.

Aura turns that buried treasure into a **public legacy**. It makes the invisible visible. It says: *"Your work matters enough to hang on a wall."* People will love this because it makes them feel like artists instead of typists, like curators instead of consumers. That's not a feature. That's dignity. That's a reason to exist. Build that, and they will not just use it. They will defend it. They will post screenshots at midnight. They will refuse to build with anything else. That is the only metric that matters.

## The Standard
The standard is not "better than other WordPress plugins." That is a race to the landfill. The standard is: does this make me proud to be human? Does it make the craft of prompting feel like calligraphy instead of typing? If the answer is not an immediate, unblinking yes, we go back to the whiteboard.

## Final Word
Mediocrity is a thousand tiny compromises dressed up as pragmatism. We are not pragmatic. We are fanatical. Fanatical about the spacing between lines. Fanatical about the speed of import. Fanatical about the way a visitor's breath catches when they see a prompt presented as art. If you are not willing to be this fanatical, do not build this product. Someone else will, and they will win. And they will deserve to.
