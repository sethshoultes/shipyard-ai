# Round 1: Steve

## Product Naming

"Promptfolio" is dead on arrival. It sounds like a sinus medication. Names matter — they're the first note of the symphony. We're calling this **Folio**. One word. Elegant. Permanent.

A folio is a collected work of distinction, not a folder on a server. If you need "prompt" in the name to know what it does, the product has already failed.

## Design Philosophy

This isn't a plugin. It's a printing press for genius.

What makes it insanely great? Ruthless reverence for the work itself. Every prompt is treated like a poem set in hot metal type. Typography is everything. White space is sacred. The interface doesn't "support" your content — it disappears so your craft can breathe.

We don't build features; we build feelings. The feeling of holding something that was made with obsessive care.

## The First 30 Seconds

You export your Claude conversation, import it, and suddenly — gasp — it looks like a MoMA exhibition.

No templates to pick. No colors to choose. It just KNOWS. The prompt becomes a hero card. The conversation flow becomes visual choreography. In thirty seconds, you go from embarrassed by your scattered notes to proud enough to send the link to your mother.

That's the bar.

## Brand Voice

We speak like we have taste. Confident. Sparse.

We don't say "robust feature set" or "seamless integration." We say, "It just works." We say, "Your work, elevated." Every word is carved, not typed. We don't explain — we reveal. If a sentence has a comma, it's probably too long.

## What to Say NO To

NO theme marketplace with five hundred mediocre choices. Maybe one perfect adaptive layout.

NO "AI-powered" badges — the magic is invisible, like electricity. NO settings pages with forty checkboxes. NO dark mode "toggle" — it follows the sun, flawlessly. NO shortcodes. NO admin clutter. NO "Pro" nag screens. NO PDF export. NO collaboration mode.

NO bloat. Simplicity isn't the absence of features; it's the presence of only the essential ones, perfectly executed.

## The Emotional Hook

Right now, the best prompts in the world live in ChatGPT threads and Notion dumps — buried, ephemeral, ugly.

Folio excavates genius and puts it on a pedestal. People will love this because it turns technicians into artists. It validates their craft.

It's the difference between a carpenter's pile of scrap wood and a solo show at the Whitney.

Pride. That's the product.
