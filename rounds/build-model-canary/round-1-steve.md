# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

**Slug.** One word. That's it. Not `slugify-ts`, not `build-model-canary`. **Slug.** When you name something with five syllables and a hyphen, you've already admitted defeat. People don't fall in love with acronyms — they fall in love with precision. Slug is a noun, a verb, a promise. It enters the room and sits down at the head of the table.

## Design Philosophy

This isn't a utility library. It's a **manifesto for focus**. We're not building a Swiss Army knife; we're crafting a scalpel. Two functions. One job each. No configuration objects, no plugins, no ecosystem. The design is done not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away — and we are *ruthless* about that subtraction. Every extra feature is a crack in the foundation.

## User Experience — The First 30 Seconds

You import it. It works. You move on. That's the experience. No README novel, no "getting started" guide that takes twelve steps. In the first thirty seconds, a developer should feel something rare: **trust**. Trust that the code won't betray them at 2 AM. Trust that the person who wrote it cared more about their time than their own cleverness. It should feel like sliding into a perfectly fitted leather seat.

## Brand Voice

We speak in statements, not suggestions. We don't say "it is recommended that..." We say: **"Slug lowercases. Slug hyphenates. Slug strips noise."** The voice is confident, minimal, and uncompromising — like a good editor who crosses out half your manuscript and hands back something that actually sings.

## What We Say NO To

- NO options parameters. Options are fear dressed up as flexibility.
- NO dependencies. If we can't do it in vanilla TypeScript, we don't deserve to ship it.
- NO `TODO` comments, no placeholders, no "we'll fix it later." Later is where good products go to die.
- NO documentation beyond what the code itself screams. The spec is the contract; the code is the poetry.
- NO support for edge cases that only exist in conference talks. Real users, real strings, real results.

## The Emotional Hook

People will love this because **it respects them**. In a world where every npm install pulls in seventeen transitive dependencies and a 40-page README, Slug is a breath of cold, clean air. It does one thing beautifully, then gets out of the way. That's not just good engineering — it's an act of empathy. You don't remember the tool that cluttered your life. You remember the one that vanished and left only the result behind.
