# Board Verdict: shipyard-post-delivery-v2 (Anchor)

**Date:** 2026-04-12
**Reviewers:** Jensen Huang, Warren Buffett, Oprah Winfrey, Shonda Rhimes
**Product:** Anchor Post-Delivery System

---

## Overall Verdict: PROCEED

**With conditions.** The board unanimously recommends shipping Phase 1 immediately, with specific conditions that must be addressed within 30-90 days.

---

## Points of Agreement

All four board members agree on the following:

### 1. The Core Positioning Is Correct
> "We don't disappear."

Every reviewer called out this hook as emotionally resonant and strategically sound:
- **Jensen:** "The positioning is good"
- **Buffett:** "The 'We don't disappear' hook is defensible through repetition"
- **Oprah:** "Four words. That's the whole thing. And it lands because it names a wound"
- **Shonda:** "A Scandal-level hook. It names the wound"

### 2. The Voice Guide Is Exceptional
All reviewers praised the voice documentation, with its before/after examples and practical quality tests.

### 3. Capital Efficiency Is Strong
- Zero incremental CAC for maintenance upsells
- 98-99% gross margins on recurring revenue
- No automation investment until demand is proven
- Milestone-based capital deployment (15 → 25 → 50 clients)

### 4. Manual-First Is Strategically Correct
The decision to stay manual until 25 clients shows wisdom:
- Allows iteration based on real client responses
- Avoids automating unproven assumptions
- Demonstrates operational discipline over technical ambition

### 5. Unit Economics Work
- $79/month Basic at ~99% margin
- $149/month Pro at ~98% margin
- LTV doubles with retention (project + recurring)
- Achievable targets (25% attach rate, $400 MRR in 60 days)

### 6. The Product Naming Is Strong
"Anchor" evokes stability and relationship—far superior to "Maintenance Plans."

## Points of Tension

The board disagrees on the following:

### 1. Technology Moat: Existential vs. Acceptable

| Reviewer | Position | Score |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Jensen** | "No moat. Any competitor can copy this in a weekend. Zero AI leverage." | 6/10 |
| **Buffett** | "The moat is operational discipline and brand positioning, not technology. That's fine." | 7/10 |

**The divide:** Jensen believes Shipyard must build AI-powered intelligence (site monitoring, predictive maintenance, LLM-generated recommendations) to survive. Buffett believes execution and reputation are sufficient moats at this scale.

**Resolution:** Both paths are valid. Ship Phase 1 with operational moat; begin building intelligence layer in parallel.

### 2. Completeness of Retention Architecture

| Reviewer | Position |
|----------|----------|
| **Shonda** | "Four emails stopping at Month 6 leaves the story unfinished. Where's Day 365?" |
| **Oprah** | "The one-year mark is a natural emotional beat. Bring back the anniversary email." |
| **Buffett** | "Ship as-is. Annual prepay can come later." |

**The divide:** Content/narrative reviewers want a complete year-one arc. Business reviewers are comfortable with incomplete but shippable.

**Resolution:** Restore Day 365 anniversary email before launch (minimal effort, high impact).

### 3. Content Flywheel: Critical vs. Future

| Reviewer | Position | Urgency |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Shonda** | "No flywheel. Nothing compounds. Clients don't bring anyone with them." | Critical gap (4/10) |
| **Jensen** | "You're collecting no data. The Notion database tracks process, not insight." | Critical gap |
| **Oprah** | "Foundation is strong. Testimonials and social proof can come later." | Future iteration |
| **Buffett** | "Track the metrics. The flywheel evidence comes from data, not planning." | Future iteration |

**The divide:** Some see the lack of compounding effects as a critical flaw; others see it as Phase 2 work.

**Resolution:** Ship Phase 1; add testimonial capture and referral prompts in Phase 2.

### 4. Token Language: Translation Required

| Reviewer | Position |
|----------|----------|
| **Oprah** | "Normal people don't think in tokens. Translate: 'Enough for about 5-10 content updates per month.'" |
| **Jensen** | Not raised |
| **Buffett** | "Token estimation risk exists—track usage religiously." |
| **Shonda** | Not raised |

**The divide:** Consumer-facing reviewers want human language; business reviewers focus on measurement.

**Resolution:** Add human-readable translations to all client-facing token references.

## Conditions for Proceeding

### Must-Do (Before Launch)

1. **Restore Day 365 anniversary email.** The year-one arc cannot end at Month 6. (Shonda, Oprah)

2. **Add email preview lines.** Each email should hint at the next touchpoint to create serialized narrative. (Shonda)

3. **Translate token language.** Replace "50K tokens" with human-readable equivalents like "5-10 content updates per month." (Oprah)

### Must-Do (Within 30 Days of Launch)

4. **Implement token usage tracking.** Capture actual usage from Day 1 to validate pricing assumptions. (Buffett, Jensen)

5. **Track the five metrics:**
   - Attach rate (target: 15%+)
   - Token usage actuals
   - Churn rate (target: <10% monthly)
   - Email reply rates
   - Time-to-send compliance (target: 95%)

### Should-Do (Within 90 Days)

6. **Add Day 90 touchpoint.** The 152-day gap between Day 30 and Month 6 is too long. (Oprah, Shonda)

7. **Build testimonial capture.** At Day 30 or Month 6, ask: "Can we share your story?" (Shonda)

8. **Add referral prompt.** At Month 6: "Know someone who needs a site?" (Shonda)

9. **Create Site Card artifact.** A simple PDF showing site URL, launch date, tokens used, maintenance tier. Give clients something to hold. (Shonda)

10. **Evaluate annual prepay option.** If any client asks, implement immediately. (Buffett)

### Consider (Within 6 Months)

11. **Site Health Monitor.** Cloudflare integration, uptime checks, performance scoring. Email clients before they notice problems. (Jensen)

12. **Client Dashboard MVP.** Even a static page showing site health gives clients a destination. (Jensen, Shonda)

13. **LLM-Powered Refresh Proposals.** Feed AI the original site + time elapsed + industry context. Generate specific recommendations. (Jensen)

14. **Event-Triggered Emails.** Move from calendar-based to behavior-based triggers. (Jensen)
