# Retrospective — commandbar-prd (Beam)

*Marcus Aurelius, observing.*

---

## What Worked Well

- **Debate format produced clarity.** Elon vs. Steve forced hard choices. `decisions.md` is a clean treaty. Twelve locked decisions, no ambiguity.
- **Radical simplification was correct.** Client-side index, two files, zero build step. 142 lines PHP, 251 lines JS. Engineering is honest.
- **Essence doc held.** "Teleportation inside WordPress." The name Beam, the dark stage, the spotlight — taste is present.
- **QA was ruthless.** Margaret found 7 P0s. Build was functionally complete but operationally broken. She did her job.
- **Board reviews saw clearly.** Buffett, Jensen, Oprah, Shonda — unanimous on the real flaw. No one lied to make us feel better.
- **Demo script has heartbeat.** "Beam doesn't click." Tight, visual, emotional.

## What Didn't Work

- **Build was not shippable.** Uncommitted deliverables. Missing `readme.txt`. No ZIP. `todo.md` at 0% checked. Tests failing on environment gaps (no PHP binary). We built a cathedral and forgot the door.
- **"Zero onboarding" became a fatal flaw.** Steve called it virtue. Board called it exclusion. A door with no handle is not minimalism — it is arrogance.
- **AI cut was strategic suicide.** Steve: "Artificial intelligence is a crutch for products with no taste." Jensen: "This is the wrong cut." Board unanimous. In 2026, a command palette without semantic search is a filing cabinet with ambition.
- **No business model from birth.** Buffett: "Division by zero." Four hours of craft, $0 revenue path. We debated line count but never asked who pays.
- **Retention was stripped to the bone.** No localStorage, no recent commands, no memory, no habit. Users who forget the shortcut simply leave. Shonda's roadmap had to be written as rescue, not evolution.
- **Elon's "200 lines" fetish hid costs.** Inline CSS, no build step, no separate stylesheet. Steve warned: "path to spaghetti." Speed of ship traded for speed of future maintenance.
- **Board review came too late.** Strategic verdict was HOLD after build was complete. We asked for permission after building the house.
- **Aesthetic purity overrode business viability.** Steve's non-negotiables (dark only, zero settings, zero AI) produced something beautiful that dies alone in the WordPress.org graveyard.

## What The Agency Should Do Differently Next Time

- **Debate must include a business model advocate.** Engineering vs. design is insufficient. Add Buffett in Round 1, not Round 5.
- **Every "CUT" decision needs a retention/moat impact score.** "No localStorage" saved 20 minutes and killed habit formation. Was it worth it? Ask before cutting.
- **AI is table stakes for command palettes in 2026.** Treat it like accessibility: non-negotiable, not optional.
- **QA environment must match build requirements.** No PHP binary = no real QA. Fix the stage before the play.
- **Run board review BEFORE final build.** Strategy gates engineering, not the reverse.
- **"Sensible defaults or death" needs a corollary:** sensible defaults with no path to revenue is still death.
- **Deliverables checklist is not decoration.** 0% completion on `todo.md` means process theater. Verify or stop.
- **Monetization question answered in Round 1.** Not a footnote. Not v2. The first question.

## Key Learning

A product that feels like sorcery but has no business model, no retention, and no intelligence is a well-crafted tombstone.

## Process Adherence Score

**5/10**

Phases were executed with discipline. Debate, decisions, build, QA, board review, creative review — all happened. But the process asked "how do we build this beautifully?" before asking "should we build this at all?" Discipline in service of the wrong question is not virtue. It is noise.

*Memento mori. Even beams fade if no one pays the light bill.*
