## Product Naming

"CommandBar" is what an engineer calls a spreadsheet function. We're not shipping a feature — we're shipping a feeling. **Call it Beam.** One word. Memorable. Light-speed. When you press Cmd+K, you don't "execute a command" — you *beam* somewhere.

A name is a promise. "CommandBar" promises bureaucracy. "Beam" promises teleportation. It sits on your tongue like a secret. It is the difference between a filing cabinet and a magic wand. If the name doesn't make someone's heart beat faster, go back to the drawing board.

## Design Philosophy

Great design is not how much you add — it's how much you remove and still have it work. This modal isn't a dialog box; it's a spotlight on a dark stage. The rest of WordPress disappears. No chrome. No clutter. No "powered by" badges. Just intent and result.

We are putting a piece of macOS inside wp-admin, and it needs to feel like it was carved from a single block of aluminum. Every corner radius, every shade of blue, every millisecond of animation must whisper the same thing: *someone cared.* That is what makes it insanely great.

## User Experience

The first 30 seconds must feel like the first time you used Spotlight. Press Cmd+K. The screen dims. The world falls away. The noise of the admin sidebar, the dashboard widgets, the notification badges — all of it vanishes into black. There is only a field, a cursor, and possibility.

Type three letters — "abo" — and there is your About page, glowing in blue, waiting for your command. Hit Enter. You are there. No menus. No hunting. No remembering whether Pages lives under Content or under some plugin's vanity tab. It feels like cheating, and that is exactly right. If it doesn't feel like cheating, we haven't gone far enough.

## Brand Voice

Confident. Quiet. No apologies, no explanations. We never say "Click here to get started." We never say "Oops, something went wrong." We say what happened, plainly, and get out of the way. The product speaks through its pixels, not through paragraphs.

When words are necessary, they are few and they are true. We don't "facilitate administrative navigation." We *go.* We don't "encounter an error state." We *pause.* The voice of Beam is the voice of a trusted friend who opens the door without knocking — because they already know you're home.

## What We Say NO To

NO settings page. If it needs a settings page on day one, we have already failed. Sensible defaults or death.

NO light mode. The dark palette is the palette. It is not a theme. It is not a preference. It is focus made visible.

NO onboarding wizard. If a user can't figure out Beam in ten seconds, the design is broken, not the user. Tutorials are confessions of failure.

NO AI suggestions. Artificial intelligence is a crutch for products with no taste. If you can't predict what a user wants with good design, you don't deserve to predict it with a model.

NO plugin activation inside the palette. That's administration, not navigation. Stay in your lane.

NO "recent commands" cluttering the empty state. Nothing is more beautiful than a blank search field full of possibility. Memory is the enemy of momentum.

## The Emotional Hook

WordPress has always been a bicycle for the mind. But for too long, riding that bicycle meant pedaling through mud — clicking through nested menus, hunting for pages, forgetting where settings live, drowning in the same ten thousand decisions someone else made for you in 2003.

Beam changes the physics. You think "I need to edit the team page" and three keystrokes later, you are there. You feel like a sorcerer. You feel respected. The tool gets out of the way so completely that you forget it exists — and that is the highest compliment any object can receive.

That feeling, that WordPress finally moves at the speed of thought, is why people will love this. Not because it has more features. Because it removes the distance between what they want and what they get. That is the whole game. Everything else is noise.
