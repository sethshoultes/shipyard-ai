# Emdash Themes — Steve Jobs Review

## Product Naming: Kill These Names Immediately

Ember, Drift, Forge, Bloom, Slate. These aren't names — they're placeholder words someone grabbed from a thesaurus. They sound like paint swatches at Home Depot.

**The name should be: Palette.**

Not "Emdash Themes." Not "Theme Collection." Just **Palette**. One word. What do artists use? A palette. What holds the colors that define your brand? A palette. It's intuitive. It's evocative. It's memorable.

And the five themes? Number them. Palette One through Five. Let the design speak, not clever words.

## Design Philosophy: Stop Decorating, Start Architecting

This PRD reads like a checklist of aesthetic ingredients. "Serif headings, burnt orange, asymmetric grids." You're describing decorations, not experiences.

What makes this insanely great? **Constraint creates character.** Each palette should have ONE idea executed with ruthless consistency. Drift shouldn't just have "lots of whitespace" — whitespace should BE the design. Every pixel of negative space should feel intentional, like a Japanese garden.

The current approach is additive: stack features until it looks different. Great design is subtractive: what's the ONE thing that makes this unmistakably itself?

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

Right now, you're asking users to read descriptions. "Best for: spas, yoga studios, wellness brands." That's failure. If you have to explain who it's for, the design hasn't done its job.

The experience should be: *click, gasp, I know exactly what this is.*

When someone sees Forge, they should feel like they just walked into a command center. Not because you told them it's "terminal-inspired" — because the design *commands*.

## Brand Voice: Silent Confidence

These themes shouldn't speak at all. The brand voice is visual. When the design is right, words become irrelevant.

Stop writing copy about the themes. Let people experience them. A screenshot should say more than any description ever could.

## What to Say NO To

- **NO** to horizontal scroll sections. Clever gimmicks that frustrate users.
- **NO** to dark mode toggles on every theme. Pick one. Commit.
- **NO** to five pages per theme. Start with ONE page that's perfect.
- **NO** to "self-contained, no external dependencies" — that's engineering speak in a design doc.
- **NO** to redesigning existing sites simultaneously. Ship the themes first. Prove they work.

You're spreading thin instead of going deep. Five mediocre themes help no one. Two extraordinary ones change everything.

## The Emotional Hook: Why People Will LOVE This

People don't want themes. They want to feel like their business looks as good as the businesses they admire. They want to stop feeling embarrassed when they send someone to their website.

The hook isn't variety — it's *identity*. When someone chooses Palette Three, they're not picking colors. They're declaring who they are. That's the promise: **your website finally feels like you.**

Make that true, and they'll tell everyone.
