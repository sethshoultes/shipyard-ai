# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
**Great Minds Agency**
**Re: LocalGenius Frontend Launch**

---

## Product Naming

**LocalGenius is wrong.** It sounds like a yellow pages directory from 2004. We need one word that captures the magic: when a customer asks a question at 11 PM and gets a perfect answer instantly.

**The name is BEACON.**

A beacon guides people home. It shines when it's dark. It's always on. One word. Ownable. Memorable. "Install Beacon on your site" — simple. "Beacon answered 127 questions this week" — powerful.

## Design Philosophy

This must be **invisible until needed, indispensable when used.**

The chat bubble doesn't scream "AI chatbot!" — it whispers "I'm here if you need me." The interface isn't cluttered with branding or features. It's a conversation, period. Like how the original iPhone removed every button until only the essential remained.

The admin dashboard has ONE job on first launch: get you live in 60 seconds. Not "configure 47 settings." Not "watch this tutorial." Pick your business type. Confirm your city. Done. The widget appears. Magic.

**Insanely great means your grandmother can install it before her coffee gets cold.**

## User Experience — First 30 Seconds

You install the plugin. The screen doesn't explode with tooltips and upsells.

Instead: "You run a **Italian Restaurant** in **Austin, Texas**, right?"
One button: **"Yes, that's me."**

Instantly, 15 FAQs populate. "Do you deliver?" "Do you take reservations?" "Are you open Sundays?"

The preview pane shows your site with the chat bubble already there, pulsing gently. You click it. It expands. It feels *alive.*

You think: "Holy shit, this already works."

**That feeling — *it already works* — is the entire product.**

## Brand Voice

Beacon doesn't talk like a SaaS tool. It talks like a confident sous chef who's run service for 20 years.

Not: "Your engagement metrics increased 23% week-over-week."
Instead: "127 people asked questions. You answered every one. Your competitors didn't."

Not: "Upgrade to Pro for advanced analytics."
Instead: "You're #3 in Austin. Here's how to beat #2."

**Direct. Specific. Zero bullshit.** Like a coach who respects you enough to tell the truth.

## What to Say NO To

**NO multi-language support at launch.** Serve Austin perfectly before you serve Madrid poorly.

**NO drag-and-drop FAQ reordering.** If someone needs to reorder FAQs, we failed at auto-generating the right ones.

**NO white-label tier.** Agencies will beg for it. Say no. Let them beg louder. Scarcity creates value.

**NO customizable fonts or animations.** You don't get to make the chat bubble bounce. We designed it. It's perfect. Don't touch it.

**Every feature you don't build is a feature that can't break, can't confuse, can't slow you down.**

## The Emotional Hook

Here's why people will LOVE this:

Every business owner has stayed up at night wondering: "Did I lose that customer because I didn't answer fast enough?"

Beacon makes that anxiety disappear. The chat bubble is always on. It answers at 2 AM on Sunday. It never gets tired. It never forgets the delivery radius.

The Weekly Digest isn't a report — it's validation. "You're winning. Here's proof. Here's how to win more."

**The hook isn't AI. It's peace of mind.** It's the feeling of "my business is covered, even when I'm asleep."

That's why the mom-and-pop pizza place will pay $29/month forever. Not because of features. Because Beacon makes them feel like they're finally keeping up with the chain restaurants that have 24/7 call centers.

**We're not selling software. We're selling the confidence that you'll never lose a customer to a unanswered question again.**
