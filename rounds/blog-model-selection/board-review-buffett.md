# Board Review: blog-model-selection
**Reviewer:** Warren Buffett
**Date:** 2026-04-15

## Score: 3/10
Content marketing disguised as revenue.

## Unit Economics
**Cost to acquire:** Zero. Blog post. No distribution budget, no funnel metrics, no CAC measurement.
**Cost to serve:** One-time content creation. Hosting negligible.
**Problem:** No user to acquire. No conversion path. Content floating in void.

## Revenue Model
**Current state:** Hobby blog.
**Monetization:** None visible.
**Business indicators missing:**
- No product mentioned
- No pricing
- No call-to-action beyond "build something"
- No lead capture
- No sales pipeline

This teaches AI engineers to optimize LLM costs. Where's the business that captures value from that knowledge?

## Competitive Moat
**Moat depth:** Puddle.

Anyone with access to Claude SDK can write this in 48 hours:
- Technical insight = "use cheap models for simple tasks" (common sense)
- TERSE optimization = obvious once stated
- Implementation = literally just `model: 'haiku'` parameter

**No defensibility:**
- No proprietary data
- No network effects
- No switching costs
- No unique technology
- Easy to replicate content

First-mover advantage expires when someone at OpenAI writes "GPT-4 vs GPT-3.5 for Agentic Workflows."

## Capital Efficiency
**Engineering time:** Unknown. PRD says p2 priority.
**Output value:** Educational content.
**Opportunity cost:** What didn't get built while writing this?

**Better capital allocation:**
- Build SDK feature that auto-selects models based on phase type
- Create benchmarking tool that measures hallucination rates
- Package this knowledge into paid consulting service
- Ship product that implements this strategy, not blog explaining strategy

Giving away intellectual property with no capture mechanism.

## Missing: The Business
Strong technical insight. Zero business model.

**Questions unanswered:**
- Who pays for this knowledge?
- What converts readers to customers?
- What product does Shipyard sell?
- How does blog traffic → revenue?

**What good looks like:**
- Blog teaches concept
- CTA: "Try Shipyard's Agent Cost Optimizer"
- Reader enters funnel
- Conversion to paid tier
- LTV > CAC measured

## The Buffett Test
Would I invest in company that writes blog posts about cost optimization without selling cost optimization service?

No.

## Recommendation
**Don't publish yet.** Build the business first.

Then blog becomes:
1. Top-of-funnel content
2. Trust builder
3. Technical credibility
4. Lead generation

Right now it's free consulting.
