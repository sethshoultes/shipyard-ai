- Built elaborate review pipeline for nonexistent artifact
- Board reviewed content quality before content existed

- Cart before horse. Reviews before work.

## What Agency Should Do Differently

**Validate deliverables exist before reviews**
- QA blocker should halt all downstream work
- No design review until file exists
- No board review until product ships
- Gate: deliverable exists → proceed to quality reviews

**Debate strategy before tactics**
- Content vs platform decision comes first
- Business model before blog post structure
- Retention architecture in V1, not V1.1 patch
- Jensen/Buffett concerns should block, not advise

**Execution gates**
- After debate: "Did we build the thing?"
- Before commit: "Does deliverable directory have files?"
- Before QA: "Can we test what exists?"
- Automate checks. Trust but verify.

**Surface strategic gaps earlier**
- Board review in planning phase, not after
- "What business are we in?" before "what's the hook?"
- Retention design in PRD, not retrofit
- Platform strategy as prerequisite, not afterthought

**Stop when blocked**
- P0 QA blocker = all work stops
- No design polish for missing artifact
- No retention roadmap for terminal post
- Fix foundation before decoration

**Value working software**
- Plans are worthless. Planning is invaluable.
- But shipping is everything.
- 742 lines of debate < 1 published blog post
- Perfect plan that ships nothing = failure
