# Shonda's Review: blog-model-selection

## Score: 7/10
Reader learns something valuable but has zero reason to return.

---

## Story Arc: 6/10

**Signup to "aha":**
- No signup. Static blog post.
- Arc exists internally: problem → cost math → solution → results.
- Emotional beats land: "hallucinated 100+ APIs" → anxiety → "$207.50 vs $13.50" → relief.
- Missing: where does reader go AFTER reading?

**Journey ends at knowledge, not action.**

---

## Retention Hooks: 3/10

**What brings them back tomorrow?**
- Nothing. Post = terminal experience.

**What brings them back next week?**
- Nothing. No sequel promised, no unresolved tension.

**Critical miss:**
- "Build something" is a CTA, not a hook.
- Hook would be: "Part 2: How we built the TERSE parser that cut tokens 94%"
- Or: "Subscribe for the Opus vs Sonnet benchmarks dropping next week"

**Zero retention architecture.**

---

## Content Flywheel: 4/10

**Does content generate more content?**
- Post teaches model selection strategy.
- Strategy contains data (cost per phase, token savings).
- Data could become comparison tables, benchmarks, case studies.
- But: no mechanism to capture reader implementations.
- No "share your model selection table" CTA.
- No community feedback loop.

**Potential flywheel:**
1. Reader implements strategy
2. Reader shares cost savings
3. Company features reader's data
4. New readers see social proof
5. More implementations

**Current state: one-way broadcast.**

---

## Emotional Cliffhangers: 2/10

**What makes users curious about what's next?**
- Post ends with "Build something."
- Neat bow. Complete package.
- No unresolved questions.
- No teaser: "Next week: The one Opus use case that justified 10x costs"

**Missed opportunities:**
- "We found ONE scenario where Haiku outperformed Sonnet on code generation—"
- "The 7-plugin build revealed a hidden cost we didn't expect—"
- "Our next experiment: multi-model consensus voting—"

**Reader leaves satisfied but not hungry.**

---

## Narrative Strengths

✓ Opens with visceral failure: "100+ API violations"
✓ Stakes escalate: "$207.50 per plugin"
✓ Solution feels earned: cost comparison builds credibility
✓ Voice is confident: "Build something" lands
✓ Technical depth without jargon soup

---

## Narrative Weaknesses

✗ No protagonist: Who discovered this? Where's the human story?
✗ No setbacks: Solution works perfectly, first try
✗ No vulnerability: "We learned the hard way" mentioned in PRD, absent in post
✗ No next episode: Story ends instead of pausing
✗ No community element: reader is spectator, not participant

---

## Product as Story: 5/10

**Current narrative:**
- Shipyard blog teaches AI engineering.
- Posts are standalone knowledge drops.
- Reader consumes, leaves.

**Missing:**
- **Series architecture:** "Model Selection" → "TERSE Formatting" → "Pipeline Orchestration"
- **Reader progression:** Beginner → Intermediate → Expert tracks
- **Social proof loop:** "42 teams shipped this strategy last month"
- **Insider access:** "Join 500 engineers getting weekly pipeline insights"

**Blog tells one story. Product needs cinematic universe.**

---

## Content Strategy: 6/10

**What exists:**
- High-quality evergreen content
- Aimed at precise audience (AI engineers)
- Practical, opinionated, useful

**What's missing:**
- **Content ladder:** Entry → Deep Dive → Case Study → Community
- **Format variety:** Blog → Video → Twitter thread → Newsletter
- **Engagement triggers:** Comments? Discourse forum? Discord reactions?
- **Attribution:** "Built by [Engineer Name] after debugging 14 hours of hallucinations"

**Good content. Not yet content strategy.**

---

## Recommendations

**Ship retention hooks:**
1. Add series trailer at bottom: "Part 2: Advanced TERSE patterns (shipping April 22)"
2. Add cliffhanger: "Next post: The Opus use case that saved us $40K"
3. Add newsletter signup: "Get model selection benchmarks in your inbox"

**Build content flywheel:**
1. Add "Share your results" form
2. Feature reader implementations monthly
3. Build leaderboard: "Fastest pipeline times using this strategy"

**Create protagonist:**
1. Name the engineer who discovered this
2. Show the 2AM debug session that sparked insight
3. Include the Slack thread where team argued about costs

**Engineer next episode:**
1. Tease unresolved question at end of each post
2. Create post series with narrative throughline
3. Build anticipation: "Part 3 reveals the hidden Haiku advantage"

---

## Bottom Line

**Post teaches. Product doesn't hook.**

Reader leaves smarter but has zero reason to bookmark, return, or tell friends.

Content = 8/10.
Strategy = 4/10.
Retention = 2/10.

**Fix:** Turn blog into series. Turn readers into characters. Turn endings into pauses.

One great post is a lecture.
Ten connected posts is a season.

**Make them want Episode 2.**
