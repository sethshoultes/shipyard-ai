# Maya Angelou Review: Homeport Email Copy

## Verdict: Almost there, but polishing what's already strong, not broken.

---

## Does the language feel human, not corporate?

**YES.** The "I" voice is confident. No marketing speak. No feature lists disguised as care. Reads like someone who builds things talking to someone who hired them.

The vulnerability works: "I'm not like that" (day 90), "I'm still thinking about..." Real people say this.

One risk: "Homeport is Shipyard's way of..." That footer line is the only corporate moment. It explains instead of showing.

---

## Is there rhythm in the sentences?

**MOSTLY.** Best rhythm in day 7 and day 365. Listen:

> "Seven days. Your site shipped seven days ago, and it's been running clean."

That lands. Short-long. Beat-pause-beat.

Day 30 loses the rhythm:
> "I've been thinking about {project_url} since we shipped it. Not obsessing—just checking in the way you'd expect someone who cares about their work to do."

That second sentence trips over itself. Too many qualifiers.

Day 180 has the weakest rhythm. Long explanatory sentences that all sound the same length. No punch. No silence.

---

## Does the headline stop you?

**Day 7:** "Your site is breathing on its own now" — YES.
**Day 30:** "Does it feel like yours yet?" — YES.
**Day 90:** "We're still here (most agencies aren't)" — Trying too hard.
**Day 180:** "Time for a refresh?" — Generic. Sounds like a SaaS upsell.
**Day 365:** "Happy Anniversary" — Almost. But paired with the content, it works.

---

## What's trying too hard?

**Day 90, line 5:** "You're old news."
Too self-aware. You don't need to tell them what bad agencies do. Just be different.

**Day 180, line 15:** "The best time to refresh is when you have something to refresh *from*. Not guesses. Not trends. Real information."
This is a tweet, not a letter. It's performing wisdom.

**Day 365, line 11:** "Twelve months is a long time in internet years."
Cliché. Everyone says this. Cut it.

---

## Three Weakest Lines — Rewritten

### 1. Day 90, Subject Line
**Current:**
"We're still here (most agencies aren't)"

**Rewrite:**
"Ninety days in"

Why: You don't need to position against other agencies. The body already does that work. The subject line should be time-stamped, not defensive.

---

### 2. Day 180, line 15
**Current:**
"The best time to refresh is when you have something to refresh *from*. Not guesses. Not trends. Real information."

**Rewrite:**
"The best time to refresh? When you've learned something worth building on."

Why: Same idea. Half the syllables. No italics doing the heavy lifting.

---

### 3. Day 30, line 7
**Current:**
"I've been thinking about {project_url} since we shipped it. Not obsessing—just checking in the way you'd expect someone who cares about their work to do."

**Rewrite:**
"I've been thinking about {project_url}. Not obsessing. Just the way you'd check on something you built."

Why: The original apologizes for caring. The rewrite owns it.

---

## Final Note

This copy already feels more human than 95% of lifecycle emails. The rewrites aren't fixes—they're refinements. The voice is there. The care is there. The rhythm just needs tightening in spots.

You'll know it's working when replies sound like someone talking to a person, not a company.
