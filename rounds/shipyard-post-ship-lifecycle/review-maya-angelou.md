# Maya Angelou Review: Shipyard Post-Ship Lifecycle

## Human, Not Corporate?
**Yes.** Voice feels like someone who built this with their hands. "I'm not like that" hits different than "We pride ourselves on ongoing support."

## Rhythm?
**Mixed.** Day 7 moves. Short sentences. Clean breath. Day 90 and 180 get preachy—too many clauses, too much explaining.

## Headlines That Stop You?
- Day 7: **YES.** "Your site is breathing on its own now" — visceral.
- Day 30: **NO.** "It's been 30 days. How are things?" — calendar reminder, not feeling.
- Day 90: **ALMOST.** "We're still here (most agencies aren't)" — good contrast, but smug edge.
- Day 180: **NO.** "Time for a refresh?" — sales question, not connection.
- Day 365: **SOFT YES.** "Happy Anniversary" — warm, but expected.

## Trying Too Hard?
Day 90, line 5: "You're old news." Overwriting. We know what you mean.
Day 180, line 15: "That's gold." Salesman tell, not builder truth.
Day 365, line 9: "The entire industry took steps you might not have noticed." Condescending.
