# Copy Review: EventDash

**Reviewer:** Maya Angelou
**Date:** 2026-04-12
**Project:** eventdash-fix

---

## The Soul of the Matter

Child, I have read through these files — the code, the test results, the research notes — and I find myself looking not at what was said, but at how it might make a person *feel* when they sit down to create an event.

Let me tell you what I found.

---

## Does the Language Feel Human, Not Corporate?

**Mostly, yes.** There is mercy in simplicity.

The decision to say **"Created."** instead of "Event created successfully!" — that is a person speaking, not a machine congratulating itself. When you strip away the excess, you reveal the truth.

**"No events yet."** — Four words. Honest. Not apologetic. Not "You haven't created any events yet! Why not add one?" which would feel like someone tugging at your sleeve.

The form labels — **"Name," "Date," "Description"** — are plain as bread. And bread nourishes.

**What concerns me:** The technical documentation drowns in jargon. "Block Kit JSON schema validated" — who is this for? The human who will create an event does not need to know she is interacting with Block Kit. She needs to know her gathering will happen.

---

## Is There Rhythm in the Sentences?

In the UI copy, **yes** — short beats, natural pauses:

- "Created."
- "No events yet."
- "Events"
- "Create Event"

These have breath between them.

In the documentation, **no** — the sentences march like soldiers on parade, uniform and exhausting:

> "The EventDash plugin is already functional via API. The core implementation is correct."

That is information. It is not communication. Communication has blood in it.

---

## Does the Headline Stop You?

**"Create Event"** — it does the job. It is clear. But it does not *stop* you. It does not make you feel the weight of what you are about to do: bringing people together.

Headlines should be doors, not labels. This is a label.

**"Events"** as a page header — again, accurate, but it sits there like furniture. It does not invite you to sit down.

---

## Is Anything Trying Too Hard?

**Yes.**

The test documentation is a monument to thoroughness. Every checkbox ticked, every status marked **PASS**, every code path documented. I understand why — someone needed to prove the work was done.

But there is a line between confidence and insistence. When you write:

> "Status: CODE VERIFIED ✅"
> "Status: CODE VERIFIED ✅"
> "Status: CODE VERIFIED ✅"

...seven times in a row, you are no longer informing. You are pleading. You are saying, *please believe me.*

Confidence whispers. Insecurity shouts.

---

## The Three Weakest Lines — And What They Could Be

### 1. Original: "Date (YYYY-MM-DD)"

This is instruction dressed as a label. The format belongs elsewhere — perhaps as helper text, perhaps as a placeholder. As it stands, it makes the user feel she is filling out a government form.

**Rewrite:**

> **"When"**

And let the placeholder in the field say *"March 15, 2026"* — show her what you mean, don't tell her how to comply.

---

### 2. Original: "title and date are required"

This is the voice of a system scolding. Required by whom? The software has revealed it does not trust the user.

**Rewrite:**

> **"Every gathering needs a name and a day."**

Now you are speaking to a person planning something that matters to her.

---

### 3. Original: "No events yet."

This is gentle, but it is passive. It describes an absence without opening a door.

**Rewrite:**

> **"Nothing here — yet."**

The dash creates a pause. The *yet* becomes a promise. You are saying: *This emptiness is waiting to be filled by you.*

---

## Final Reflection

The work here is clean. The bones are good. But remember: people do not come to software to *use* it. They come because they have something to celebrate, someone to gather, a moment to mark.

The copy must honor that. Every word should feel like it was written by someone who understood what it means to bring people together — not by someone who understood JSON.

You will be forgotten if you are merely correct.

You will be remembered if you make them feel *seen.*

---

*"There is no greater agony than bearing an untold story inside you."*

Let your software help people tell theirs.
