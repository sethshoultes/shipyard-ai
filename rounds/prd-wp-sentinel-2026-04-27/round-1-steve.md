# Steve's Positions: WP Sentinel

## Name: Change It

"WP Sentinel" is a sysadmin's perl script from 1998. "WP" is noise. "Sentinel" is a robot guarding a cargo bay.

This isn't a machine — it's a partner. One word: **Keeper**.

"I installed Keeper on my site." Two syllables. Human. Humble. Indispensable.

## Design Philosophy: Invisible Indispensability

The widget shouldn't look like a plugin. It should look like WordPress grew a conscience.

Every pixel earns its place. We don't surface metrics — we tell stories in color and silence. When everything is fine, you barely notice it. When something breaks, it speaks with calm clarity.

Engineering that feels like poetry.

## First 30 Seconds: Magic

Install. Refresh. Done. No setup wizard. No API key begging. No "choose your plan."

The widget appears, already pulsing with your site's health. You open chat: "I checked your site. Your permalinks need attention. Want me to fix them?"

It read your mind before you knew what to ask. That's not AI — that's magic.

## Voice: Brilliant and Kind

Speak like the smartest person in the room who actually likes you. Warm, confident, precise.

No "Error 403." No "remediation protocols." No "we apologize for the inconvenience."

"Your permalinks are broken. I can fix that."

If you wouldn't say it to a friend over coffee, don't write it.

## Say NO To

NO dark mode. NO admin themes. NO enterprise dashboards with 47 metrics. NO airplane cockpits.

NO API keys before value. Health monitoring works instantly. Chat comes later.

NO "Are you sure?" dialogs. Say what happens: "This clears your cache. Your site may be slower for 30 seconds."

NO user manuals. If it needs documentation, the design failed.

NO "Powered by Claude" badges. The technology disappears. The experience is everything.

## Emotional Hook: The End of 3 AM Panic

This is peace of mind — the ultimate luxury. A world-class mechanic living inside your website, checking the oil while you sleep.

Clients won't love it because it uses AI. They'll love it because it makes them feel competent, protected, and calm.

It's not a tool. It's a guardian.
