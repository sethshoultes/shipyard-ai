# Round 1: Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

**Closer.**

Not "Auto-Close." Not "Issue-Autoclose-System." One word. *Closer.* It's what a great salesperson is. It's what this feature does. It closes the loop. It closes the deal. It closes the issue. When someone asks "what shipped today?" the answer is "Closer shipped." Beautiful.

## Design Philosophy

This is a *completion gesture*. Think about the satisfying click when you close a MacBook lid. That's what we're building — the digital equivalent of that moment. The issue opens a door. The pipeline walks through. Closer shuts it behind you. No loose ends. No orphan issues haunting your repo like ghosts.

What makes this insanely great? **Invisibility.** The best features disappear. Users shouldn't *feel* Closer working — they should only notice its absence when it's gone. Like oxygen. You don't celebrate breathing until you can't.

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

There is no first 30 seconds. That's the point.

The user files an issue. They forget about it. Days later, they get a notification: "Issue closed — Shipped via Great Minds pipeline." They click through, see their feature deployed. They smile. They didn't have to do anything. *That's* the experience — the absence of friction, the presence of magic.

## Brand Voice

Confident. Brief. Done.

"Shipped via Great Minds pipeline. Project: {name}" — that's all we say. No exclamation points. No emojis. No "We're excited to announce that your issue has been resolved!" We close the issue like a surgeon closes an incision: professionally, cleanly, with zero excess motion.

## What to Say NO To

- **NO** to configuration options. Close on ship. Period. No toggles.
- **NO** to elaborate comment templates. One line. Ship it.
- **NO** to status labels, transition states, or "processing" indicators. Binary: open or closed.
- **NO** to retry logic with exponential backoff. If GitHub is down, log it and move on. We don't beg.
- **NO** to notifications beyond GitHub's built-in. We close. GitHub notifies. Stay in our lane.

The PRD already says "keep it simple" — that's not a suggestion, it's a commandment. Every line of code is a liability. Every config option is a bug waiting to happen.

## The Emotional Hook

**Closure.**

Humans crave completion. There's a reason we binge entire seasons, finish plates we're not hungry for, read to the end of bad books. The Zeigarnik Effect — our brains literally nag us about unfinished tasks.

Every open issue is a tiny splinter in the mind of someone who filed it. Closer removes that splinter. Automatically. Silently. The user doesn't love the feature — they love the *feeling* of things being handled. Of a system that respects their time enough to clean up after itself.

That's why people will love this: not because it closes issues, but because it closes *loops*. And closed loops mean peace of mind.
