# Steve Jobs — Position Memo

## Product Naming
**AgentBridge is dead on arrival.**

It sounds like a procurement catalog item from 2003 — two words, technical, soulless. We ship **Relay**. One word. A relay receives a signal and passes it forward with perfect fidelity. It implies motion, not stagnation. A bridge just sits there; a relay runs. It is memorable because it actually means something.

When you name a product after what it does, you do not need a tagline. One word enters the vocabulary; two words enter the spreadsheet. I want people to say "I installed Relay on my site" the way they say "I put AirPods in my ears" — naturally, instantly, permanently.

## Design Philosophy
This is the USB-C moment for WordPress.

Before USB-C, every gadget needed a different cable — a drawer of tangled frustration, adapters upon adapters. After USB-C, you just plug in and the world flows through. Relay must feel like it was carved into WordPress by the core team itself — native, invisible, inevitable.

The best design is no design. If the user notices the interface, we have failed. It should feel less like installing a plugin and more like discovering a capability that was always waiting beneath the surface. The admin screen should be so clean that a screenshot of it could hang in a museum of modern art: one button, one field, absolute clarity. No chrome. No noise. Just purpose.

## First 30 Seconds
Install. Tools → Relay. One card. One URL. One token. One copy button. Paste into Claude Desktop. Done.

If it takes forty-five seconds, we go back to the drawing board. No wizards. No configuration. No "Next." The plugin should be so certain of its purpose that it literally pre-fills the Claude config snippet with your actual credentials. You copy once. That is the experience.

The first thirty seconds should feel like a magic trick: where did all the complexity go? If the user has to open documentation, we have already lost them. Documentation is a crutch for broken design. Great products teach themselves through sheer, arrogant simplicity.

## Brand Voice
Confident. Minimal. Human.

We say: "Your site is now listening." We do not say "leverage synergistic AI workflows" or "unlock unprecedented value." We trust the user is intelligent and busy. Short sentences. Active verbs. No jargon on the admin screen — "Connection URL," not "SSE Transport Endpoint."

We speak like someone who has done the hard work so the user does not have to think. Every word in the UI is a promise kept. Every sentence in the readme is a battle against boredom. We are not building a manual; we are building a conviction.

## What We Say NO To
- **NO** onboarding wizards. A wizard is an admission of design failure. If you need seven steps to explain a product, the product is broken.
- **NO** feature toggle grids. The ten tools are not optional; they are obvious. Do not make the user play air-traffic controller with checkboxes.
- **NO** freemium banners, purple gradients, or "Go Pro" buttons in the settings pane. It must look like a system preference, not a marketplace bazaar.
- **NO** technical jargon in the UI. "JSON-RPC" belongs in GitHub, not WordPress admin. "Bearer Token" becomes "Secret Key."
- **NO** scope creep. If it does not help a user connect in thirty seconds, it dies. Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication, and sophistication means saying no to a thousand good ideas so the one great idea can breathe.
- **NO** dark patterns, notification spam, or admin notices begging for reviews. The settings page is sacred ground.

## Emotional Hook
People will love Relay because it makes the future arrive early.

WordPress has been administrative drudgery for a decade — click, wait, save, repeat, lose your will to live. Suddenly, the site listens. You speak to Claude; your site obeys. The emotional hook is liberation.

We are not selling a protocol adapter. We are selling the end of admin fatigue. Your website becomes a participant in the intelligence age — alive, responsive, almost sentient. That feeling is what creates irrational love.

You do not like Relay; you feel *relieved* by it. And relief is the most powerful emotion in software. Our competition is not other plugins; our competition is the status quo — the dull ache of opening wp-admin one more time. We win when users feel like they have been given hours of their life back.

That is not a feature. That is a gift.
