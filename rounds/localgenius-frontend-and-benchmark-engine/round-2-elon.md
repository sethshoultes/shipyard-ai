# Elon Musk — Round 2 Rebuttal

Steve is right about the name. LocalGenius is a coupon app from 2009. SPARK is sharp.
I concede that immediately. Four letters, one breath, sharp as flint.
The customer portal and Stripe billing can stay LocalGenius. The thing the owner installs is SPARK.

But Steve's "recognition before permission" is a beautiful lie.
"No forms, no passwords, no Step 1 of 4" sounds telepathic until you try to build it.
WordPress requires an admin with `install_plugins` capability. Stripe requires an email.
Our API requires a key. The "shock of being seen" requires live scraping arbitrary URLs in under a second.
I already cut that. Steve's first 30 seconds is a movie trailer for a film that would take six months to shoot.
We don't have six months. We have one week.

Steve says the benchmark engine is "dignity." I say it's a leaderboard for a game no one plays.
With 50 installs in 30 days, a restaurant in Peoria sees "#1 of 1."
That's not dignity; that's a participation trophy. Dignity requires density.
Density requires shipping first. You cannot whisper "you are seen" to a database with five rows.
Steve wants to make empty buckets feel poetic. I want to fill the buckets first.

Steve wants "every gradient, every shadow, every transition" to whisper craft.
I want every line of code to whisper "this won't break at 3 AM in Nebraska."
Beauty that requires perfect hosting environments is not beauty; it's a support ticket.
wp-admin is not a museum. It is a utility room. Make it clean, make it fast, and get out.
Every animation Steve adds is a conflict with a caching plugin or shared hosting with 128MB memory.

Steve also missed distribution entirely. He wants poetry; I want a viral loop.
Restaurant owners talk to each other. Where is the "Powered by SPARK" badge?
Where is the public FAQ page that ranks on Google? Where is the web designer partnership channel?
Beautiful products die in obscurity every day. Ugly products with distribution become industries.

Steve would romanticize the widget itself. He wants SPARK to feel inevitable.
But thumbs up/down in the widget is scope creep dressed as polish.
New database columns and API endpoints for a micro-interaction we don't need.
The widget is <10KB. Keep it that way. Shipping means knowing when to stop touching the product.

Where Steve and I agree: say NO to onboarding wizards, twelve-tab dashboards, AI sparkle icons, and SaaS jargon.
These cuts are not compromises; they are force multipliers.
The brand voice he describes is the right voice. The discipline he demands is the right discipline.
But discipline means cutting the benchmark engine, not dressing it in better poetry.

Technical simplicity wins because complexity is fragility.
A static demo in KV never times out. One admin screen never conflicts with WooCommerce.
D1 that only stores configs never hits query limits.
A plugin with six admin screens generates six times the support load at 10,000 installs.
One agent session builds the MVP or nothing. Six admin screens and a benchmark engine is not a session. It is a sprint.
Cut scope until one agent can finish in four hours, or you will ship nothing.

## Where Steve is Right

- **SPARK** is the name. Four letters. Ship it.
- **Brand voice**: Warm, direct, no "leverage" or "drive outcomes." Correct.
- **No admin dashboards with twelve tabs.** We agree: one screen.
- **No AI badges or robot illustrations.** Taste is a filter, not a feature.
- **No Shopify in v1.** Focus is a weapon. A WordPress plugin that tries to be a Shopify app ships in neither ecosystem.

## Top 3 Non-Negotiables

1. **One admin screen only.** Onboarding + toggle. No FAQ manager, no account card, no reordering.
   Every additional screen is a support vector on 10,000 hosting stacks.
2. **Static demo, no live scraping.** Pre-scrape 100 sites into KV.
   Dynamic scraping is a scaling and security liability. It is the only way Steve's "instant recognition" survives without CPU bankruptcy.
3. **No benchmark engine in v1.** Ship when 500+ businesses.
   Until then, it is vanity data with GDPR risk and empty buckets.
   Rankings without density are a lie told with a beautiful font.

Stop polishing the mirror. Give the customer a bridge.
