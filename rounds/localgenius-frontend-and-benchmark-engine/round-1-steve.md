# Steve Jobs — Round 1 Positions

## 1. Product Naming: "LocalGenius" Dies Today

"LocalGenius" is a name chosen by a committee that feared being misunderstood. It is two words, five syllables, and sounds like a coupon app from 2009. The product must have a one-word name or it will never live in the heart.

The widget is called SPARK. The product is SPARK. The company can be whatever the lawyers need. But the thing a restaurant owner installs, talks about to their friend, and checks on their phone at midnight is SPARK. Four letters. One breath. Sharp as a flint. When they say "I have SPARK on my site," it sounds like they have an advantage, not a plugin.

## 2. Design Philosophy: Telepathy, Not Technology

If this product is "easy to use," we have already failed. Easy is table stakes. It must feel inevitable. The benchmark card should not look like analytics; it should feel like a standing ovation in a small room. The onboarding screen should feel like walking into a restaurant where the host knows your name and your favorite table. Every gradient, every shadow, every transition must whisper: "this was made by people who love craft."

## 3. The First 30 Seconds: Recognition Before Permission

The first 30 seconds are not an "onboarding flow." They are a first date. The user enters their URL. Before they have finished blinking, we show them their own business—hours, cuisine, photos—already breathing inside our widget. No forms. No passwords. No "Step 1 of 4." Just the shock of being seen. That is the hook. That is the moment they fall in love.

## 4. Brand Voice: Speak Like a Great Bartender, Not a SaaS Manual

Never say "AI-powered," "leverage," "solution," "optimize," or "drive outcomes." Those words are pus. Our voice is warm, direct, and confident. We say: "You're #3 of 47." Not: "You have achieved a competitive ranking within your vertical cohort." We say: "Your customers asked 12 questions this week. You answered every one." We speak like a local business owner speaks to a regular—minus the fatigue.

## 5. What To Say NO To: Simplicity is the Ultimate Sophistication

NO to onboarding wizards with progress bars. NO to admin dashboards with twelve tabs. NO to "customize your theme" beyond light, dark, and brand match. NO to supporting Shopify in v1, v2, or v3. NO to "AI" badges, sparkle icons, or robot illustrations. NO to monthly newsletters. NO to feature requests from people who will never pay. We are editing the world down to what matters. When in doubt, remove.

## 6. The Emotional Hook: Dignity for the Invisible

Every local business owner is fighting alone against chains, aggregators, and algorithms that bury them. At 11 PM they feel invisible. SPARK tells them: "You are not invisible. You are #3 of 47. You are seen. You are winning." The benchmark engine is not data. It is dignity. It is proof that their late nights matter. People will not love this because it answers questions. They will love it because it remembers they exist.
