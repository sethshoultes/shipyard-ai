# SPARK — 2-Minute Demo Script

NARRATOR
You know that sound? The phone ringing while you're plating a pork chop? That's someone asking if you're open on Thanksgiving.

[SCREEN: Phone buzzing on a steel counter. Kitchen. Timestamp 8:47 PM.]

NARRATOR
Forty times a day. Same six questions. You didn't open a restaurant to become a human FAQ page.

[SCREEN: WordPress admin. Cursor clicks the single menu item: SPARK.]

NARRATOR
So. One field. Your URL. That's the whole onboarding.

[SCREEN: SPARK admin screen. Single input. Hands type "tastypizza.example.com" and hit tab.]

NARRATOR
Watch this. Name, hours, brand color—already there. Ten questions your customers actually ask. Do you deliver? Gluten-free? Vegan cheese?

[SCREEN: Preview card populates. Ten FAQ toggles appear. Five are ON.]

NARRATOR
You toggle off catering. You're not doing weddings this month. You leave delivery on. You write a greeting—"Hey, we're open late"—and flip the switch.

[SCREEN: Toggle slides to ON. Widget preview renders bottom-right, matching the brand color.]

NARRATOR
Forty-three seconds. The whole widget's ten kilobytes. Your site doesn't flinch.

[SCREEN: Live frontend site. Bubble appears. Visitor clicks. Chat opens: "Hey, we're open late."]

NARRATOR
But here's the thing.

[SCREEN: Scroll down the same admin screen. Benchmark section loads.]

NARRATOR
Every week, SPARK looks at every restaurant in Chicago. Anonymized. No names, no spying. Just math.

[SCREEN: Benchmark card builds. "Rank: 3 of 47." "Percentile: 94." Green arrow trending up.]

NARRATOR
You thought you were just keeping the lights on. You're third in the city for answering customer questions. Third.

[SCREEN: Close-up on the number. "3 of 47." Beat.]

NARRATOR
That's not a dashboard. That's proof you're winning.

[SCREEN: Fade to black. SPARK wordmark.]
