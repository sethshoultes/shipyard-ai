# Position: Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Name

**Calm Commit** is dead. It sounds like a meditation app for seniors. We're calling this **Still**. One word. A state of mind. When your repository is clean, your history sings, and your intent is crystal clear — you are still. That's the product.

## Design Philosophy

This isn't a tool. It's a lens. You don't interact with a lens; you look through it. Still disappears completely until the exact moment you need it, then it presents exactly one perfect suggestion — not three, not five, one. Making the user choose between options is admitting our algorithm isn't good enough yet. We ship when there's one right answer.

## The First 30 Seconds

You stage your files. A single line appears, ghost-like, above your cursor — the commit message you would have written if you'd slept eight hours and drank one perfect cup of coffee. You hit Enter. It's done. You feel smarter than you are. That's the whole demo.

## Brand Voice

Still speaks like a master craftsman who doesn't waste words. No exclamation points. No emojis. No "Hey there!" We say: "Refactor authentication middleware. Extract token validation to reduce cyclomatic complexity." Period. Confidence without arrogance. Precision without coldness.

## What We Say NO To

NO configuration panels. NO "learn your style" onboarding. NO picking from multiple suggestions. NO web dashboards. NO team analytics. NO supporting rebase workflows we're not proud of. If it requires a Settings tab, it doesn't ship. Simplicity isn't the absence of clutter; it's the presence of only what deserves to be there.

## Emotional Hook

Developers secretly hate the version of themselves who writes "fix stuff." Still rescues them from that shame. It turns the chaotic, embarrassing trail of "wip" and "omg" into a museum of intent. People won't just use this. They'll brag about their git history. They'll screenshot their logs. Pride is the most addictive feature.
