# Maya Angelou Copy Review

---

## Does it feel human?

**Yes.**

Voice is sharp, present, real. Talks like someone who's done the work.
No corporate buffer. No hiding behind "solutions" or "ecosystems."
Reads like builder talking to builder.

---

## Is there rhythm?

**Mostly.**

Strong rhythm in short bursts:
- "You write a PRD. We build and deploy..."
- "No meetings, no revisions, no waiting."
- "Ship. Deploy. PRD."

Lost rhythm in objections section — answers get long, defensive, explanatory.

---

## Does the headline stop you?

**The one-liner? No.**

"Autonomous AI agency that ships production-ready Emdash sites from your PRD in weeks, not months."

Too much qualification. Reader trips on "Emdash" before they feel anything.
Doesn't land emotionally. Intellectually correct ≠ memorable.

**The elevator pitch? Yes.**

"You write a PRD. We build and deploy..." — immediate, concrete, human.

---

## Is anything trying too hard?

**Yes.**

"Words We Never Use" section feels performative. Listing what you're *not* shows insecurity.
The swagger undermines itself.

"Gross." "Never." "See above." — edge without purpose.
Being opinionated is good. Being showy about it reads try-hard.

---

## 3 Weakest Lines Rewritten

### 1. One-Liner (Original)
> "Autonomous AI agency that ships production-ready Emdash sites from your PRD in weeks, not months."

**Rewrite:**
> "Write a PRD. Get a live site. Four weeks."

**Why:**
Rhythm. Clarity. Confidence.
Reader feels speed before they analyze terms.

---

### 2. Target Audience — Local Business (Original)
> "You're too busy running your business to deal with web designers. We'll build your site in three weeks for less than you'd spend on a contractor."

**Rewrite:**
> "You run a business. We'll give you a website that works. Three weeks. Done."

**Why:**
Original condescends ("too busy"). Rewrite respects.
Shorter = stronger.

---

### 3. Objection 5 — Freelancer (Original)
> "A freelancer might cost less per hour. But you'll spend 3-6 months in Slack, waiting for status updates, explaining requirements, asking for revisions. We charge flat tokens, deliver in 4-6 weeks, and never miss a deadline. Total cost of ownership? We're a steal."

**Rewrite:**
> "A freelancer costs less per hour. You'll pay more in time. We charge once. We ship fast. You move on."

**Why:**
Original is defensive, long-winded.
Rewrite is certain. Doesn't beg. States fact, exits.

---

## Final Note

Copy knows what it wants to be: direct, builder-first, no BS.
Gets in its own way when it performs rebellion instead of just being clear.

Cut the posturing. Keep the punch.
