# Steve's Design Edict: Changelog Theatre

## Name: CUT

"Changelog Theatre" is bureaucratic poetry. It sounds like a community college improv troupe. **The name is Cut.** Three letters. One syllable. Unforgettable. A film cut is the fundamental act of storytelling; a code cut is the fundamental act of shipping. It is visceral, decisive, and beautiful. Say it out loud: "We just Cut a new release." That's the sound of scissors through the ribbon. That's the name. Cut is not a label. It is a promise: decisive, crafted, permanent.

## Design Philosophy: Dignity, Not Decoration

Developers pour their souls into 400 lines of code at 2 AM, and their reward is a grey box that says "Fixed CSS bug." This is a crime against craft. Cut doesn't decorate your changelog — it **restores dignity to your work.** Every frame should feel like the opening titles of a Pixar film. Calm. Confident. Typography that breathes. Whitespace that commands attention. No clip art. No "fun" animated gifs. This is not a birthday card. It is a marquee. Motion is not decoration — it is punctuation. A gentle slide. A confident fade. Nothing bounces. Nothing wobbles. Restraint is power.

## The First 30 Seconds: The Gasp

They install the plugin. They paste their readme. They press the button. And they gasp. Not "oh, that's nice." A **gasp.** Their "Fixed bug in settings page" has become a cinematic moment with a calm voice saying, "We heard you. Settings now remember your choices." In thirty seconds, the invisible becomes visible. That feeling — the first time you scrolled an iPod wheel — that's the bar. Miss it, and you've built a PowerPoint generator. Hit it, and you've changed how an entire industry celebrates its labor.

## Brand Voice: The Director, Not the Salesman

We do not say "Hello fellow developers!" We do not use exclamation points. Ever. We speak like a director who trusts their lead actor. Warm, editorial, absolute. "Your work deserves an audience." "Every release is a premiere." "They used to ignore your updates. Now they wait for them." Periods only. Confidence without volume. We are not their friend. We are not their vendor. We are the curator of their moment.

## What We Say NO To

NO templates. Templates are creative bankruptcy disguised as choice. We pick the typeface. We pick the motion. We pick the voice. The user picks their story — we handle the stage.

NO customization panels with forty-seven toggles. If you want to make it ugly, use a different product. Simplicity means removing, not adding.

NO generic stock music. If it sounds like an elevator in a dentist's office, it dies.

NO "enterprise tier." NO "white-label option." NO "custom CSS injection." This is a sculpture, not a pottery class.

NO support for every changelog format under the sun. We pick one. We do it perfectly. Then we consider a second.

NO "API-first" obsession. The product is the experience. The API is a side effect, not a strategy.

## The Emotional Hook: The Refrigerator Door

Parents tape their children's drawings to the refrigerator because they see the work, and they want the world to see it too. Developers are invisible. Users click "update now" without reading a word, without knowing a human being suffered for that fix. Cut makes the invisible visible. It says: **I see you. I see your work. And it matters.** That is not a feature. That is love.

## The Final Cut

Mediocrity is a virus. It spreads through committees and compromise. We do not ship a settings page. We ship a feeling. If the team cannot look at the output and feel pride — genuine, chest-swelling pride — then we burn it and start again. This is not a plugin. It is a standing ovation for people who have never heard applause.
