# ClipCraft — 2-Minute Demo Script

---

**NARRATOR**
I wrote twelve hundred words this morning. Good ones, I think. Three sources, two interviews, a lede I’m actually proud of.

[SCREEN: A blog post glowing on a laptop. Cursor blinks. A phone sits next to it, dark.]

**NARRATOR**
And now I’m supposed to turn it into... what? Fifteen vertical clips? By Thursday?

[SCREEN: Hand hovering over a phone. Screen lights up. TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts — an endless scroll of faces and motion. Cut back to the laptop. The blog post looks very still.]

**NARRATOR**
I don’t have an editor. I don’t have a voice actor. I definitely don’t have six hours to stare at a timeline and will my paragraph breaks into jump cuts.

[SCREEN: Cursor clicks a tab. Reel.shipyard.company loads. Clean white page. One field.]

**NARRATOR**
So I paste the URL.

[SCREEN: URL drops into the input field. A single button: "Make Reel." Cursor clicks it.]

**NARRATOR**
And I wait. Not long.

[SCREEN: Status card appears. "Reading." Then "Outlining." Then "Voiceover." A progress bar that actually moves.]

**NARRATOR**
It’s pulling the article. Finding the spine. Writing a script I didn’t have to write. Generating a voice that sounds like someone who slept eight hours and believes in the topic.

[SCREEN: Behind the status bar, abstract frames flicker — title cards, pull quotes, b-roll slots assembling themselves.]

**NARRATOR**
I didn’t pick a template. I didn’t sync audio. I didn’t keyframe a single caption.

[SCREEN: Progress bar snaps to full. Status reads: "Ready."]

**NARRATOR**
I just gave it words.

[SCREEN: A vertical video fills the phone screen. 1080 by 1920. Clean typography. A pull quote slides in. The AI voice begins — measured, alive. Footage cross-dissolves under text. Captions appear, perfectly timed.]

**NARRATOR**
And it gave me back... this.

[SCREEN: Video plays through. Smooth. Professional. Fifteen seconds of someone else’s content, sure — but it’s *my* story, my voice, my byline moving through the world instead of sitting still on a server.

The video ends. Below it: a shareable link. A download button. A "Make Another" prompt.]

**NARRATOR**
Twelve hundred words. Sixty seconds of film. Zero hours in an editing suite.

[SCREEN: Phone drops into a pocket. The laptop screen dims. Somewhere, a notification pings — someone just watched the clip.]

**NARRATOR**
That’s not a workflow. That’s a resurrection.

[SCREEN: Logo. Reel.shipyard.company.]

**NARRATOR**
ClipCraft. Dead text into living film.

[SCREEN: Fade to black.]

---
*End of script. Running time: approximately 2 minutes.*
