# Steve Jobs — Design & Brand Position
## Scoreboard Update PRD

---

## Product Name: **Scoreboard**

One word. Perfect. Don't touch it. It evokes competition, achievement, transparency. Everyone knows what a scoreboard is. The moment you see it, you know what it does.

---

## Design Philosophy: **Radical Transparency**

This isn't a spreadsheet. This is a **monument to completed work**. Every row is a battle won. Every stat is proof of execution. The scoreboard should make you PROUD when you ship and ACCOUNTABLE when you fail.

Most teams hide their metrics in dashboards nobody reads. We're putting ours in the repo root — in markdown — where every developer sees it every single day. That's insanely great because it's **undeniable**.

---

## User Experience: First 30 Seconds

You open the repo. You see `SCOREBOARD.md` right there at the top. You click it.

**BAM.** 25 shipped projects. Success rate: 89%. Average pipeline duration: 47 minutes.

You scroll down. Every project is there — name, date, QA verdict, board score, deliverables. No fluff. No excuses. Just facts.

You think: "This team ships. I want to be part of this."

That feeling — **confidence in competence** — that's what the first 30 seconds delivers.

---

## Brand Voice: **Unflinching Honesty**

The scoreboard doesn't celebrate. It doesn't apologize. It **reports**.

- "PASS on first try" — not "Successfully validated with zero blockers"
- "BLOCK (3 cycles)" — not "Required iterative refinement"
- "REJECT" — not "Deferred pending strategic alignment"

We speak like engineers, not marketers. Every word earns its place.

---

## What to Say NO To

**NO** to charts and graphs. This is markdown, not a BI tool. Text is faster, clearer, and version-controlled.

**NO** to incomplete data. If you don't know the pipeline duration, you write "—" not "~45 min". Precision or silence.

**NO** to filtering. Show ALL projects — successes AND failures. Hiding failures is what mediocre teams do.

**NO** to automation (for now). This update is manual because someone needs to **care** about the data. Later, when the format is perfect, we automate.

---

## The Emotional Hook: **Pride in the Pile**

Developers don't get trophies. They get merged PRs and shipped features. The scoreboard is the closest thing to a trophy case we have.

When you add your project to that table, you're not filling out a form. You're **carving your name into the monument**. You're saying: "I built this. It passed QA. The board approved it. It's real."

That's why people will love this. Because it makes the invisible work **visible**. Because it turns shipping code into **shipping legacy**.

---

## Bottom Line

The scoreboard isn't a feature. It's a **mirror**. It shows you who you are as a team. Make it accurate. Make it complete. Make it undeniable.

And if you can't look at it and feel proud, ship better work.
