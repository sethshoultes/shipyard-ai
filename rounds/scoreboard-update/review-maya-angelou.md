# Maya Angelou Review — Scoreboard Copy

## Does the language feel human, not corporate?

**Mixed.**

"Machine truth, never guessed" — yes. Human voice. Sharp.

"Last Updated: 2026-04-15 22:32 UTC" — robot timestamp.

"Success Rate: 94%" — corporate dashboard speak.

Most of it reads like what a computer would write *about* computers.

## Is there rhythm in the sentences?

**No.**

Rhythm needs variation. This is all staccato data or all flatline labels.

Headline bounces ("Machine truth, never guessed") but body marches in identical patterns:
- Shipped / QA / Board / Duration
- Shipped / QA / Board / Duration
- Shipped / QA / Board / Duration

No breath. No pulse.

## Does the headline stop you?

**Which headline?**

"# Shipyard AI — Scoreboard" — functional, forgettable.

"Machine truth, never guessed" — *yes.* This stops. Swagger without showing off.

## Is anything trying too hard?

**No.**

If anything, it's not trying enough.

The footer earns its confidence. Rest is dressed in uniform.

## Three weakest lines rewritten:

### 1. Original:
**"Last Updated: 2026-04-15 22:32 UTC"**

Rewrite:
**"Fresh as of today, 10:32pm."**

*Why:* Time should feel lived-in, not logged.

---

### 2. Original:
**"## Core Metrics"**

Rewrite:
**"## What We've Built"**

*Why:* Metrics measure. Builds mean something.

---

### 3. Original:
**"**Total Shipped:** 32
**Total Failed:** 2
**Success Rate:** 94%
**Average Pipeline Duration:** 30.5 hours"**

Rewrite:
**"32 shipped. 2 didn't make it. 94% clear the gate. Most take a day and change."**

*Why:* Numbers can breathe. Let them.
