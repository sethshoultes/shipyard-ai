# Steve's Verdict: Ship It, But Don't Screw It Up

**Naming.** AgentPress. One word. Two syllables. It sounds like something that *does* something, not something you *configure*. We keep it.

If anyone says "Agent Press" as two words, correct them immediately. Names are assets; don't dilute them. I don't want to see "AgentPress Pro," "AgentPress Suite," or "AgentPress AI." The product is AgentPress. Period.

The name whispers what it does: an agent that presses words and images into existence inside the world's most popular publishing engine. That's poetry and function in one breath.

**Design Philosophy.** This isn't a plugin. It's a cortex. WordPress is the body; AgentPress is the brain that tells the fingers what to type.

What makes it insanely great? You don't see the wires. You speak in human language, and the machine organizes itself. That's not automation—that's intention made real. Like a conductor waving a baton and hearing the orchestra respond before the motion finishes.

The routing layer is the magic. If users have to think about which agent handles what, we've built a control panel, not a product. The best technology is invisible. AgentPress should feel less like software and more like a colleague who reads your mind.

**The First 30 Seconds.** You type: "Write a blog intro about hiking boots." You hit return. Two seconds later, you have prose worth publishing.

No dropdowns. No "select your tone" modals. No routing diagrams. If the first 30 seconds feels like filing a ticket at the DMV, we've already lost. It should feel like snapping your fingers and the lights turn on.

The REST endpoint is the only interface that matters in the MVP. Everything else is decoration. The admin dashboard exists to reassure the user that something smart happened behind the curtain, not to demand their attention.

**Brand Voice.** We don't "leverage AI to empower content creators." We don't "delight users with seamless workflows." We don't "unlock potential." That language is a virus.

We speak like someone who knows exactly what they're doing and doesn't need to explain it twice. Confident. Sparse. Every word earns its place. Think: the voice of a master craftsman who hands you a perfect tool and says nothing, because the tool speaks for itself.

If a sentence could appear on a Salesforce landing page, delete it. The README should be three paragraphs, not a novel. Let the demo do the talking.

**What to Say NO To.** NO to onboarding wizards. If you need a wizard, the product is broken.

NO to a front-end chat interface in the MVP. WordPress already has an interface; it's called the REST API.

NO to SaaS tiers, billing dashboards, or "Pro" badges in v1. NO to custom CSS frameworks in wp-admin—use what WordPress gave us. NO to more than three built-in agents at launch. NO to real-time streaming in the MVP. NO to explaining how Claude works in the user docs; they don't care.

Focus is saying no to a hundred good ideas so one great idea can breathe. Simplicity isn't the absence of clutter; it's the presence of clarity. I am as proud of what we don't ship as what we do.

**Emotional Hook.** People will love this because for the first time, their website won't feel like a chore to maintain. It'll feel like having a world-class agency working inside their server at 3 AM, never sleeping, never invoicing, never complaining.

They'll love it because it turns a CMS into a collaborator. That's not a feature—that's a relationship.

They'll brag about it at conferences. They'll install it on every site they run. Because AgentPress doesn't just execute tasks; it makes them feel like they have superpowers. And once someone feels powerful, they never go back to the old way.
