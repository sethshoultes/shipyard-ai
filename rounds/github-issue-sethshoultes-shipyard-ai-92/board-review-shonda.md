# Board Review — Shonda Rhimes
## github-issue-sethshoultes-shipyard-ai-92 (Reel / ClipCraft)

---

### Verdict: 3/10
A functional utility with no soul. Nobody binge-writes blog posts.

---

### Story Arc
- **No protagonist.** No auth, no profile, no "your videos" gallery. User is anonymous.
- **No rising action.** Paste → invisible extraction → wait. The LLM-chosen key points are hidden from the user until render completes. No "here's what we found" beat.
- **Aha moment is deferred but unsupported.** "A few minutes" with nothing to look at. No frame previews, no audio waveform, no text storyboard. Just a spinner.
- **Ending is flat.** Download link. Curtain. No credits, no encore, no "make another."

### Retention Hooks
- **None.** Single-session tool. No email when render finishes. No library. No history.
- User downloads MP4, leaves, never remembers the URL.
- No reason to return tomorrow. No streak. No "you've made 7 videos this month."

### Content Flywheel
- **Dead end.** Output leaves the platform entirely. No watermark. No branded outro (OutroCard exists but isn't leveraged for discovery).
- No template marketplace. No community remixes. No "trending hooks" feed.
- The WordPress plugin was scoped out — that was the distribution engine.

### Emotional Cliffhangers
- "Rendering your video..." is not suspense. It's a loading screen.
- Missing beats:
  - No voice preview before committing to render.
  - No "your video will be 42 seconds" reveal.
  - No social proof ("847 videos rendered today").
  - No countdown or progress stages (audio → frames → encode → upload).

### What Would Make Me Care
- Show the extracted key points immediately. Let user edit/reorder. Give them agency = emotional investment.
- Generate a 3-second thumbnail preview instantly. Something to look at while waiting.
- Add a "Your Reels" page. Even localStorage. Something to come back to.
- Email the link. Capture the user.
- End with "Turn another post into a Reel?" + one-click suggested URLs from their history.

### Bottom Line
This builds the machine. It forgets the human watching it.
