# AdminPulse Demo Script
**Runtime: 2 minutes**

---

NARRATOR: Here's a thing that happens.

[SCREEN: WordPress dashboard, busy with widgets. A site admin is logged in. Clock shows 7:42 AM.]

NARRATOR: It's a Tuesday morning. You've got fourteen client sites. You're drinking coffee that's already gone cold. And somewhere—on one of those sites—something is broken.

[SCREEN: Cursor hovers over the sidebar. Clicks "Tools." Then "Site Health." Page loads slowly.]

NARRATOR: WordPress has a Site Health page. It's been there since 5.1. It tells you everything: your PHP version, your SSL status, whether your plugins are up to date. The problem is—

[SCREEN: Site Health page finally loads. Red badge says "2 critical issues."]

NARRATOR: —you have to go *looking* for it.

[SCREEN: Quick montage: Admin clicks back to dashboard. Clicks to another site. Clicks Tools > Site Health again. Another site. Same ritual. Again.]

NARRATOR: Fourteen sites. Fourteen clicks to Tools. Fourteen clicks to Site Health. That's twenty-eight clicks just to find out if anything's on fire. And here's the thing about fires—

[SCREEN: Freeze on one Site Health page showing "Your site could not complete a loopback request."]

NARRATOR: —they don't wait for you to check.

[SCREEN: Hard cut to black. Beat. Then: AdminPulse logo fades in, simple and clean.]

NARRATOR: This is AdminPulse.

[SCREEN: Fresh WordPress dashboard loads. In the widget area, there's a new widget: "Site Health." It shows a green checkmark and the words "Everything looks good!"]

NARRATOR: One widget. Right on your dashboard. The moment you log in, you know.

[SCREEN: Cursor doesn't click anything. Just sits there. The widget is already loaded, already showing status.]

NARRATOR: No clicking through menus. No hunting. No hoping you remembered to check. You log in—and it's *there*.

[SCREEN: Switch to a different site. Dashboard loads. This time the widget shows: "2 issues need attention." Below: a red "Critical" badge next to "Plugin update required." A yellow "Recommended" badge next to "Persistent object cache not detected."]

NARRATOR: When something's wrong, you see it in red. When something *could* be better, you see it in yellow. Critical first, recommendations second. The way your brain already works.

[SCREEN: Cursor hovers over the critical issue. A link appears: "Update plugins."]

NARRATOR: And every issue has a link. Not a suggestion—a *link*. Click it, you're there. Fix it, it's gone.

[SCREEN: Click "Update plugins." Plugins page opens. One-click update. Return to dashboard. Widget now shows "1 issue needs attention." The critical item is gone.]

NARRATOR: That's it. That's the whole thing. Twenty-eight clicks become *zero*. You log in, you see your health, you fix what needs fixing, you move on with your morning.

[SCREEN: Dashboard with the healthy green checkmark widget. Cursor clicks "Refresh" button at bottom of widget.]

NARRATOR: And if you're the type who likes to double-check—

[SCREEN: Widget shows brief loading animation—three subtle pulsing lines. Then: "Everything looks good!" with the green checkmark.]

NARRATOR: —there's a refresh button. Results cached for an hour so your server doesn't break a sweat. Fresh data when you want it.

[SCREEN: Pull back to show full dashboard. The Site Health widget sits among the others—At a Glance, Activity, Quick Draft. It belongs.]

NARRATOR: No settings to configure. No documentation to read. Install it, activate it, forget it exists—until the moment you need it.

[SCREEN: Final shot. Early morning light. Coffee cup. Dashboard open. Green checkmark visible. The admin closes the laptop.]

NARRATOR: AdminPulse. Because the best way to find out your site is broken...

[SCREEN: Black screen. White text fades in:]

NARRATOR: ...shouldn't be from a customer.

[SCREEN: "AdminPulse" with tagline: "Site Health. At a glance."]

---

*End of demo.*
