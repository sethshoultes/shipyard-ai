# Steve Jobs — Round 2: The Experience IS the Metric

## Where Elon Optimizes for the Wrong Metric

Elon is measuring lines of code and session count. I am measuring **activation rate per human heartbeat.** He says "one editable screen" because it's fewer files. But users don't fall in love with file counts—they fall in love with being *seen.* He cuts the widget preview panel because it's "duplicated code that will drift." I say: if the user cannot see what their customer sees, they do not trust you. Trust is the metric. Build the preview. Make it not drift.

He says "users don't want a narrative arc." Every Apple product ever shipped says otherwise. The narrative arc is not scope creep—it is the *removal of anxiety.* A single collapsible screen with fifteen fields is not simplicity; it is a door labeled PUSH that requires pulling. That screen optimizes for engineer convenience, not user comprehension. The metric should be time-to-delight, not time-to-render.

## Defending What Elon Would Attack

Elon will say the wizard is engineering theater. I say: the wizard is not three PHP files. It is one continuous gesture—pouring cream into coffee. If your implementation has step-state machines, you built it wrong, not because the concept is wrong. He will say "Sous" is precious. I say "LocalGenius" is a feature list, and feature lists are commodities. Warmth is the only defensible differentiation in a market of scrapers. He will say design polish slows the sprint. I say an ugly product that works is a truck. We are not building a truck. We are building something that makes a restaurant owner feel proud.

## Where Elon Is Right

The synchronous schema.org scrape on activation is indefensible. Move it async or kill it—never make a user wait on a network call they didn't request. He's right about the percentile query at 100,000 users; pre-compute it nightly. He's right to cut the 24-hour email and the tooltip theater—those are patches for a product that didn't land. Fix the landing, not the follow-up. And he's right that this must ship: a beautiful product in a drawer is a corpse. Ship, but ship with a soul.

## Top 3 Non-Negotiables

1. **The name is Sous.** A brand is a promise, and "LocalGenius" promises a schema.org scraper. Sous promises a partner.
2. **Zero blank-slate onboarding.** The first screen must be recognition—"We found Maria's Trattoria. Is this you?"—not a form. The widget preview lives here. No preview, no ship.
3. **Working is beautiful.** One continuous gesture. No save buttons. No airplane cockpits. No "please wait." Beauty is the absence of friction, and friction is measured in abandoned activations. If the machine can infer it, the machine must do it. The user reviews, not creates.
