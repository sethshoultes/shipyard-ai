# Steve Jobs — Round 1: Design & Brand Positions

## Product Naming

"LocalGenius" sounds like a geography quiz app from 2004. The brand voice in this PRD is literally named **Sous** — a warm, human, single-syllable word that means "helper." That's your name. **Sous.** One word. Memorable as breath. It whispers "I have your back" instead of shouting "I am smart." Intelligence is table stakes; warmth is the differentiator. If you ship "LocalGenius," you are selling schema.org scrapers. If you ship **Sous**, you are selling trust.

## Design Philosophy

This isn't a dashboard. It's a maître d' greeting you at the door. The PRD says "working, not beautiful" because the board got burned by empty directories. I say: **working IS beautiful.** Beauty is not ornament; beauty is the absence of friction. The onboarding wizard is not three web pages — it is one continuous gesture. Step one flows into step two like pouring cream into coffee. No save buttons. No "please wait" spinners. If the machine can infer it, the machine should do it. The user reviews, not creates. That is insanely great.

## The First 30 Seconds

The user activates the plugin and sees... nothing? No. They see a single sentence: *"We found Maria's Trattoria. Does this look like you?"* One button. "That's me." In thirty seconds, the software has done the homework. The emotional register is not "configuration" — it is **recognition.** Like walking into your neighborhood spot and the owner already knows your table.

## Brand Voice

Sous speaks like a person who actually likes people. Never "leverage AI." Never "optimize workflow." Those phrases are written by committees who have never run a restaurant. Sous says: "Your response time is faster than two out of three Italian spots in Denver. Room to climb." It's warm. It's specific. It's proud without being arrogant. Every sentence should pass the mom test: would you say this to your mother over dinner? If not, burn it.

## What We Say NO To

We say NO to the blank-slate onboarding. A blank slate is not freedom — it is abandonment. We say NO to admin dashboards that look like airplane cockpits. We say NO to giving users fifteen options when they need one. We say NO to "launching" without a widget preview. If the user cannot see what their customer sees, we have not shipped a product. We have shipped a promise. And promises are not software.

## The Emotional Hook

People will love Sous because it makes them feel like the expert they already are. The restaurant owner didn't fail because she lacked intelligence; she failed because technology asked her to become a database administrator. Sous says: *You run the restaurant. I'll run the answers.* That feeling — of being understood without having to explain yourself — that is the thing people will tell their friends about. That is the dent in the universe.
