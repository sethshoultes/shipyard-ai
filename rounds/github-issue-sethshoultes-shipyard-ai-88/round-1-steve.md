# Steve Jobs — Position Memo

## Product Naming

"Whisper Blocks" dies today. That name is an engineer's nickname, not a product. **Scribe.** One word. Human for ten thousand years. It says: *we turn your voice into craft.* Anything longer than one syllable is a committee compromise.

## Design Philosophy

A transcript is not a data dump. It is a **reading experience.** Look at a vinyl record sleeve — the way the lyrics breathe on the page, the way whitespace sings. That's our bar. Speaker labels should feel like chapter headings. Timestamps should disappear until you need them, then arrive like a gentle whisper. Typography is the interface. If it doesn't feel like paging through a beautifully bound book, we failed.

## First 30 Seconds

Drag. Drop. **Breathe.** The transcript unfurls like a scroll — word by word, alive. Click anywhere, hear the voice. No settings. No "configure API key." No spinner of death. The first thirty seconds must deliver the same jolt as the first pinch-to-zoom: *holy shit, it just knew.* If we don't nail that, nothing else matters.

## Brand Voice

We don't "transcribe." We **capture voice.** We don't "export SRT files." We **set words free.** Warm. Confident. Reverent about language. We speak like someone who believes words deserve to last forever.

## What We Say NO To

NO settings panel before magic. NO monospace default fonts. NO WordPress metabox clutter. NO "processing..." anxiety spinners. NO twelve export options that 98% of users ignore. Simplicity isn't "easy mode" — it's the *courage* to remove what doesn't electrify. Every feature that survives must earn its place by sparking joy. Everything else is debris.

## Emotional Hook

People pour their souls into audio — podcasts, lectures, interviews — and then the words **vanish into the air.** We are building a time machine for voice. Every transcript is a resurrection. When a podcaster sees their spoken words rendered with the care of a published novel, they don't think "useful tool." They think: *my work matters.* That's the love affair. Make them feel that, and they'll never leave.
