# Board Review — Shonda Rhimes
## Poster Child (Issue #84)

---

## Verdict: 2/10

Skeleton without a heartbeat. Spec is thorough. Code is missing. Even if built, product is a one-night stand — no reason to come back.

---

## Story Arc

- **No arc.** Paste URL → get PNG. End of story.
- No "who are you" moment. No stakes. No transformation.
- The "aha" (beautiful poster) is a still image, not a relationship.
- Compare: Canva hooks you with templates, progress, a gallery. This has none.

## Retention Hooks

- **Zero.** You generate once per repo. Never return.
- No account. No history. No "your posters" page.
- No trending feed to browse. No weekly digest of hot repos.
- No notification when your poster gets shared.
- The spec explicitly bans: database, auth, analytics, Pro tier. Cut every hook.

## Content Flywheel

- **Dead on arrival.**
- Output *could* be viral billboard, but spec cut the viral mechanics:
  - No "Made with Poster" watermark.
  - No branded footer linking back.
  - No social sharing buttons.
  - No embeddable widget for READMEs.
- GitHub Actions integration — cut. Distribution dies.
- No gallery of community posters. No discovery loop.

## Emotional Cliffhangers

- **None.**
- AI tagline generation — cut. That was the magic, the surprise, the "how did it know?"
- No "unlock Pro fonts" tease. No "see what others made." No "your repo is trending — refresh your poster?"
- One template, no customization. No curiosity about what else is possible.

## What Would Fix It

- **Add a heartbeat:** user gallery, history, "your most-viewed poster" stats.
- **Add friction that rewards return:** weekly auto-refresh when stars cross thresholds.
- **Add the AI tagline back.** That was the hook in the original dream.
- **Watermark the output.** Every share is a funnel.
- **GitHub Actions integration.** Distribution *is* retention for dev tools.

---

## Bottom Line

A beautiful poster for a funeral. No recurring characters. No season two.

**Score: 2/10** — spec reads like a novel, deliverable is a blank page.
