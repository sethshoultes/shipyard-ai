# Steve's Position: Issue #84

## Naming

**Poster Child is dead.**

"Child" is diminutive. It apologizes. It sounds like a charity case. We don't make children — we make icons. The name is **Poster.** One word. Like Mac. Like iPod. Like Nest. You paste a URL, you get a Poster. End of story. If you need two words to explain what you do, you have already lost.

## Design Philosophy

This is not a "template tool."

Templates are the enemy of taste. They give users enough rope to hang themselves, and users *will* hang themselves. We design the one perfect layout — the inevitable layout. The one that feels like it could not have been any other way. The typography breathes. The color palette extracts from the repo itself, like the project is revealing its own DNA. Three seconds of computation, one week of craft. White space is not empty space. It is courage.

## The First 30 Seconds

Paste. Hold your breath. Gasp.

Your scrappy weekend hack — the one with the spaghetti code and the TODO comments — suddenly looks like it belongs on the wall of a modern art museum. That gasp. That skipped heartbeat. That is the product. No settings. No onboarding carousel. No "choose your style" wizard. Paste, magic, share. If we don't deliver a visceral reaction in thirty seconds, we have failed.

## Brand Voice

We speak with certainty, not arrogance.

Short sentences. No jargon. No "synergistic workflows" or "leveraging AI." We say: *"Your repo deserves a face."* We say: *"Make it unforgettable."* We don't ask what you want — we show you what you need. We are the taste you didn't know you had. Every word we publish should feel like it was typed by someone who cares more than is reasonable.

## What We Say NO To

NO templates. NO customization panels. NO "download as JPG" — PNG only, because JPG is for amateurs. NO analytics dashboards. NO social scheduling. NO "Pro tier with extra fonts." NO sliders. NO color pickers. NO "brand kits."

If it isn't the magic moment, it is out. Simplicity is not minimalism — it is surgical removal of everything that distracts from the miracle. We are building a catapult, not a Swiss Army knife. Every feature request that isn't the core magic gets the same answer: "That would make it better at being worse."

## The Emotional Hook

Every developer knows the pain.

You poured six months of your life into something beautiful. You hit publish. And then you watched it die in obscurity because the packaging looked like a high school homework assignment. Poster is the suit your project puts on before it walks into the room. It is the difference between a demo tape and a Rolling Stone cover. Between a garage band and a headline act.

People won't just share your repo. They will *respect* it.

And respect is the most addictive drug on earth.
