# Maya Angelou Copy Review — LocalGenius Pulse

## Verdict: Almost there. Gets human wrong in spots.

---

## What Works

**Rhythm exists:**
- "Next week, I'm trying..." flows
- "I noticed X. I'm going to test Y." has pattern
- Short sentences land

**First-person voice:**
- AI speaking directly feels warm
- "I'm studying what worked" — curious, not robotic

**No corporate jargon:**
- Clean of synergy/leverage garbage
- Avoids "utilize" disease

---

## What Fails

**Line 34:** "I'm trying a different approach to your social media posts."
- "Approach" is consultant speak
- Too formal for sous chef

**Line 62:** "No promises, but the early data looks interesting."
- "Early data looks interesting" — that's analyst language
- Trying too hard to sound casual

**Line 126:** "I'm analyzing your data for next week."
- "Analyzing your data" — cold, technical
- Reads like IT department

---

## The 3 Weakest Lines (Rewritten)

### BEFORE (line 34):
"Next week, I'm trying a different approach to your social media posts."

### AFTER:
"Next week, I'm switching up how I write your posts. Let's see what happens."

---

### BEFORE (line 62):
"No promises, but the early data looks interesting."

### AFTER:
"Can't promise anything, but I'm seeing something."

---

### BEFORE (line 126):
"I'm analyzing your data for next week."

### AFTER:
"I'm looking at your numbers. More soon."

---

## Pattern to Fix

Stop saying what you're doing. Just do it.

**Don't:** "I'm analyzing patterns in your bookings"
**Do:** "Your bookings are doing something weird on Tuesdays"

**Don't:** "I'm testing a theory"
**Do:** "I think I found something"

---

## Bottom Line

Copy has good bones. Rhythm's there. Voice is close.

But it hedges. "Trying," "testing," "analyzing" — too much process talk.

Sous chef doesn't announce they're analyzing the sauce. They taste it and tell you what's wrong.

Be direct. Trust the insight. Stop performing curiosity.
