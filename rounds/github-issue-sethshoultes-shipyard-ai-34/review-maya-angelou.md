# Maya's Review

## Verdict: Strong bones, thin blood

You built trust with numbers. Weakened it with jargon.

---

## What Works

- **100x faster** — I feel that
- **Ship gate: "Does it run on Peak Dental without errors? If yes, ship it."** — Human decision, human words
- Wave structure shows progress without explaining itself
- Tables breathe. Data speaks.
- You deleted code. That takes courage.

---

## What Tries Too Hard

- "Pre-flight validation" — Airplane metaphor nobody asked for
- "Requirements traceability" — Checkbox language. Corporate immune response.
- "Denormalize to KV array" — Showing off for other engineers, not for humans
- Wave emoji spam — ✅ loses power when overused
- "Supervised by: Phil Jackson (Zen Master)" — Winking at your own cleverness

---

## What Stops Me

Nothing stops me.

Headline delivers status, not story.

Compare:
- **Current:** "SEODash Plugin Fix - Phase 1 Waves 0-3 Complete"
- **Better:** "We Made SEODash 100x Faster By Deleting 100 Lines"

---

## Where Rhythm Dies

Bullets become lists.
Lists become inventories.
Inventories become autopilot reading.

Too much of this:
```
- ✅ REQ-001 to REQ-005 (Pre-flight validation)
- ✅ REQ-006 to REQ-008, REQ-072 (Remove keywords)
```

Not enough of this:
```
**Total Code Removed:** ~100 lines (simpler = better)
```

---

## The 3 Weakest Lines (Rewritten)

### 1. BEFORE:
> "Successfully executed **8 tasks across 4 waves** (Wave 0 pre-flight + Waves 1-3):"

**AFTER:**
> "8 tasks. 4 waves. Here's what changed:"

**Why:** Nobody celebrates successful execution. They celebrate results.

---

### 2. BEFORE:
> "**Analysis:** All test failures are **pre-existing mock KV implementation issues**, NOT regressions from this work."

**AFTER:**
> "19 tests fail. None because of us — they've always been broken."

**Why:** Own it plainly. Drop the lawyer voice.

---

### 3. BEFORE:
> "Validated Emdash plugin API via comprehensive documentation review"

**AFTER:**
> "Read every Emdash doc. Confirmed the patterns work."

**Why:** "Validated via comprehensive review" is what committees say when they want credit for reading.

---

## Final Word

You have good instincts.

When you wrote "simpler = better" in parentheses, that was you.

When you wrote "requirements traceability," that was someone else.

Trust the first voice more.
