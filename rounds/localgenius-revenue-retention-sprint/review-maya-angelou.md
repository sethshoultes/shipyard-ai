# Verdict

Mostly human. Two lines have no heartbeat. One headline stops traffic. Footer reads like a lease.

# Human, Not Corporate?

- "Breathe annually" — human. Surprising. Good.
- "Pay once. Focus on your guests." — warm hand on the shoulder.
- "Your reviews are handled. Your posts went live." — corporate status update. No pulse.
- "Hands-off marketing" — agency jargon. Nobody feels that.
- "Stripe Customer Portal for changes" — you made me read a vendor manual.

# Rhythm?

- "Breathe annually" — three syllables, exhale at the end. Music.
- "Pay once. Focus on your guests." — two beats, clean.
- "Go month by month" — tries to swing, lands thin.
- "For one location / For 2–5 locations" — functional. No rhythm.

# Headline Stops You?

- "Breathe annually" — yes. Strange. Beautiful. You pause.
- "Go month by month" — no. It nods and keeps walking.
- "Welcome to Sous." — polite. Doesn't stop a soul.

# Trying Too Hard?

- "Your AI sous chef" — not trying, just accurate.
- "best value" badge — trying to sell. Sous serves. Doesn't sell.
- "Save $70 — 2 months free" — fine. Math is honest.

# 3 Weakest Lines

**1. Tagline**
> Your reviews are handled. Your posts went live.

- Rewrite: **Your guests feel heard. Your story stays lit.**

**2. Confirmation Body**
> You're all set for 12 months of hands-off marketing.

- Rewrite: **One year. You cook. We carry the words.**

**3. Footer Legal**
> LocalGenius · Cancel anytime, prorated refund · Stripe Customer Portal for changes

- Rewrite: **Cancel anytime. Prorated refunds. Change your plan in two clicks.**
