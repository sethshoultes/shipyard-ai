# Shonda Rhimes Retention Roadmap — LocalGenius v1.1
## What Keeps Users Coming Back

> “Annual billing is a lock-in, not a hook. Don’t confuse commitment with desire.”

This roadmap turns LocalGenius from a utility into a habit.

---

## 1. The Story Arc (Onboarding as Pilot Episode)

**Problem:** Signup → pricing fork → confirmation email → silence till Sunday. No chapter two.

**v1.1 Fixes:**
- **Cold open.** First screen after signup shows one real review already drafted (using public sample data), so the user sees the product’s value in 10 seconds.
- **First-win moment.** Within the first session, the user publishes a response. Celebrate with a micro-animation: “Your first guest feels heard.”
- **Rising action.** Day 3 email: “You have 2 new reviews waiting.” Day 7: “Your first week in numbers.”
- **Season finale.** Annual users get a Month 6 midpoint celebration: “Halfway. You’ve saved 40 hours. Here’s what guests love most about you.”

---

## 2. Retention Hooks & Rituals

| Hook | Mechanics | Why It Works |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| **The Sunday Serial** | Weekly digest sent Sunday 6 PM. Not a receipt—a magazine. | Respects the restaurant rhythm. Anticipation beats interruption. |
| **Streaks** | “You’ve responded to every review for 3 weeks.” | Loss aversion + pride. |
| **Milestones** | 30-day, 60-day, 100-day badges. | Creates social-proof assets owners can share on Instagram. |
| **Time-Saved Ticker** | Dashboard hero: “You’ve saved 12 hours this quarter.” | Dopamine hit tied to relief, not hype. |
| **MoM Narrative** | Digest leads with month-over-month story, not week-over-week deltas. | Boards and owners think in months, not weeks. |

**What we’re removing:** Static SQL deltas emailed weekly. Replace with generative, event-driven narratives.

---

## 3. The Content Flywheel

**Problem:** Emails go out. Nothing comes back. Dead end.

**v1.1 Fixes:**
- **Feature Your Restaurant.** One digest slot per week highlights a user’s review-response story. Owners compete to be featured.
- **User Posts → Digest Features User → User Feels Seen → User Posts More.** Start with a simple “Reply to this email with your busiest night this week” loop.
- **Community Tab (lightweight).** Leaderboard of most-improved response times in the city. No forums, no bloat—just leaderboards and shouts.
- **Blog / Editorial.** One story per month: “How Maria’s Diner turned a 1-star into a regular.” Establishes brand voice and SEO.

---

## 4. Emotional Cliffhangers

**Problem:** Subject line “Sous — Reviews handled this week.” Yawn. Confirmation email says “You’re all set for 12 months.” Translation: go away for a year.

**v1.1 Fixes:**
- **Subject lines as episode titles.**
  - “Last week: your nicest guest. This week: a mystery diner.”
  - “Will next week be your best review week yet?”
- **Preview teasers.** End every digest with: “Next Sunday: we’re ranking your top 3 guest compliments. Don’t miss it.”
- **Confirmation email rewrite.** Instead of “You’re all set for 12 months,” say: “Your next review response is already drafted. We’ll send it Monday.” Give them a reason to log in *tomorrow*.
- **Curiosity gaps.** One unanswered question per digest that forces the owner to open: “One guest used a word no one has used before. We’ll tell you next week.”

---

## 5. Why They Log In Tomorrow

**Problem:** There is none. The product is a Sunday email.

**v1.1 Fixes:**
- **Draft queue.** Dashboard shows 3 pre-written responses waiting for approval. Every login is an edit-and-publish, not a blank-page panic.
- **Event-driven stories.** LLM writes a unique weekly narrative based on actual events: new menu mention, holiday traffic, staffing change inferred from review sentiment.
- **Mid-week micro-touch.** Wednesday SMS (opt-in): “Your response to the ‘cold fries’ review just got a ❤️ from the guest.”

---

## 6. Accessibility & Inclusion (Oprah’s Table Stakes)

- Large touch targets for kitchen hands.
- Non-English digest support (Spanish, Mandarin, Vietnamese depending on market).
- Screen-reader-first markup for all digest and dashboard elements.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected in all celebrations and animations.

## One-Line North Star

> Turn the weekly digest into a *serial*—last week’s win, this week’s preview, one question that demands an answer. Make them need to open it.
