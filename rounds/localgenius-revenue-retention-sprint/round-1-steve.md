# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
## LocalGenius Revenue & Retention Sprint

### Product Naming
LocalGenius is a spreadsheet column, not a brand. It tells me *what* you do and none of *why* I should care. We need one word that lives in the heart, not the head. I want **"Sous"** — your AI sous chef. One syllable. Kitchen-native. Intimate. It says: I prep the marketing so you can cook. If legal chokes, call it "Sous" in every headline, every subject line, every button. Names are sounds that become feelings. LocalGenius is a cold sore. Sous is a warm hand on your shoulder.

### Design Philosophy
Insanely great means invisible. The best design doesn't announce itself — it gets out of the way like a great waiter. When a restaurant owner opens this, they shouldn't see a dashboard. They should see *proof that someone else did the work*. One number. One smile. One reason to exhale. Everything else is clutter.

### The First 30 Seconds
Thirty seconds is an eternity. In the first three, a restaurant owner must feel a weight lift off their chest. The pricing page isn't a grid of options — it's a clear fork in the road: keep drowning monthly, or breathe annually. The digest isn't a report card; it's a love note from their future self saying "look how far you've come." If they feel admin work, we failed. If they feel *relief*, we won.

### Brand Voice
We speak like the best maître d' at their favorite restaurant: confident, warm, never apologetic. No "we're excited to announce." No "leverage our AI platform." We say: "Your reviews are handled. Your posts went live. You saved four hours this week." Period. We are the sous chef who talks only when it matters, and when we do, we say exactly what they need to hear.

### What We Say NO To
NO to "save $70" as the headline — that's coupon logic, not value logic. NO to feature lists. NO to "Pro" and "Base" — those are server racks, not human needs. NO to explaining proration with three paragraphs of gray text. NO to any screen that requires a tutorial. NO to A/B tests that ask "which button is less terrible" instead of building the one button that is obviously right.

### The Emotional Hook
People don't love tools. They love *partners*. A restaurant owner wakes up at 5 AM, stands on concrete for twelve hours, and collapses into bed wondering if anyone noticed they exist. Sous notices. Sous remembers. Sous says "last month you touched 300 customers you would have lost." That isn't data. That's dignity. That's sleep. That's why they will never cancel.
