# Board Review: LocalGenius Lite (SPARK)
**Reviewer:** Shonda Rhimes
**Date:** 2026-04-19
**Focus:** Narrative Arc, Retention, Content Strategy

---

## Score: 4/10
**One-line:** No story beyond installation — utility without emotional investment.

---

## Story Arc Analysis

### Act 1: Setup
- Landing page shows one value prop: "paste script, get AI chat"
- No user journey. No persona-driven narrative
- Call-to-action = "copy code" (transaction, not transformation)

**Missing:** Why should I care? What problem am I solving emotionally?

### Act 2: Conflict
- User pastes script → widget appears
- Visitor asks question → gets answer
- **Dead end.** No progression. No stakes. No tension.

**Missing:** Where's the struggle? The learning curve? The surprise?

### Act 3: Resolution
- Welcome message: generic AI greeting
- Answer appears in <2 seconds
- Then what? Nothing. No payoff. No aha moment.

**Missing:** Transformation. User doesn't become anything. They just... used a widget.

---

### Missing Character Arc
User doesn't evolve from:
- **Novice → Expert**
- **Skeptic → Believer**
- **Solo → Community member**

They stay frozen: "person who pasted code once"

### Missing Plot Twists

- No "wow, it understood my SPA content" reveal
- No Easter eggs. No delighters
- Predictable = forgettable

### Missing Themes
What's this story about?
- Convenience? (shallow)
- Intelligence? (technical)
- Connection? (absent)
- Empowerment? (not felt)

Product says: "Works fast."
Should say: "Your visitors finally understand you."

---

## Specific Recommendations

### Immediate (Retention)
1. **Daily recap email:** "3 questions today. Here's what visitors wanted to know."
2. **Dashboard with story:** Show question timeline, most active pages, visitor confusion points
3. **Achievement system:** "First 10 questions answered!" → dopamine hit
4. **Weekly insight:** "Your visitors asked about pricing 12x. Consider updating that page."

### Short-term (Content Flywheel)
1. **Auto-FAQ generator:** "Export 10 most-asked questions" → content for site
2. **Public showcase:** Submit your SPARK-powered site → backlinks + social proof
3. **Template library:** "Top 50 SPARK sites by industry" → aspiration + discovery
4. **Analytics blog:** "What customers ask e-commerce sites" → SEO + thought leadership

### Long-term (Emotional Arc)
1. **Onboarding narrative:** Welcome sequence that reveals product gradually
2. **Founder story:** Why build this? Who are you? What's the mission?
3. **Customer stories:** Video testimonials showing transformation, not features
4. **Progressive features:** Start free → earn custom branding → unlock analytics
5. **Community element:** SPARK user Slack/Discord → connection + retention

---

## The Core Problem

**SPARK is a tool, not a story.**

Tools are rented. Stories are remembered.

Right now:
- No stakes (what happens if I don't use it?)
- No payoff (what do I gain beyond "answered questions"?)
- No arc (where am I going with this?)

User journey today:
```
Install → Works → Forgotten
```

User journey should be:
```
Curious → Delighted → Empowered → Evangelist
```

---

## What Great Looks Like

**Superhuman:** Every email teaches you shortcuts. You level up. You become power user.
**Notion:** Templates + community = aspiration. You want to show off your workspace.
**Stripe:** Developer-first storytelling. You're building the future. Atlas gives identity.

**SPARK needs:** Identity. Progress. Belonging.

Not just "works fast" but "you're part of something."

---

## Final Verdict

**Built well. Zero soul.**

Technically solid. Fast. Clean. Functional.

Emotionally empty. No reason to care tomorrow. No story to tell friends.

PRD optimized for shipping, not storytelling.

---

**Score: 4/10**

Ship it to learn. But know: retention will be brutal without narrative hooks.

People don't come back to utilities. They come back to relationships.

Right now, SPARK is a one-night stand. Make it a love story.
