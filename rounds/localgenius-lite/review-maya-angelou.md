# Maya Angelou Copy Review

## Verdict: Trying too hard to be clever. Lost the human.

---

## Does the language feel human, not corporate?

**Landing page:** No.
- "instantly brilliant" — awkward self-praise
- "Blazing Fast" — startup cliché
- "Built for Developers" — feature dump, not feeling

**README:** Mostly yes.
- Direct. Clean. Until the Phil Jackson quote — forced.

---

## Is there rhythm in the sentences?

**Landing:** Broken.
- "One script tag. Zero configuration." — staccato works here
- "That's it. Your site now has an AI assistant." — rhythm dies with "now has"
- "Shadow DOM prevents style conflicts." — technical, flat

**README:** Better flow.
- "Add this to your HTML:" → "That's it!" — natural breath

---

## Does the headline stop you?

**"Your website, instantly brilliant."**

No.
- "Instantly brilliant" tries to wow. Sounds like ad copy.
- Visitor thinks: "Says who?"
- Doesn't create feeling. Creates suspicion.

---

## Is anything trying too hard?

**Yes. Three places:**

1. "Your website, instantly brilliant." — oversell
2. "Blazing Fast" with rocket emoji — tech bro energy
3. Footer: "Powered by SPARK" — self-important

---

## 3 Weakest Lines — Rewritten

### 1. Headline (landing)
**Before:**
"Your website, instantly brilliant."

**After:**
"Your website answers questions now."

*Why:* Simple promise. No judgment. Let the visitor feel smart, not sold.

---

### 2. Subheading (landing)
**Before:**
"One script tag. Zero configuration."

**After:**
"One line. Done."

*Why:* Shorter. More confident. Less showboating.

---

### 3. CTA Final (landing)
**Before:**
"Ready to make your site brilliant?"

**After:**
"Ready?"

*Why:* The visitor already knows what happens. Respect their intelligence. Don't repeat yourself.
