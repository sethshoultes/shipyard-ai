# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
## MemberShip Plugin Review

---

### Product Naming

**The name is wrong.** "MemberShip" with a capital S in the middle? That's clever-clever nonsense. Nobody searches for that. Nobody remembers it.

Call it **Belong**.

One word. Emotional. It captures the *feeling* of what membership actually is — people don't buy access to content, they buy belonging to something. "Belong by Emdash" rolls off the tongue. It's a verb. It's an invitation. It's human.

---

### Design Philosophy

Here's what makes this insanely great — or *could* make it insanely great: **Membership is invisible until it matters.**

The best membership systems are like the best security at a luxury hotel. You don't see guards at every door. You just... belong here. Or you don't. The transition from outsider to insider should feel like a velvet rope lifting, not a toll booth demanding a ticket.

Right now, this is 3,984 lines of toll booth. We need 800 lines of velvet rope.

---

### User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

When a creator installs Belong, the first thing they see shouldn't be "Configure Stripe." That's like walking into Apple and someone handing you a soldering iron.

**Second zero:** "Who do you want to welcome inside?"

They type: *"My premium yoga students."*

We auto-generate a plan called "Premium Yoga Students." One click to set a price. One click to mark which pages are for members. Done. They're live.

The Stripe setup happens when they hit publish — not before. Configuration follows intention.

---

### Brand Voice

This product speaks like a **confident friend who runs a successful studio downtown**. Not a SaaS robot. Not a developer explaining edge cases.

- Instead of "Access Denied," say: "This one's for members — want to join us?"
- Instead of "Invalid credentials," say: "That didn't work. Let's try again."
- Instead of "Subscription renewed," say: "You're set for another month. Thanks for being here."

Every message should feel like it came from the creator, not from software.

---

### What to Say NO To

**Kill the groups feature.** It's scope creep disguised as flexibility. Most creators have ONE membership level. Maybe two. "Groups" is us solving a problem nobody asked about yet.

**Kill drip content — for now.** Ship belonging first. Ship payments first. Drip is a v2 feature that will distract us from the core experience being bulletproof.

**Kill the 114 thrown Response objects.** Not because they're broken — because they represent a philosophy of "catch everything that moves." Stop catching. Start guiding.

Simplicity isn't about having fewer features in the settings. It's about having fewer *concepts* in the creator's head.

---

### The Emotional Hook

People will **LOVE** this because it makes them feel like a *real business*.

That yoga teacher in Portland? When her first student pays $29/month and gets access to her premium content — she's not "using a plugin." She's running a membership business. That moment of "I did this. People are paying me. I belong to them and they belong to me."

That's the magic. Not Stripe integration. Not content gating. The *moment of becoming*.

We're not selling software. We're selling the feeling of building something people want to join.

---

*"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication."*
