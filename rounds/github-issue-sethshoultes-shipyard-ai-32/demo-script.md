# ReviewPulse Demo Script

**Runtime:** 2 minutes
**Tone:** Human, urgent, conversational

---

**[SCREEN: A coffee shop owner, MARIA, hunched over her laptop at 6 AM. Three browser tabs open: Google Business, Yelp, Facebook. Steam rises from her cup.]**

NARRATOR:
This is Maria. She owns a coffee shop in Portland. Great coffee. Great pastries. And at six in the morning, before she's even turned on the espresso machine, she's doing *this.*

**[SCREEN: Quick cuts between tabs. Google: 4.2 stars. Yelp: 3.8 stars. Facebook: 4.5 stars. Different review counts. Different interfaces.]**

NARRATOR:
Checking three platforms. Trying to remember — did she reply to that two-star review from Tuesday? Was that on Google or Yelp? And that glowing five-star she wanted to feature on her website... where did that go?

**[SCREEN: Maria scrolls endlessly. The Yelp interface loads slowly. She sighs.]**

NARRATOR:
This isn't running a business. This is playing whack-a-mole with your reputation. And the worst part? A bad review can sit there for *days* before she even sees it.

**[SCREEN: Hard cut to black. Then: ReviewPulse logo fades in. Clean. Simple.]**

NARRATOR:
What if it didn't have to be this way?

**[SCREEN: ReviewPulse dashboard loading. Single view. All reviews in one feed — Google reviews have a small "G" badge, Yelp has "Y." They're sorted by date, newest first.]**

NARRATOR:
This is ReviewPulse. One dashboard. Every review. Google, Yelp — doesn't matter where it came from. It's here.

**[SCREEN: Mouse hovers over a 2-star review. It's highlighted with a subtle red "flagged" indicator.]**

NARRATOR:
See that? ReviewPulse automatically flags anything two stars or below. Maria doesn't have to go hunting for problems. Problems come to her.

**[SCREEN: Click on the flagged review. A side panel opens with full details: author name, date, the complaint about slow service.]**

NARRATOR:
One click, she's reading it. And look — right here — she can reply directly.

**[SCREEN: Maria types a thoughtful response. Hits "Send." The review now shows "Replied" with a timestamp.]**

NARRATOR:
That two-star just became an opportunity. The customer feels heard. Google sees engagement. Everyone wins.

**[SCREEN: Dashboard view shifts to the Analytics page. A large "4.3" average rating. A green up-arrow labeled "Trending Up."]**

NARRATOR:
Now here's where it gets interesting. ReviewPulse isn't just showing Maria her reviews — it's showing her the *trend.* Last thirty days compared to the thirty before that. She's up two-tenths of a point.

**[SCREEN: Bar chart showing reviews by source: Google (47), Yelp (23), Manual (5).]**

NARRATOR:
And she can see where her reviews are coming from. Turns out? Most of her happy customers are on Google. That's useful. That's something she can act on.

**[SCREEN: Back to review list. Maria clicks a 5-star review. Then clicks "Feature this review."]**

NARRATOR:
That amazing review she wanted on her website? She clicks "Feature" —

**[SCREEN: Cut to Maria's live coffee shop website. A beautiful widget at the bottom shows: "4.3 stars from 75 reviews" with a carousel of featured testimonials.]**

NARRATOR:
— and it's there. Live. On her site. Her best customers, in her customers' words, doing her marketing for her.

**[SCREEN: Close on the widget. Real reviews. Real names. Real stars. Then pull back to show the whole website, polished and professional.]**

NARRATOR:
No copying and pasting. No screenshots. No begging the web developer to make an update. Just... done.

**[SCREEN: Split screen — left side shows old Maria: three tabs, stressed, scrolling. Right side shows new Maria: one tab, smiling, sipping coffee.]**

NARRATOR:
Maria used to spend forty-five minutes every morning managing her reviews. Now? Five minutes. And she actually *knows* what people are saying about her business.

**[SCREEN: ReviewPulse dashboard. A notification pops in: "New 5-star review from Google." Maria smiles.]**

NARRATOR:
That's ReviewPulse. Your reviews. One place. Finally.

**[SCREEN: Logo. Tagline beneath it: "Stop chasing reviews. Start using them."]**

NARRATOR:
Stop chasing reviews. Start using them.
