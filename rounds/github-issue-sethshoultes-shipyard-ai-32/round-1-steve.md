# ReviewPulse: Design & Brand Position

## Product Naming

**Kill "ReviewPulse."** It's forgettable. It sounds like enterprise middleware from 2008.

Call it **Chorus**. One word. The name tells the whole story: your customers are singing your praises, and we help you conduct that symphony. Reviews aren't data points—they're voices. A chorus.

## Design Philosophy

Here's what makes this insanely great: **zero chrome**.

The plugin should disappear. The reviews are the star, not our interface. Every pixel we add between a business owner and their customer's words is a pixel too many. The admin panel should feel like reading a letter, not operating software.

The display widgets? They should look like they grew organically from the site's design—not like a widget was bolted on. We're not building a plugin. We're building glass—you look through it, not at it.

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

You connect your Google account. One click.

Boom—your reviews appear. Not in a loading spinner purgatory. Not behind three config screens. They're just *there*. Beautiful. Readable. Actionable.

You see a review that deserves a response. You click it. You type. You send. Done.

That's the first 30 seconds. Connect. See. Respond. Three verbs. If it takes longer, we've failed.

## Brand Voice

**Warm. Confident. Never robotic.**

"You've got 3 new reviews this week" not "3 reviews detected in sync cycle."

"Sarah loved her experience" not "Positive sentiment: 0.92 confidence."

We speak like the best maitre d' you've ever met—professional, attentive, human. We celebrate wins and handle problems with grace. No jargon. No dashboards. No analytics theater.

## What to Say NO To

- **NO to sentiment analysis graphs.** You don't need a chart to know a 1-star review is bad.
- **NO to AI-generated response suggestions.** Your voice is your brand. We don't manufacture it.
- **NO to review aggregation scores.** One angry customer matters more than your 4.7 average.
- **NO to notification overload.** One daily digest. That's it.
- **NO to competitive benchmarking.** Focus on your customers, not your rivals.

Simplicity isn't about doing less—it's about doing the right things magnificently.

## The Emotional Hook

Business owners lie awake at night wondering: *What are people saying about me?*

Chorus gives them peace. It's the difference between anxiety and awareness. When a customer raves, you celebrate it. When they complain, you fix it—fast, personally, with dignity.

People will love this because it makes them feel *in control* of their reputation without becoming slaves to it. That's the hook: **confidence without obsession**.

---

*The goal isn't to manage reviews. The goal is to make every customer feel heard—and to let business owners sleep at night.*
