# Shipyard Pulse — Demo Script

**Runtime:** 2 minutes
**Tone:** Human, warm, slightly urgent at the open. Not a pitch deck. A story.

---

## SCENE 1: THE PROBLEM (0:00 - 0:35)

NARRATOR:
So here's a thing that happens.

[SCREEN: A calendar. Tuesday morning. Coffee cup visible at the edge of frame.]

NARRATOR:
You built a website six months ago. Maybe it's for your bakery. Maybe it's your law practice. Maybe it's the nonprofit you pour your weekends into.

[SCREEN: A simple, beautiful small business website. Loading. Then—frozen. Spinner.]

NARRATOR:
And then one day a customer says: "Hey, I tried to visit your site and... it's kind of slow?"

[SCREEN: An iPhone showing the same site, loading bar stuck at 60%.]

NARRATOR:
And you feel that thing. That little twist in your stomach. Because you don't know. You haven't checked. You've been busy *running the business* the website is supposed to support.

[SCREEN: Quick cuts—a florist arranging flowers, a lawyer in a meeting, someone coaching a little league team. Life happening.]

NARRATOR:
When was the last time it went down? Is it slow for everyone or just that one customer? Is something broken and you just... don't know?

[SCREEN: Black screen. Single line of text: "You shouldn't have to wonder."]

---

## SCENE 2: THE PRODUCT (0:35 - 1:30)

NARRATOR:
This is Pulse.

[SCREEN: A clean, minimal dashboard. White space. One large number in the center: "94" with the letter grade "A" beside it. Below: "Your site is healthy."]

NARRATOR:
Once a month, Pulse checks your site. Load time. Uptime. The stuff that matters. And it gives you one number. Your Health Score.

[SCREEN: The score "94" gently highlighted. Below it, two simple metrics: "Load time: 1.2s" and "Uptime: 99.8%".]

NARRATOR:
Not a hundred metrics. Not a dashboard you need a degree to read. One number. One glance. You either feel good, or you know something needs attention.

[SCREEN: Cursor hovers over the Health Score. A small tooltip appears: "Based on load time, uptime, and Google PageSpeed performance."]

NARRATOR:
And here's the thing—you don't even have to come here.

[SCREEN: Transition to an email inbox. A new message appears. Subject line: "Your site had a great month."]

NARRATOR:
Every month, Pulse sends you an email. And the subject line tells you everything.

[SCREEN: Email opens. Clean design. The Health Score is right there at the top—big, confident, readable in two seconds. Below: "Load time improved 15% this month." A single button: "See details."]

NARRATOR:
Three seconds. That's all you need. Your site is fine. You can go back to your life.

[SCREEN: The email fades slightly. The user's thumb taps the archive button. Done.]

NARRATOR:
And if something *does* need your attention?

[SCREEN: A different email. Subject: "Your site slowed down this month." Health Score shows 67, grade "D".]

NARRATOR:
You'll know. And we'll tell you exactly what to do about it.

[SCREEN: Email shows a simple recommendation: "Your images are larger than necessary. Here's how to compress them."]

---

## SCENE 3: THE WOW (1:30 - 2:00)

NARRATOR:
Here's what we didn't build.

[SCREEN: Quick cuts of crossed-out features—Slack notifications, complex dashboards, weekly reports, login screens.]

NARRATOR:
No weekly alerts. No noise. No app to check. No password to remember—unless you want to.

[SCREEN: Back to the monthly email. Simple. Calm. The Health Score prominent and clear.]

NARRATOR:
Pulse is the opposite of everything fighting for your attention. It's one email a month that earns the right to exist by telling you something worth knowing.

[SCREEN: Split screen—left side shows the business owner at their shop, smiling at a customer. Right side shows the Pulse email sitting unopened but present in their inbox. A quiet confidence.]

NARRATOR:
Your website is alive. You don't have to check. We're watching.

[SCREEN: The Shipyard Pulse logo. Tagline below: "Know your site is healthy."]

NARRATOR:
And the best part?

[SCREEN: Pricing appears. "$99/month. Cancel anytime."]

NARRATOR:
It costs less than the anxiety.

[SCREEN: Fade to black. Single button: "Start your free trial."]
