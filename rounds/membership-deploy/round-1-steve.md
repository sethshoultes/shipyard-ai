# Steve Jobs — Design & Brand Positions
## MemberShip Plugin Deploy

---

## Product Naming: NO

"MemberShip" is forgettable committee-speak. Two words mashed together. What does it *mean*? Nothing.

Call it **Portal**. One word. Evocative. It's the gateway between public and private. Between stranger and community. When someone types `portal.sunriseyoga.com`, they *feel* the transition. They're crossing a threshold.

---

## Design Philosophy: Invisible Simplicity

The best plugins are the ones you never *see*. They feel like native features. Like they were always there.

This isn't a "plugin" — it's infrastructure. It's the plumbing that makes membership *work*. Zero banned patterns means zero technical debt. Zero shortcuts. Zero apologies.

If you have to explain how it works, you failed. It should just... work.

---

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

Someone visits Sunrise Yoga. They see "Members Only" content. They click.

**Boom.** Email field. Clean. White. Nothing else.

They type their email. Hit enter.

**Boom.** "Check your inbox." That's it. No loading spinners. No progress bars. No "please wait while we..."

They open the email. Click the link.

**Boom.** They're in. The page loads. The content appears. They feel *welcomed*, not *processed*.

Three moments. Three seconds each. Nine seconds to magic.

---

## Brand Voice: Confident Silence

This product doesn't speak. It whispers.

No "Congratulations!" No "Welcome to our community!" No exclamation points anywhere.

Just: "You're in." Period.

Status messages are factual, not chatty. "Invalid email" not "Oops! That email doesn't look quite right!" We're not your friend. We're your doorman.

---

## What to Say NO To

**NO** to admin dashboards on day one. If you need charts and graphs to understand your members, you don't understand your members.

**NO** to feature flags. **NO** to A/B tests. **NO** to "we could add..." conversations.

**NO** to "enterprise features." If Sunrise Yoga needs something, we build it. If someone else needs something different, they're using the wrong product.

**NO** to middleware. **NO** to lifecycle hooks. **NO** to "extensibility." Extensibility is just fear of making decisions.

This is THREE STEPS. Copy. Test. Document. That's the entire PRD. Don't add a fourth.

---

## The Emotional Hook: It Just Works

People will love this because they'll never think about it.

Remember the iPod? You didn't love the firmware. You loved that you stopped thinking about MP3 players and started thinking about music.

This plugin is the same. Studio owners won't love the API. They'll love that membership *just works*. They'll love that they never get support tickets. They'll love that they forgot they even installed it.

The emotional hook is **absence of friction**. It's the feeling of glass. Smooth. Seamless. Inevitable.

---

## Final Position: Ship It or Kill It

This PRD is perfect because it's ruthlessly focused. Three steps. No scope creep. No "nice to haves."

Either we execute these three steps flawlessly, or we admit we can't ship simple things and burn the whole project.

There's no middle ground. No "partial deploy." No "MVP with future enhancements."

Ship it clean. Ship it tested. Ship it documented. Or don't ship at all.

That's the standard.
