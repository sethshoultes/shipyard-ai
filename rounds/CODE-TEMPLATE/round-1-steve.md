# Steve's Take — CODE-TEMPLATE

## Product Naming

**Codex.** One word. It sounds like something forged, not drafted. A template is a piece of paper — a codex is a standard that outlasts empires. Builders don't "fill out" a Codex. They *enter* it. Names shape behavior. Call it CODE-TEMPLATE and you get bureaucracy. Call it Codex and you get religion.

The filename can stay technical for the file system. But when we speak about this product, we speak about Codex. The word matters because the word *makes* it matter.

## Design Philosophy

This isn't documentation. It's a **lathe for thought**. A PRD should carve away everything that doesn't serve the build until the builder has no choice but to write great code. Most PRDs are cluttered junk drawers — ours is a surgeon's tray. Every field earns its place by preventing a mistake. If it doesn't stop a bug or spark an insight, it's dead weight. Cut it.

The philosophy is **ruthless utility dressed in absolute clarity**. We don't decorate. We engineer. Every line in this template is a guardrail that keeps a builder from driving into a ditch at midnight.

## The First 30 Seconds

You open this file and you feel **clarity like a door slamming shut**. No preamble. No throat-clearing. Frontmatter, then Background, then the steel. You know exactly where you are and what must be done. It's the same feeling you get when a Mac wakes from sleep — no questions, just readiness.

There is no confusion because there is no ambiguity. The structure *forces* focus. You can't wander. You can't hide behind vagueness. The template demands your best thinking upfront so the builder never has to clean up your mess.

## Brand Voice

We speak like a **master craftsman handing a chisel to an apprentice**. Direct. Uncompromising. We say "Do NOT say 'implement a function' without giving the signature" because ambiguity is violence against the builder's time. We don't suggest — we specify. The voice is the product. If the voice is soft, the code is soft.

We are not polite. We are clear. Politeness is padding. Padding is waste. Every sentence in this template should feel like a command that makes the reader more competent.

## What We Say NO To

- NO "synthesize this for me" — embed the data or delete the PRD.
- NO vague acceptance criteria — "works correctly" is a confession of laziness.
- NO delegation of reading to the builder — the PRD is the source of truth, not a treasure map.
- NO hollow builds — if there aren't three source files and a test, it isn't real.
- NO empathy without standards — feeling sorry for a sloppy PRD doesn't ship software.

Simplicity isn't minimalism. It's the **relentless removal of the inessential** until only the inevitable remains.

## The Emotional Hook

People will love this because it **respects their time like nothing else they've used**. Most templates are homework. Codex is a superpower. When a builder opens it, they don't sigh — they lean forward. They know that if they follow the steel, they will ship something solid. That feeling of trust, of frictionless momentum, is addictive. You don't adopt Codex. You **convert** to it.

The love comes from relief. Relief that someone finally had the courage to say: *this is how it's done, no exceptions, no excuses.*
