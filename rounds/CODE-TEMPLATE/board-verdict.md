# Consolidated Board Verdict — CODE-TEMPLATE

**Panel:** Oprah Winfrey (6/10), Warren Buffett (5/10), Jensen Huang (3/10), Shonda Rhimes (3/10)
**Average Score:** 4.25/10

---

## Points of Agreement Across Board Members

1. **Functional but forgettable.** All four reviewers acknowledge the template works—gates catch hollow builds, verbatim contracts reduce hallucination, validation is fast—but agree it inspires no loyalty, emotion, or memory. Oprah calls it "utterly forgettable"; Shonda calls it "infrastructure without heartbeat."

2. **Not a business in its current form.** Jensen, Buffett, and Shonda converge on the same capital conclusion: this is an internal linting tool, not an investable product. Buffett labels it a "cost center"; Jensen says "zero defensibility"; Shonda says "dead on arrival."

3. **Zero competitive moat.** Jensen and Buffett both note it is copyable in a weekend. There is no data network, no learning loop, no switching cost, and no lock-in. Oprah implies the same by calling the branding and experience "hollow."

4. **Onboarding fails the human test.** Oprah and Shonda agree that new users are dropped into jargon rather than guided through a journey. Oprah: "New developer needs 20 minutes to feel safe. Should be 2." Shonda: "No protagonist. No journey from chaos to clarity."

5. **Validation is primitive.** Jensen calls regex validation in 2026 "laughable." Buffett praises the *concept* of gates but gives no credit to the implementation. There is consensus that the enforcement layer is underpowered.

6. **No feedback loop or compounding.** All reviewers note that usage today does not make the product smarter tomorrow. No corpus of PRDs, no success correlation, no template evolution, no community contribution mechanism.

---

## Points of Tension

1. **Severity of diagnosis.** Shonda advocates "fix it or kill it" with near-zero patience for the current artifact. Oprah sees "solid bones" and recommends renovation. Buffett is willing to keep it as proprietary hygiene. The board is split between mercy-kill and intensive-care.

2. **AI-native vs. capital-efficient simplicity.** Jensen demands an LLM-native rebuild with semantic grading, auto-generated acceptance criteria, and embedding-space reuse. Buffett celebrates the pure-shell, zero-dependency stack and warns against scope creep. They disagree on whether intelligence or austerity is the right foundation.

3. **Open-source vs. proprietary.** Buffett argues MIT licensing before proving internal value is "cart before horse"—charity, not investment. Oprah wants broader accessibility ("Quick Start for Writers"). Jensen ignores licensing entirely and focuses on platform extensibility. No unified stance on distribution strategy.

4. **Product scope: narrow excellence vs. platform expansion.** Jensen sketches a platform path (PRD marketplace, predictive scoring, API for orchestrators). Buffett worries about phantom assets (AgentPress plugin references, non-existent repos). Oprah wants a warmer *template*, not a platform. The board disagrees on whether to deepen or widen.

5. **Audience: coders-only vs. inclusive builders.** Oprah insists on entry points for PMs, designers, non-technical writers, and neurodivergent planners. Jensen and Buffett write primarily for engineering leadership. Shonda speaks to storytellers but still assumes a technical builder as protagonist.

---

## Overall Verdict: **HOLD**

Do not ship, fund, or open-source further in its current state. The artifact has structural integrity but no soul, no defensibility, and no economic engine. Three of four board members rate it at or below 5/10, and two effectively recommend termination without major surgery. However, Oprah’s "solid bones" assessment and Buffett’s observation that the gate "pays for itself on first prevented rebuild" indicate that a rebuilt version *could* graduate.

The project stays in HOLD until it meets the conditions below.

---

## Conditions for Proceeding

1. **LLM-Native Validation Layer.** Replace regex/string-matching validation with semantic understanding: LLM-graded PRD quality, ambiguity detection, and auto-generation of acceptance criteria from background context. Close the loop between spec and build trace. (Addresses Jensen)

2. **Proven Internal ROI.** Produce metrics—time saved, rebuilds prevented, build accuracy improvement—that justify continued investment. Do not open-source or productize until faith-based ROI becomes evidence-based ROI. (Addresses Buffett)

3. **Human-First Onboarding & Branding.** Lead with before-after pain, not jargon. Include a "Quick Start for Writers" parallel to installation docs. Rename from machine-first "Codex" branding to mission-driven language. Target <2 minutes to first feeling of safety. (Addresses Oprah)

4. **Narcissistic Retention Architecture.** Inject builder persona journey, validation-score progress loops, PRD streak counters, and episodic case studies ("The PRD That Saved AgentPress"). End every section with stakes, not yawns. (Addresses Shonda)

5. **Resolved Scope & Asset Hygiene.** Remove phantom references (AgentPress plugin code, non-existent GitHub repos). Deliver exactly what is documented, or document exactly what is delivered. (Addresses Buffett)

6. **Explicit Business Model Decision.** Choose one path and commit:
   - **Proprietary Internal Tool:** Keep private, optimize for agency-wide adoption and network effects.
   - **Platform Product:** Build SaaS wrapper, pricing tiers, support contracts, API, and plugin architecture.
   Do not straddle both. (Addresses Buffett & Jensen)

**Gate to exit HOLD:** Pass a live board demo showing (a) a non-coder completing their first PRD in under 5 minutes, (b) a semantic validator catching a real ambiguity, and (c) a retention metric proving a user returned voluntarily within 7 days.
