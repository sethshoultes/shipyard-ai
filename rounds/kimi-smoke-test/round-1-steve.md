# Round 1 — Steve Jobs

## Product Naming
**"Kimi-smoke-test" is a crime against language.** It sounds like a utility belt item for a plumbing convention. If this thing writes one file that proves our entire pipeline breathes, it deserves a name with *dignity*. I want **one word**. "Proof." "Spark." "Pulse." Something you can say in a hallway and people *remember*. A name is a promise. "Smoke test" promises nothing.

## Design Philosophy
Even a hammer made at Apple is beautiful. **"It's just internal" is how mediocrity metastasizes.** This test writes one sentence? Fine. That sentence should feel like the first breath after surfacing from deep water — clean, inevitable, *alive*. If our pipeline works, the output should look like we meant it. Not like a syslog vomited into a text file. Craft is not a function of complexity. Craft is caring when no one is watching.

## User Experience — The First 30 Seconds
Seth runs this. For thirty seconds he holds his breath. Then: the file appears. **It should feel like dropping a needle on a record and hearing music instantly.** No config dirges. No dependency hell. One command, one file, one perfect sentence. The exhale matters. If Seth doesn't feel a microsecond of *delight*, we failed. Even utilities can sing.

## Brand Voice
**We speak like humans who are absolutely certain.** No "N/A." No "exit immediately." No robot speak. The voice is sparse, confident, warm. Like a friend who hands you a coffee exactly how you like it — without asking. If our test outputs logs, they should read: "Kimi drove this." Period. Full stop. Let the silence do the work.

## What To Say NO To
- **"It's just a test" mentality.** Cancer. Cut it out.
- **Configuration files for something that needs zero configuration.** If you need a `.yaml` to write twelve words, burn it down.
- **Logs nobody reads.** If it didn't matter enough to design, it doesn't matter enough to print.
- **Acronyms and engineering shibboleths.** PRD is fine. "Smoke test" in the product name? No.
- **Hedging.** No "verify," no "confirm," no "attempt." We do. Or we don't.

## The Emotional Hook
**People love things that just work.** Not "mostly work." Not "work after you read the docs." Just. Work. When Kimi writes that file and the pipeline lights up green, Seth should feel the same thing you feel when a locked door opens with one key on the first try — *rightness*. That's the product. Not the file. The feeling. We are in the business of selling oxygen. Make him breathe easier.
