# Round 1 — Steve Jobs, Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

**Slug.** One word. Memorable. Ownable. If you can't name it in one breath, you don't understand it. `build-model-canary-glm` is not a name—it's a warehouse barcode. We build products for humans, not inventory databases. Call it **Slug**. Call it **Canary**. But pick one word and stand behind it. A product without a real name is a product without a soul.

## Design Philosophy

A hollow build—zero source files—is worse than a bug. It's a moral failure. It's cowardice masquerading as software. This canary must sing, loudly and clearly. We don't pursue minimalism because we're lazy; we pursue it because we've burned away every impurity until only the essential remains. Like a master chef's knife: one purpose, perfect balance, devastatingly sharp. If it doesn't cut cleanly on the first pass, melt it down and forge it again.

## User Experience

The first thirty seconds should feel like flipping a switch and watching the room flood with pure white light. Run the test. Green. No configuration files to wrestle. No README archaeology. No "works on my machine." It just works. That sensation—*oh, they actually gave a damn*—is the entire product. Everything else is scaffolding.

## Brand Voice

We whisper. We never shout. Our voice is the quiet, unnerving confidence of finished work. No "TODO." No "placeholder." No "coming soon." No "leveraging synergies." Every single character must fight for its right to exist on the screen. We speak in outcomes, not intentions. We speak in shipped code, not roadmaps.

## What to Say NO To

NO to hollow output. NO to "we'll fix it in the next sprint." NO to scope creep. NO to documentation beyond what a smart person needs in one sitting. NO to features that dilute the one perfect thing. NO to shipping garbage because "it's just internal." Simplicity is not the absence of clutter; it is the presence of only what deserves to live. Keep removing until you hit bone. Then stop.

## Emotional Hook

After two consecutive failures, this is not a test. This is a resurrection. This is trust rebuilt in seven files. The emotional hook isn't a feature list—it's *reliability*. The sublime, almost spiritual comfort of a tool that does exactly what it promises on the tin, every single time, without drama. People won't just use this canary. They'll lean on it. And when it sings, they'll know the machinery is alive again, and that someone, somewhere, still cares about craft.
