# Round 1: Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

"WordPress Intelligence Suite" is corporate white noise. Six syllables of forgettable. It sounds like a Microsoft Server pack from 1998. **We need ONE word.** I want to call this **NEXUS** — the single point where speed, team, and intelligence converge. Or **SPARK** — what happens when WordPress wakes up. But whatever we choose, burn the acronym. Burn the word "Suite." If you need three words to name it, you don't understand it.

## Design Philosophy

The PRD says "feel like WordPress built it." I say: **WordPress admin looks like a database threw up on a spreadsheet.** Native is not a virtue. Inevitable is. Every pixel should feel so right that the user can't imagine the dashboard without it. We're not decorating WordPress. We're replacing its nervous system. If it doesn't make you smile, it's wrong.

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

You do NOT get three minutes. You get thirty seconds. The PRD proposes a wizard. Wizards are what engineers build when they don't know what the user wants. **We know.** The moment this plugin activates, LocalGenius should already be configured — scraped, populated, live. Dash should glow with a single, beautiful tooltip: "Press Cmd+K." Pinned should already have a note waiting: "Welcome. Touch here." No setup. No choices. **Magic first, permission later.**

## Brand Voice

This product does not "cross-promote." It does not "upsell." It speaks like a brilliant colleague who arrives early and has already done the work. Warm. Confident. Never apologetic. When you hit a limit, it says: *"You're moving fast. Want me to keep up?"* Not: *"Upgrade now!"* We sell power, not features.

## What to Say NO To

- **NO to feature toggles.** The user sees ONE product, not three plugins wearing a trenchcoat.
- **NO to the template marketplace in v1.** Focus means saying no to a thousand good ideas so you can say yes to the great one.
- **NO to "upgrade cards" in the dashboard.** Don't build a billboard. Build a cockpit.
- **NO to white-labeling as a headline feature.** If agencies want to hide our name, we haven't made them proud enough to show it.
- **NO to annual-only billing masquerading as a feature.** It's not a feature. It's a terms-of-service clause. Keep it out of the product story.

## The Emotional Hook

People will love this because for the first time, WordPress stops being a **tool** and starts being a **teammate**. It knows what you need before you ask. It answers your customers while you sleep. It remembers what your team agreed on last Tuesday. This isn't software. **It's competence you can install.**
