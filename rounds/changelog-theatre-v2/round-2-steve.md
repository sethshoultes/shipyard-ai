Elon wants to ship static slides with a voiceover and call it "honest iteration." That is not honesty. It is giving up before the fight starts.

**Where he optimizes for the wrong metric:**
He measures whether `outputUrl` returns an MP4. I measure whether someone watches to the final frame. Elon proposes HTML5 vertical scroll or static slides as v0.5. That is not a product. It is a faster way to be ignored. Changelogs are already boring. If we strip the cinema, we have nothing.

He wants to watermark every video and deploy reply bots as "distribution." That is not growth. It is graffiti. We do not build billboards. We build cathedrals and let people find them.

**What I will defend:**
Cut the score? Cut the voice? Cut the scene composer? Then we are ClipCraft with better copy. The score and narration are not features. They are the reason this product exists. Without emotional architecture, we are a utility. Utilities get commoditized. Experiences get remembered.

We do not need a celebrity voice clone — we need *weight*, *cadence*, *urgency*. OpenAI's voices can deliver precision if we direct them with taste. And "no preview mode" stands. You do not preview a symphony in fragments. The artist controls the timing.

**Where Elon is right:**
Workers cannot render MP4s. Physics wins. We need a real render target — Fly.io, Remotion Lambda, a VPS. I accept that. He is right about burn: free cannot mean infinite. Cap renders. Build a paid tier. And decouple the renderer from the API — clean interfaces are not in conflict with beauty.

**Three non-negotiables:**
1. **The first 30 seconds are sacred.** Black screen. Single shaft of light. Title as film. No dashboards. No config. One button, then curtain.
2. **Voice and score are the product.** Premium TTS. Licensed or generated music with real emotional weight. No robotic utility.
3. **No growth-hack distribution.** No watermarks. No reply bots. No spam. We spread by beauty, not volume. Organic admiration is the only metric.

Aria is a mirror. If we build what Elon suggests, we have built a faster mirror that nobody wants to look into.
