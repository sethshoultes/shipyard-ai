# Aria — Demo Script (2:00)

---

NARRATOR
You shipped forty-seven commits this week.

[SCREEN: Tight on a GitHub commit graph. A sea of green dots.]

NARRATOR
Forty-seven. And not one person — not your CEO, not your mother, not that investor who ghosted you three emails ago — is ever gonna read them.

[SCREEN: Scroll blur through a wall of commit messages. "fix: typo." "wip." "tests passing."]

NARRATOR
We have a storytelling problem. Code is invisible by design. It enters the repo, it dies in the repo. And when someone finally asks — "Hey, what'd you build this month?" — you open your mouth and... nothing. Forty-seven commits. Zero narrative.

[SCREEN: Cut to black. A cursor blinks in a single input field. Placeholder text: "owner/repo"]

NARRATOR
So. You paste the repo.

[SCREEN: Hands type "sethshoultes/shipyard-ai" into a stark black webpage. One button below it: "Roll Credits."]

NARRATOR
You pick a voice. Alloy, Echo, Onyx — doesn't matter, they're all better than yours.

[SCREEN: Cursor clicks the button. Screen snaps to black. A thin gold line appears at the bottom — a rising curtain. Text fades in: "Fetching commits..."]

NARRATOR
Then you wait. Six minutes. Eight. Twelve, if the internet's having feelings. But here's the thing — it's honest waiting. No fake progress bar dancing across the screen like a liar. Just a black curtain, rising. Because good cinema takes time.

[SCREEN: The gold line climbs. Status text cross-dissolves: "Writing the script..." then "Recording narration..." then "Rendering scenes..."]

NARRATOR
Behind that curtain? We're pulling raw commits from GitHub. GPT-4 is arguing with itself about which ones matter. OpenAI is warming up its vocal cords. And a headless browser in a data center somewhere is compositing three scenes of vertical cinema.

[SCREEN: The gold line reaches the top. The screen blooms open — 9:16 vertical video, black background, off-white type.]

NARRATOR
Then it happens.

[SCREEN: Title card — "SHIPYARD-AI" in sharp serif type. Subtle light shafts. Ambient drone swells.]

NARRATOR (V.O., overlapping)
"In the beginning, there was nothing but a blank repository and a dream that wouldn't die."

[SCREEN: Scene two. Text overlays drift across the frame like smoke, timed to narration. Each commit rephrased as dramatic narration.]

NARRATOR (V.O.)
"They fixed the API rate limit at 3 AM. They rewrote the queue worker because the old one choked under load. They deleted four thousand lines of code and called it progress."

[SCREEN: Slow push-in on the final text overlay.]

NARRATOR (V.O., building)
"They shipped. They broke things. They shipped again."

[SCREEN: Hard cut to outro. Black. "That was you. That mattered." Then: "Made by Aria."]

NARRATOR
That was you. That mattered.

[SCREEN: Freeze on the end card. Underneath, a download link materializes. MP4. Sixty seconds. Your week, turned into a movie trailer.]

NARRATOR
Forty-seven commits. One minute of cinema. Paste the repo. Roll the credits. Let your work be remembered.

[SCREEN: Cut to black. URL: aria.sh. Single input field waiting.]

NARRATOR
Aria. Changelog theatre. Because invisible work is a tragedy.

[SCREEN: Fade out.]
