# Aria

## Naming
"Changelog Theatre" is a specification, not a name. We don't name children "Human Baby v2." This product is **Aria** — one word, like iPod, like Mac, like Siri. A solo voice lifted by orchestration, turning the mechanical into the operatic. When you hear the name, you know: something beautiful is about to happen. If the name needs a subtitle, the product is too complicated.

## Design Philosophy
Most developer tools treat code like accounting. We treat it like cinema. The philosophy is this: **every commit is a decision, and every decision is dramatic.** We don't decorate data; we reveal the story that was already there. It is a typewriter in a cathedral — raw, honest text elevated by architecture of sound and motion. If the output doesn't give you chills, it isn't done. "Good enough" is a dangerous drug.

## The First 30 Seconds
You paste a repository. You press one button. The screen goes black. A single shaft of light. Your repository name appears not as a file path, but as a film title. Then the voice — that Sorkin cadence, that walk-and-talk rhythm: *"Three days. Fourteen commits. One obsession."* The score breathes beneath it. You're not waiting for a render. You're waiting for the curtain. No dashboards. No config panels. Just the drop of a needle on a record.

## Brand Voice
Aria speaks like a producer who respects your intelligence. Confident, fast, precise. We never say "Your changelog has been generated." We say, "Your week, narrated." We assume you are an artist, not a technician. We don't explain the orchestra. We let you hear it. Every sentence is a promise kept.

## What We Say NO To
NO settings panels. If you need preferences, we have already failed. NO commit hashes on screen — nobody has ever cried reading `a1b2c3d`. NO watermarks; your work is the watermark. NO "download as MP4" options — the video plays, full stop. NO preview modes; you don't preview a symphony, you sit down and listen. NO voice-selector dropdowns. Aaron Sorkin or silence. Choice is not always freedom. Sometimes it's noise.

## Emotional Hook
Developers spend their lives in dim rooms stacking bricks they never see assembled. Aria holds up a mirror and says: *That was you. That mattered.* For sixty seconds, you are not a ticket number. You are not a resource. You are the protagonist of a film you didn't know you were making. People will not love this because it is useful. They will love it because it is **recognition**.
