# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer, Great Minds Agency

## Product Naming

**Shipyard.** Not Shipyard AI. Not Shipyard Pro. Just Shipyard. When you staple
"AI" to your name, you announce you are a feature, not a philosophy. A shipyard is
where raw steel becomes a vessel that crosses oceans. That is the poetry. One word.
Two syllables. Memorable because it means something. If you need a subtitle, bury
it — but the product must breathe as Shipyard.

## Design Philosophy

We do not build intake forms. We build a confessional. The customer arrives anxious
— they have a vision but no vessel. By the time they finish, they should feel like
we have read their mind. Every field, every checkbox, every breath of whitespace
must feel inevitable, like the next note in a symphony. If it is not insanely
great, it is garbage. Period.

## The First 30 Seconds

The customer lands. They see one question. Not a wall of YAML frontmatter. Not a
spreadsheet with forty-seven empty cells that looks like a tax audit designed by a
committee. One beautiful, human question. The progress bar breathes. The typography
whispers confidence. The color knows when to get out of the way. In thirty seconds,
they think: *These people actually care.* That is the bar. If they feel
administrative dread, we have failed and we should start over.

## Brand Voice

We speak like the master craftsman at the bench, not the carnival barker or the
startup bro. Warm, certain, exact. No "leverage our cutting-edge AI synergies."
No exclamation points. No emojis. We say: *"We will build this. It will be
beautiful. Here is exactly how."* Every sentence carries weight because we do not
waste the customer's time. Authority is the ultimate kindness.

## What We Say NO To

NO to every field that exists because "someone might need it someday." Simplicity
is not the absence of clutter; it is the presence of only what is essential. NO to
multi-tier complexity that treats customers like cattle in a chute. NO to
"AI-generated" as a product feature — it is a method, not a benefit, and it ages
like milk. NO to asking the customer to do the work. If they could fill out forty
metadata fields, they would have built the site themselves. Cut it in half. Then
cut it again.

## The Emotional Hook

People do not love tools. They love the feeling of becoming someone who *ships.* Our
customer is the person with a dream who has been burned by agencies, drowned in
Jira tickets, buried under revision seventeen of a Figma file that no one owns.
Shipyard is the moment the fog clears and they see their name, live, on a real URL
that the world can touch. That is not a transaction — it is liberation. They will
love us because we returned their ambition to them: polished, launched, and
undeniable.
