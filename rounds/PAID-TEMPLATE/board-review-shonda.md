# Board Review — Shonda Rhimes

**Verdict: 5/10.** Clean procedural. No season renewal.

---

## Story Arc
- Intake → contract → agents → deploy preview = solid three-act structure.
- "Aha moment" is the preview URL reveal. Strong.
- But no mid-season twist. Customer fills form, waits, gets site. One episode. No binge.

## Retention Hooks
- Referral loop (free month) = fine incentive, lousy *emotion*.
- No progress updates between intake and deploy. Dead air.
- No "your site is 60% built" heartbeat. No reason to open inbox tomorrow.
- Deploy preview is approval mechanism, not reunion.

## Content Flywheel
- None. Zero. Ghost town.
- No case studies surfaced during build. No "see what we shipped last week."
- No community of past customers. No "Shipyard Spotlight."
- Content strategy = crickets. No flywheel, no fuel.

## Emotional Cliffhangers
- Preview URL = one cliffhanger. Works once.
- No "what's next" after ship. No sequel hook.
- Beta program mentions case studies but buries them in ops. Should be front-of-house.
- No tease of upcoming features. No "v2 ships next quarter."

## What's Missing
- Serialized build updates (push notifications, not just email).
- Gallery of shipped sites = content engine + social proof + FOMO.
- Post-launch chapter: analytics dashboard, visitor count, "your first 100 views."
- Character growth: customer should feel *transformed* from intake to wrapped.

## Score
**5/10.** The build pipeline is Grey's pilot — competent, emotional beats in right places. But where's season 2? Where's the "I can't wait to see what happens next"?

Right now, Shipyard is a procedural. We need a serial.
