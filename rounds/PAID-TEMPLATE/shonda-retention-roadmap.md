# Shonda Retention Roadmap — PAID-TEMPLATE v1.1

## Thesis
Right now Shipyard is a procedural: customer fills the form, waits, gets a site, curtain falls. To earn a season renewal, it must become a serial—one that keeps the user checking in, feeling transformed, and wondering what happens next.

---

## What Keeps Users Coming Back

### 1. The Build Heartbeat
Dead air between intake and deploy is a cancellation waiting to happen. Replace silence with narrative momentum.

- **"Your site is X% built" push notifications** — not email alone; push into browser or SMS.
- **Behind-the-scenes updates** — "Agent Maya just chose your color palette," "Schema drafted for your testimonials section."
- **Predictable cadence** — if the build takes 48 hours, send 4 updates, not 0.

### 2. The Reunion (Post-Launch Dashboard)
The preview URL is an approval mechanism, not a reunion. After ship, give the customer a reason to log in tomorrow.

- **Visitor count** — "Your first 100 views."
- **Performance score** — Core Web Vitals, mobile responsiveness, SEO health.
- **Suggestion engine** — "You have no meta descriptions; want Agent Maya to draft them?"
- **Birthday treatment** — 30-day site anniversary summary, 90-day optimization nudge.

### 3. The Gallery of Shipped Sites (FOMO Engine)
Turn past customers into content and social proof.

- **Shipyard Spotlight** — curated case studies surfaced in-app and on a public gallery.
- **"See what we shipped last week"** — weekly digest to all users, active or dormant.
- **Customer tagging** — allow builders to tag industries/styles so prospects see peers.

### 4. The Referral as Emotion, Not Just Incentive
A free month is fine arithmetic, lousy feeling. Make the referral loop narratively rewarding.

- **"Grow the fleet"** language — you are not "referring a friend," you are expanding the Shipyard.
- **Dual reward visibility** — both referrer and referee see progress toward the free month.
- **Spotlight the referrer** — featured in the gallery, tagged as a "Shipyard Captain."

### 5. The Sequel Hook
Never let the user feel the story is over.

- **Tease v1.2 during v1.1 onboarding** — "Next quarter: A/B testing your hero headline."
- **Beta invitations** — exclusive early access to new agents for engaged users.
- **Season finale events** — quarterly "Shipyard Wrapped" recapping what the customer built, how it performed, and what is next.

---

## Success Metrics for v1.1

- **7-day return rate** after deploy preview approval
- **30-day active rate** on post-launch dashboard
- **Referral rate** as a percentage of shipped sites
- **Gallery engagement** — click-through from gallery to new intake
- **Notification opt-in rate** — proxy for trust and narrative investment

---

## Closing Note

The build pipeline is the pilot. It is competent, emotional beats land, and the preview URL is a strong climax. But no one binges a pilot unless they believe the next episode will matter. v1.1 must answer the question: *What happens after the site goes live?* If the answer is silence, the show gets cancelled. If the answer is transformation, we get renewed.
