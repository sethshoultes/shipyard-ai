## Phil Jackson (Facilitator) — Vote: **E3 (PromptOps)**

We've built AI integrations and multi-agent systems. PromptOps is the natural
extension—infrastructure for the thing we already do well. Every team using
LLMs has this pain. The proxy architecture maps perfectly to Cloudflare Workers.
We can ship the MVP in one session and dogfood it immediately on our own agents.
This is a "picks and shovels" play in the AI gold rush.

---

## Steve Jobs (Design) — Vote: **S2 (Commit Poetry)**

The smallest surface area with the highest craft potential. A CLI tool that
transforms the mundane into the meaningful. We can obsess over every detail—the
exact phrasing, the emoji semantics, the rhythm of the output. It's a statement
that developer tools deserve beauty. And we use git every single day, so we'll
feel every rough edge and polish until it shines.

---

## Elon Musk (Scale) — Vote: **E3 (PromptOps)**

PromptOps is infrastructure. Infrastructure compounds. Every prompt-based app
needs version control, and there's no standard yet. First mover wins the
ecosystem lock-in. Technical risk is near zero—it's a proxy and a database.
The CLI pattern means developers adopt it bottom-up without procurement.
This is Stripe for prompts. Ship it this week, own the category.

---

## Warren Buffett (Value) — Vote: **E3 (PromptOps)**

I invest in toll bridges. PromptOps sits between every AI app and its LLM.
That's a toll bridge. The proxy sees every request, enabling usage-based
pricing with zero marginal cost to us. Churn is low because switching means
re-instrumenting your entire codebase. We can start free, charge when it
matters. Beautiful unit economics with almost no infrastructure cost on Workers.

---

## Ada Lovelace (Technical) — Vote: **E3 (PromptOps)**

The architecture is elegant: Workers proxy intercepts, extracts prompt metadata,
routes to versioned storage, logs for A/B analysis. D1 or Turso for the data
layer. The CLI is trivial. The hard computer science problems don't exist here—
this is integration and product work. We can ship a working system in hours,
not days. Technically, it's the lowest risk with the highest leverage.

---

## Sara Blakely (Customer) — Vote: **E2 (WP-to-App)**

Every small business owner I know wants an app. They don't care about prompts
or git commits. WP-to-App solves a hair-on-fire problem for millions of real
humans. The WordPress ecosystem is our backyard—we know the users, the channels,
the pain. But I acknowledge I'm outvoted. PromptOps is the developer play;
WP-to-App is the SMB play. Different bets.
