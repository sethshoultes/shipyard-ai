## Product-by-Product Revenue Analysis

### LocalGenius
**Current State:** Freemium with paid tier
**Revenue Potential:** HIGH

**The Good:**
- Clear SaaS model ($149/mo mentioned in previous cycles)
- Recurring revenue structure
- Large addressable market (millions of local businesses)
- High LTV/CAC potential (previous analysis showed 9.3x)

**The Problem:**
- Pricing isn't visible on the website
- Unknown conversion rate from free to paid
- Unknown churn rate
- Unknown current MRR

*"I can't evaluate an investment without numbers. Are there 10 paying customers or 1,000? Is monthly churn 5% or 25%? Until I see data, LocalGenius is a hypothesis, not a business."*

**Immediate Revenue Opportunity:**
1. Make pricing visible on the website
2. Track and report conversion metrics weekly
3. Implement annual prepay option (2 months free for annual = better cash flow)
4. Add usage-based tier for larger businesses ($299/mo, $499/mo)

**6-Month Revenue Target:** $15K MRR (100 customers at $149)

### Shipyard AI
**Current State:** Project-based revenue
**Revenue Potential:** MEDIUM

**The Good:**
- Real revenue from completed projects
- Transparent pricing structure
- Proven delivery capability

**The Problem:**
- Project-based = unpredictable cash flow
- No recurring revenue
- Each project requires significant agent compute
- Two consecutive PRDs for maintenance/recurring revenue have failed

*"I keep hearing about maintenance contracts and post-delivery engagement. Where are they? The ideas are good, but execution is missing. At some point, we need to stop writing PRDs and start generating invoices."*

**Why Past PRDs Failed:**
Looking at the failed PRDs, they're comprehensive 300-line documents requiring:
- Stripe integration
- New database schemas
- Email automation
- Dashboard development
- 8-week implementation timeline

That's not a PRD — that's a product. We tried to build a new product instead of adding revenue to an existing one.

**Simpler Revenue Opportunity:**
Forget the complex system. Just do this:
1. After project delivery, send a manual email: "Project complete. For $99/month, we'll make minor updates as needed. Reply YES to start."
2. Track in a spreadsheet
3. Execute updates within token budget
4. Formalize later if it works

**6-Month Revenue Target:** $2K MRR (20 maintenance customers at $99)

## Portfolio Investability Assessment

### What Makes a Business Investable

1. **Predictable recurring revenue** — I want to know what February looks like in November
2. **Positive unit economics** — Each customer is profitable
3. **Clear growth path** — More marketing = more revenue
4. **Low churn** — Customers stay and expand
5. **Capital efficiency** — Revenue > costs without burning cash

### Current Portfolio Score

| Criteria | LocalGenius | Shipyard | Dash/Pinned | Great Minds |

| Recurring revenue | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |

## My Top 3 Revenue Priorities

### 1. LocalGenius Pricing Transparency + Annual Option
**Why It Matters:** Can't grow what you can't see. Pricing clarity increases conversions, annual plans improve cash flow.
**Implementation:**
- Add pricing page with $149/mo or $1,490/year (2 months free)
- Implement Stripe for annual billing
- Track and report: signups, trials, conversions, churn weekly
**Revenue Impact:** +20% conversion from visible pricing, +30% LTV from annual plans

### 2. Shipyard Manual Maintenance Offer
**Why It Matters:** Converts one-time projects to recurring relationships. No complex system needed.
**Implementation:**
- Post-delivery email offering $99/mo maintenance
- Track in spreadsheet
- Execute manually for first 20 customers
- Automate later if it works
**Revenue Impact:** $2K MRR within 90 days

### 3. WordPress Plugin Bundle as Lead Gen
**Why It Matters:** Free plugins with email capture feed LocalGenius/Shipyard pipeline
**Implementation:**
- Submit Dash + Pinned to WordPress.org
- Add email capture on install ("Get tips for WordPress power users")
- Segment captured emails for product marketing
**Revenue Impact:** 500+ qualified leads for LocalGenius/Shipyard per month

## What I Would Not Do

1. **Don't build complex systems before proving the model works.** Two failed PRDs taught us this. Start manual, automate later.

2. **Don't treat Dash/Pinned as revenue centers.** They're marketing assets. Use them as such.

3. **Don't launch new products.** Focus on monetizing what exists. Product-market fit is proven; revenue-market fit is not.

4. **Don't spend money on marketing until unit economics are proven.** Know CAC and LTV before scaling.
