## The First-5-Minutes Test

Every product gets one chance to show someone they're in the right place. I'm reviewing these products through the eyes of someone who just heard about them, clicked a link, and is now deciding whether to stay or leave.

---

## Product-by-Product Analysis

### 1. LocalGenius (localgenius.company)

**First Impression: B+**

*What works in the first 5 minutes:*
- The headline speaks directly to pain: "You didn't open a restaurant to do marketing."
- Maria's Kitchen example shows real results (340 visits, 4 reviews, 23 bookings)
- "5-minute setup, no credit card" removes commitment anxiety
- Conversational interface preview feels approachable

*Where new users get confused:*
- **Pricing is hidden.** The homepage says "See pricing" but doesn't show it. A confused mind says no. Show me the number.
- **"AI employee" is abstract.** What does that mean? Show me the actual conversation. Let me see what I'd type and what LocalGenius would say back.
- **The capabilities are too broad.** Website, reviews, social, analytics, email — it sounds like everything. New users don't believe in everything. They believe in one thing done well.
- **No human face.** The About page mentions "6 people in Austin" but no photos. I trust faces.

*The 5-minute fix:*
Add a 60-second demo video on the homepage showing: (1) a real conversation with LocalGenius, (2) an actual review response being generated, (3) a social post being created. Let me see it before I sign up.

### 2. Shipyard AI (shipyard.company)

**First Impression: C**

*What works in the first 5 minutes:*
- "PRD in. Production out." is clear
- The 6-stage pipeline visualization is compelling
- Token pricing table shows transparency

*Where new users get confused:*
- **What is a token?** Most people don't know. "500K-2M tokens" means nothing to someone who just wants a website built.
- **What is Emdash?** The page mentions "Emdash sites" and "Emdash themes" but never explains what Emdash is. Am I in the right place?
- **No examples of finished work.** I see a pipeline diagram but no portfolio. What has Shipyard actually built?
- **Who is this for?** Developers? Business owners? Agencies? The page assumes I already know.
- **No dollar amounts.** Tokens are a billing unit, but I need to know: "This typically costs $X."

*The 5-minute fix:*
Add a "See what we've built" section with 3 live examples. Add a pricing translator: "A typical marketing site: ~500K tokens (~$XX)". Explain Emdash in one sentence at the top.

### 3. Dash (WordPress Command Bar)

**First Impression: A-**

*What works in the first 5 minutes:*
- Name + tagline immediately clear: "Cmd+K command palette for WordPress admin"
- Zero dependencies, 6KB — signals it won't break anything
- Installation is 3 steps
- Screenshots would help but the description is clear

*Where new users get confused:*
- **What's a command palette?** Power users know. A WordPress blogger who wants to save time might not.
- **No video/GIF.** I want to see it in action before I install.

*The 5-minute fix:*
Add a 10-second GIF showing: press Cmd+K, type "settings", hit enter, arrive at settings page. That's the entire pitch in motion.

---

### 4. Pinned Notes (WordPress Sticky Notes)

**First Impression: A-**

*What works in the first 5 minutes:*
- "Post-it sticky notes for WordPress admin dashboard" — instant mental model
- Five colors, @mentions, note aging — features are concrete and visual
- Double-click creation is delightful

*Where new users get confused:*
- **"Team handoff notes" — what does that mean?** Give me a scenario. "Leave a note for the night editor: 'Don't publish until CEO approves.'"
- **No screenshot.** I can imagine sticky notes but I want to see them.

*The 5-minute fix:*
Add one screenshot of the dashboard with 3-4 colorful notes. That image IS the documentation.

### 5. Great Minds Plugin

**First Impression: B**

*What works in the first 5 minutes:*
- "Drop in a PRD. The agents debate strategy, plan in waves, build in parallel" — evocative description
- 14 agents with clear roles — this is intriguing
- Installation is one command

*Where new users get confused:*
- **Who is this for?** Developers using Claude Code. But how technical do I need to be?
- **What's a PRD?** Product Requirement Document. Not everyone knows.
- **The pipeline is complex.** Debate → Plan → Execute → Verify → Ship. That's a lot of phases. A new user might feel overwhelmed.
- **No example output.** What does a completed project look like?

*The 5-minute fix:*
Add a "See it in action" section: "We gave the agents this PRD: [link]. They built this: [link to live site]. Here's the debate transcript: [link]."

## Cross-Portfolio Patterns

### Pattern 1: Show Don't Tell
Four of five products describe capabilities without demonstrating them. Users believe what they see, not what they read.

**Fix:** Every product needs a video, GIF, or interactive demo on the first screen.

### Pattern 2: Missing Audience Identification
"Who is this for?" is unclear on Shipyard AI and Great Minds Plugin. People need to see themselves in the product.

**Fix:** Add a persona statement: "Built for [specific person] who wants to [specific outcome]."

### Pattern 3: Technical Jargon Leakage
Tokens, PRDs, FULLTEXT search, REST API — these terms appear before users understand the value proposition.

**Fix:** Lead with outcomes ("Save 10 clicks per day") then explain mechanism ("using a JSON index and...").

### Pattern 4: Hidden Pricing Creates Distrust
LocalGenius and Shipyard AI both require navigation to see pricing. This feels like you're hiding something.

**Fix:** Put pricing on the homepage. If you're proud of your price, show it.

## Oprah's Verdict

**You're speaking to yourselves, not to your audience.**

Every product in this portfolio was built by people who understand it deeply. That deep understanding created a blind spot: you assume others see what you see.

The restaurant owner doesn't know what an "AI employee" does until you show her.
The agency owner doesn't know what a "token" costs until you tell him in dollars.
The WordPress admin doesn't know what a "command palette" feels like until she watches a GIF.

**Show them. Show them in the first 5 seconds, not after they've committed.**

The biggest opportunity is Shipyard AI. It's the most confusing and the highest-value product. Fix the homepage, add a portfolio, translate tokens to money, and you'll see a step change in conversion.
