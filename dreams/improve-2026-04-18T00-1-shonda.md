## The Retention Question

Every product has a moment where the user decides: "Do I come back tomorrow?" That decision isn't random. It's engineered. I'm reviewing these products for their retention mechanics — the hooks, the cliffhangers, the "I have to check this" moments.

---

## Product-by-Product Analysis

### 1. LocalGenius (localgenius.company)

**Retention Status: MODERATE**

*Current hooks:*
- **Weekly digest (Monday)** — This is your retention anchor. Every Monday, the user has a reason to open LocalGenius. But is it enough?
- **Review notifications** — New reviews trigger responses, creating engagement loops.
- **Social post scheduling** — If users schedule posts, they return to see performance.

*What's missing:*

- **No daily hook.** Monday digest is good, but what about Tuesday-Sunday? The user forgets you exist.
- **No streak mechanic.** There's no "You've responded to 12 reviews in a row" or "3-week engagement streak."
- **No cliffhanger.** The weekly digest shows "340 visits" but doesn't say "Last week you had 280. What changed?" The story ends instead of continuing.
- **No milestone celebrations.** "You just hit 100 reviews!" "Your best week ever!" These create emotional peaks.
- **No competitive element.** "Restaurants like you average 400 visits. You're at 340." Light competition drives behavior.

*Retention improvements:*
1. **Daily micro-notification** — "3 people viewed your menu today" — just enough to stay top of mind
2. **Weekly cliffhanger** — End the digest with: "Next week, we're testing a new post format. I'll show you how it performs."
3. **Milestone badges** — Visual celebrations for 50 reviews, 1000 visitors, 6-month streak
4. **Trend lines, not snapshots** — "Visits are up 22% month-over-month" tells a story. "340 visits" is a fact.

### 2. Shipyard AI (shipyard.company)

**Retention Status: LOW (by design)**

*Current hooks:*
- None. This is a project-based service. Users engage when they have a project.

*The challenge:*
Shipyard isn't a daily-use product. It's more like hiring a contractor. You don't "retain" a contractor — you rehire them.

*What's missing:*
- **No post-project relationship.** After the site ships, what's the touchpoint?
- **No "what's next" prompt.** "Your site is live. What's the next thing you want to build?"
- **No maintenance check-ins.** "It's been 30 days since launch. Ready for V1.1?"

*Retention improvements:*
1. **Post-launch email sequence** — Day 7: "How's the site performing?" Day 30: "Ready for enhancements?" Day 90: "Time for a refresh?"
2. **Build history dashboard** — Let returning users see all their past Shipyard projects
3. **"What I learned" report** — After each build, send a summary: "Here's what we built, what we learned, and what you might want next."

### 3. Dash (WordPress Command Bar)

**Retention Status: HIGH (if habit forms)**

*Current hooks:*
- **Keyboard muscle memory** — Once Cmd+K is habit, it's sticky
- **Recent items** — The more you use it, the better it gets
- **Speed improvement** — Users feel faster, creating positive reinforcement

*What's missing:*
- **No onboarding nudge.** New users might install and forget. There's no "Try pressing Cmd+K now" prompt.
- **No usage celebration.** "You've saved 47 clicks this week with Dash" would reinforce the habit.

*Retention improvements:*
1. **First-day tutorial overlay** — "Press Cmd+K to try it now" on first admin page load
2. **Weekly stats (optional)** — "You searched 23 times this week. Top searches: settings, posts, users."

### 4. Pinned Notes (WordPress Sticky Notes)

**Retention Status: MODERATE**

*Current hooks:*
- **@mentions create inbox** — If someone mentions you, you have to come back
- **Note aging** — Visual urgency (stale notes) prompts action
- **Team coordination** — If the team uses it, you must use it

*What's missing:*
- **No digest/summary.** "You have 3 unread notes from this week."
- **No resolution tracking.** Notes get archived, but there's no "You resolved 5 notes this week" celebration.

*Retention improvements:*
1. **Daily digest email (opt-in)** — "3 notes mention you. 2 notes are aging."
2. **Resolution streaks** — "Your team closed 12 notes this week. Best week ever."

## Cross-Portfolio Retention Patterns

### Pattern 1: Notification Gaps
Only LocalGenius has a clear notification loop (Monday digest). Other products are "open when you remember."

**Fix:** Every product needs at least one regular touchpoint — email, notification, or in-app prompt.

### Pattern 2: No Progress Narrative
Products show snapshots, not stories. "340 visits" vs. "340 visits, up 22% from last week, your best month yet."

**Fix:** Add trend lines, comparisons, and narrative framing to all metrics.

### Pattern 3: No Celebration
None of these products celebrate milestones. Users achieve things, but the product doesn't notice.

**Fix:** Add milestone badges, streaks, and achievement notifications.

### Pattern 4: No Cliffhangers
The weekly digest ends. The project ships. The note archives. Nothing says "tune in next week."

**Fix:** Every touchpoint should end with a forward hook: "Next week, we'll try X" or "Coming soon: Y."

---

## The Retention Formula

**Habit + Celebration + Cliffhanger = Retention**

1. **Habit** — A regular action that becomes automatic (Cmd+K, Monday digest check)
2. **Celebration** — Recognition when they achieve something (milestone, streak)
3. **Cliffhanger** — A reason to come back (trend preview, upcoming feature)

Most products in this portfolio have one element. None have all three.

## Shonda's Verdict

**You're ending episodes. You should be creating cliffhangers.**

Every product in this portfolio delivers value and then... stops. The site ships. The digest arrives. The note resolves. End of story.

But people don't come back for endings. They come back for "what happens next?"

The Monday digest shouldn't end with "Here's your week." It should end with "I'm going to try something new next week. I'll let you know how it goes."

The Shipyard project shouldn't end with "Your site is live." It should end with "Three features we didn't have time for. Ready when you are."

**The fix isn't engineering. It's storytelling.**

Add the hook. End every interaction with a reason to return. Make them wonder what's coming next.

LocalGenius has the best retention opportunity because it already has a weekly touchpoint. The digest just needs to become a chapter in an ongoing story, not a standalone report.
