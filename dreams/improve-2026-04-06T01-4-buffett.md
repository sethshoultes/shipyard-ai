That's not fatal, but it's urgent. Let me analyze each product's revenue potential.

## Product-by-Product Revenue Analysis

### 1. LocalGenius — Highest Revenue Potential

**Business Model Clarity:** LOW
- No visible pricing
- No stated revenue model (subscription? usage-based? freemium?)
- Value proposition is strong but monetization is invisible

**Revenue Opportunity Assessment:**

| Model | Potential ARR/Customer | Market Size | Feasibility |
|-------|----------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Monthly SaaS ($99-299/mo) | $1,188-$3,588/year | 30M+ local businesses in US | HIGH |
| Freemium + Premium ($0 → $199/mo) | $2,388/year (if converts) | Larger funnel, lower ARPU | MEDIUM |
| Pay-per-post/action | Variable | Hard to predict | LOW |
| White-label for agencies | $500-2000/mo | Smaller market, higher ARPU | MEDIUM |

**My Recommendation:** SaaS at $149/month with annual discount to $1,188/year.

**Why:** Local business owners understand monthly software costs. $149/mo is less than one part-time employee hour. Annual pre-pay improves cash flow and reduces churn.

**Unit Economics Target:**
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): <$300
- Lifetime Value (LTV): >$2,000 (assumes 18-month average retention)
- LTV:CAC ratio: >6:1
- Gross margin: >80%

**Path to $1M ARR:** ~700 paying customers at $1,428 ARPU (blended annual)

### 2. Shipyard AI — Clear Revenue Model, Needs Volume

**Business Model Clarity:** MEDIUM
- Token-based pricing is stated but not in dollars
- Project-based revenue (not recurring)
- No maintenance/support tier mentioned

**Revenue Opportunity Assessment:**

| Model | Revenue/Project | Annual Potential | Notes |
|-------|----------------|------------------|-------|
| Project-based ($2,000-$15,000) | One-time | Depends on volume | Current model |
| Retainer/Maintenance ($200-500/mo) | $2,400-$6,000/year | Recurring | Not yet offered |
| Site hosting ($50-100/mo) | $600-$1,200/year | High margin | Not yet offered |

**The Problem:** Project-based revenue is lumpy. You feast when projects come in, starve when they don't.

**My Recommendation:** Add **"Shipyard Care"** post-launch maintenance tier.

**Why:**
- Creates recurring revenue from one-time customers
- Increases LTV by 2-3x
- Provides ongoing data for pattern library (Jensen's moat point)
- Low marginal cost—mostly monitoring and minor updates

**Pricing:**
- Basic ($99/mo): Uptime monitoring, security updates, monthly report
- Pro ($249/mo): + Content updates (4 hours/mo), priority support
- Enterprise ($499/mo): + Dedicated account manager, same-day response

**Path to $1M ARR:**
- 50 projects/year at $8,000 average = $400K project revenue
- 30% convert to Care Pro = 15 customers × $2,988/year = $45K recurring
- After 3 years: 45 recurring customers × $3,000 = $135K ARR
- Need to scale project volume OR increase Care conversion

### 3. Dash — WordPress Plugin Revenue

**Business Model Clarity:** NONE
- Currently open source with no monetization
- No freemium tier
- No premium features mentioned

**Revenue Opportunity Assessment:**

| Model | Revenue Potential | Market Size | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------------|-------|
| Freemium (Free + Pro) | $5-15/mo per Pro user | ~40% of web runs WordPress | Standard plugin model |
| Agency license ($99-299/year) | Higher ARPU, smaller base | WP agencies | Better margins |
| White-label | $500+/year | Hosting companies | Highest ARPU |

**The Opportunity:** WordPress plugin economy is proven. Plugins like WP Rocket, Gravity Forms, and ACF generate $10M+ annually.

**My Recommendation:** Freemium with Pro tier at $59/year.

**Free Features (current):**
- Basic command palette
- Standard commands
- Local search

**Pro Features ($59/year):**
- AI-powered command suggestions
- Command analytics (time saved tracking)
- Custom command builder
- Team command sharing (agencies)
- Priority support

**Path to $1M ARR:**
- Need ~17,000 Pro customers at $59/year
- If 3% of free users convert (industry standard): need ~567,000 free users
- WordPress.org distribution is key—1M+ active installs is achievable for quality plugins

### 5. Great Minds Plugin — The Crown Jewel

**Business Model Clarity:** UNCLEAR
- Installable via `npx plugins add`
- 15 skills available
- No stated pricing

**Revenue Opportunity Assessment:**

This is the highest-value asset but hardest to monetize directly.

| Model | Revenue Potential | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| One-time license ($499-999) | Low recurring | Common for dev tools |
| Subscription ($99-299/mo) | Better but limited market | Requires ongoing value |
| API metering | Scales with usage | Complex to implement |
| Consulting/implementation | High ARPU | Doesn't scale |

**The Insight:** Great Minds is infrastructure, not a product. The *products it builds* are the revenue source.

**My Recommendation:** Don't monetize Great Minds directly. Monetize the services it enables:
- LocalGenius subscriptions
- Shipyard projects
- Future products

**However:** If you want to license the technology:
- **Agency License ($2,500/year):** Use Great Minds to build for clients
- **Enterprise License ($10,000+/year):** Customization + support
- **Revenue share:** 5% of products built with Great Minds (hard to enforce)
