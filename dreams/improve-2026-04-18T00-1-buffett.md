## Product-by-Product Analysis

### 1. LocalGenius (localgenius.company)

**Revenue Status: ACTIVE**
- Base: $29/month
- Pro: $79/month
- Franchise: $79/location
- 14-day free trial

**Unit Economics Assessment:**

*Strengths:*
- LTV/CAC of 9.3x (from prior board review) is excellent
- $29 entry point is impulse-buy territory for SMBs
- Monthly billing creates predictable revenue
- Franchise pricing captures enterprise value

*Weaknesses:*
- **No annual discount.** You're leaving money on the table. Annual prepay at $249 ($20.75/month) gives you 12 months of cash today instead of hoping for 12 monthly payments.
- **No usage-based pricing.** A restaurant doing $5M/year should pay more than a food truck. You're charging the same for very different value delivery.
- **Missing expansion revenue.** Once a restaurant is on Base, what triggers Pro upgrade? The path isn't clear.

*Revenue improvement opportunities:*
1. **Annual pricing tier** — Offer 2 months free for annual prepay. This improves cash flow and signals customer commitment.
2. **Usage-based tier** — Add a "Growth" tier at $149/month for restaurants over certain transaction volume, with advanced features (multi-location dashboard, API access).
3. **Clear upgrade triggers** — In the Base tier, show Pro features as "locked" with visible value: "Upgrade to see which of your posts drove the most bookings."

**Investability Score: 7/10**
Good unit economics, clear market, reasonable pricing. Missing: expansion revenue path and annual billing.

### 2. Shipyard AI (shipyard.company)

**Revenue Status: ACTIVE (service-based)**
- Per-project token pricing
- Emdash sites: 500K-2M tokens
- Themes: 750K tokens
- Plugins: 500K tokens
- Revisions: 100K tokens per round

**Unit Economics Assessment:**

*Strengths:*
- Token pricing creates transparency
- Fixed scope = no scope creep
- Revisions are separately priced (smart)

*Weaknesses:*
- **No recurring revenue.** Each project is a one-time transaction. This is a service business, not a SaaS business.
- **No maintenance contracts.** After building a site, what's the ongoing relationship? Zero.
- **Token-to-dollar translation missing.** I can't evaluate margin without knowing what 500K tokens costs.
- **No retainer model.** Agencies would pay monthly for reserved capacity.

*Revenue improvement opportunities:*
1. **Maintenance plans** — $99/month for ongoing support, updates, and priority queue. Creates recurring revenue.
2. **Token packs** — Prepaid token bundles at discount: "Buy 5M tokens, get 500K free." Improves cash flow.
3. **Agency retainers** — $999/month for 2M tokens/month, unlimited projects within token budget. Locks in revenue.
4. **Publish dollar pricing** — Even if tokens fluctuate, show typical project ranges in dollars.

**Investability Score: 5/10**
Good technology, weak business model. Service businesses don't scale like software businesses. Needs recurring revenue layer.

### 3. Dash (WordPress Command Bar)

**Revenue Status: FREE (open source)**
- MIT license
- No monetization currently

**Monetization Opportunities:**

*The free model is appropriate for adoption.* But there are paths to revenue:

1. **Freemium + Pro** — Free: basic commands. Pro ($29/year): custom commands, recent items sync across sites, priority support.
2. **White-label licensing** — Agencies pay to rebrand Dash for their clients.
3. **WordPress.com partnership** — Get Dash pre-installed on WordPress.com hosting plans. Revenue share.
4. **Hosting bundle** — Partner with WP hosting providers. They bundle Dash Pro as a perk.

**Investability Score: 3/10**
No revenue, but high-quality free product builds credibility and distribution for other products. Consider as a marketing channel, not a revenue center.

## Warren's Verdict

**You've built products. You haven't built a business.**

LocalGenius is the only true business here. It has recurring revenue, clear unit economics, and a defined market. The rest are either service businesses (Shipyard) or open-source projects (Dash, Pinned, Great Minds).

I don't invest in service businesses. They don't compound. You trade time for money, and you can't sell that.

**My advice:**

1. **Focus on LocalGenius.** It's the only SaaS with the potential to reach $1M+ ARR.
2. **Add annual billing immediately.** It's the simplest revenue improvement you can make this week.
3. **Convert Shipyard to recurring.** Maintenance plans turn one-time buyers into monthly subscribers.
4. **Stop building free plugins.** Dash and Pinned Notes are nice, but they don't build equity value. If you must keep them, use them as top-of-funnel for LocalGenius or Shipyard.

The question isn't "what should we build next?" The question is "what gets us to $1M ARR fastest?" Everything else is a distraction.
