# Oprah Winfrey — Board Review: New User Confusion & First-5-Minutes
**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Review Cycle:** IMPROVE

---

## Executive Summary

I walked through each product as if I were a first-time visitor. My verdict: **we're building for ourselves, not for our users.** The products assume knowledge we haven't earned the right to assume. The first 5 minutes determine whether someone becomes a customer or bounces forever—and right now, we're losing too many in those critical moments.

---

## Product-by-Product First-5-Minutes Audit

### 1. LocalGenius (localgenius.company)

**First Impression:** Clean, confident messaging. "You didn't open a restaurant to do marketing" lands emotionally.

**The Confusion Points:**

| Minute | What I'm Thinking | What's Missing |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| 0:30 | "This sounds good, but what does it actually look like?" | No product screenshots or demo video |
| 1:00 | "How much does this cost?" | No pricing anywhere |
| 2:00 | "Is this just for restaurants or all local businesses?" | Messaging leans heavily restaurant, unclear if my salon qualifies |
| 3:00 | "What if I already have a website?" | No explanation of what happens to existing assets |
| 4:00 | "Can I talk to someone before committing?" | No chat, no phone, no clear human touchpoint |

**Trust Gaps:**
- No customer testimonials
- No "as seen in" or media mentions
- No faces—no team, no founder, no story
- No case studies showing before/after

**Emotional Journey:** Curiosity → Interest → Hesitation → Abandonment

**Fix:** Add a 60-second demo video showing actual product UI. Show real customer faces with real results. Add pricing (even if it's "starting at $X"). Create a "Is LocalGenius right for me?" quiz that qualifies different business types.

### 2. Shipyard AI (www.shipyard.company)

**First Impression:** "PRD in. Production out." is clever but intimidating.

**The Confusion Points:**

| Minute | What I'm Thinking | What's Missing |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| 0:30 | "What's a PRD? Do I need to know how to write one?" | No explanation of PRD for non-technical users |
| 1:00 | "What does a token budget mean in dollars?" | No price translation |
| 2:00 | "Show me something you've built" | No portfolio gallery |
| 3:00 | "How long does this take?" | No timeline estimates |
| 4:00 | "What if I don't like what you build?" | Revision process unclear |

**Trust Gaps:**
- Feels like it's for developers, not business owners
- No visible completed projects to prove quality
- "100% ship rate" is a claim without evidence
- No founder story or "why we built this"

**Emotional Journey:** Intrigue → Confusion → "This isn't for me" → Exit

**Fix:** Add a "PRD Template" download that shows exactly what to submit. Create a portfolio page with 5+ completed projects (screenshots, metrics). Add a "How It Works" video showing the actual process. Translate tokens to dollar ranges: "A marketing site typically costs $X-$Y."

## Cross-Product Patterns

### The Three Universal Problems

1. **No Pricing Transparency**
Every product hides or omits pricing. This creates anxiety and signals "expensive" or "not for me."

2. **No Visual Proof**
We describe features instead of showing them. Screenshots, videos, and GIFs are missing across the board.

3. **No Human Connection**
No founder story, no team photos, no customer faces. These products feel like they came from nowhere.

| 1 | **Add demo videos to LocalGenius and Shipyard** | Converts curious visitors into engaged prospects | MEDIUM |
| 2 | **Publish Dash and Pinned to WordPress.org** | Eliminates installation friction, adds social proof via reviews | LOW |
| 3 | **Create "Is This For Me?" quizzes/qualifiers** | Reduces confusion, increases conversion quality | MEDIUM |

## The Emotional Truth

People don't buy products. They buy **clarity**, **confidence**, and **connection**.

Right now:
- **Clarity:** We're at 4/10. Too much assumed knowledge.
- **Confidence:** We're at 5/10. Claims without proof.
- **Connection:** We're at 3/10. No humans visible anywhere.

The technical excellence is there. The empathy for new users is not.

---

## My Recommendation

Before building any new features, spend one sprint on **"The Welcome Mat"**:
- Demo videos for LocalGenius and Shipyard
- WordPress.org listings for Dash and Pinned
- Customer testimonials (even 2-3 would help)
- Founder story / "Why We Built This" on each product
- Pricing transparency (ranges are fine)

This isn't glamorous work, but it's the difference between "interesting product" and "my product."
