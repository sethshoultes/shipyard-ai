# Board Votes
## Dream Cycle: 2026-04-29T07-2
---

## Steve's Pick: Aperture
**Justification:** Aperture is the purest expression of what technology should feel like: a chore disappears and a moment of creative joy takes its place. The featured-image hunt is universal pain — every writer, every marketer, every agency feels it. Aperture replaces that pain with magic. The interaction is a single button. The result is a beautiful, unique image that matches the brand. It is emotionally resonant in a way that infrastructure products never are. And it ships in one session because the surface area is small: a sidebar button, a Worker call, a media library upload. Small surface, huge delight.

---

## Elon's Pick: Relay
**Justification:** Relay attacks the largest untapped intersection in the WordPress ecosystem: every site has a form, and none of them are intelligent. The TAM is essentially the entire WordPress install base. The technical architecture is solved — REST capture, stateless Worker, Claude classification, email/Slack routing — and we have built every piece of this pipeline before. The freemium model creates a perpetual acquisition engine through wordpress.org. Unit economics are immediate: free tier drives volume, paid tier unlocks AI. This is a painkiller, not a vitamin, and the build risk is near zero.

---

## Phil's Pick: Relay
**Justification:** This is the triangle offense in product form. Relay fuses our three proven strengths into one play: WordPress plugin speed (Dash, Pinned), AI integration (Claude + Workers AI), and SaaS pricing mechanics. Every other idea uses one or two strengths; Relay uses all three simultaneously. It is dogfood-able on our own contact forms. It ships in one session because the scope is constrained: capture, classify, route, display. No multi-tenant SaaS complexity, no migration edge-case labyrinth. The plugin lives on WP.org and sells itself. When every strength touches the ball, we win.

---

## Naval's Pick: Relay
**Justification:** Distribution is the only moat that matters, and Relay has the best distribution mechanics of the cycle. A freemium plugin on wordpress.org is a search engine with a billion update checks per month. Every install is a qualified lead. Every form submission is a usage event that reminds the user the product exists. The upgrade path is natural: once a user sees AI correctly route a high-value lead, the $9/month upgrade is obvious. This creates a compounding acquisition loop that no landing page or ad campaign can match. Relay is not just a product; it is a self-fueling distribution engine.

---

## Jensen's Pick: Beacon
**Justification:** The future of software is observable, and WordPress is the largest unobservable platform on Earth. Beacon plants our flag in telemetry infrastructure — a category with higher margins, stickier contracts, and clearer enterprise value than consumer-facing plugins. Error patterns and performance traces are training data for the next generation of AI-powered debugging. We already have the Worker orchestration patterns; Beacon simply points them at a new signal source. Infrastructure compounds. Every site that installs Beacon makes the product smarter for the next site. This is where the industry is heading, and we should own the WordPress telemetry layer before Datadog notices it.
