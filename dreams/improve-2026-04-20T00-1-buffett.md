## Portfolio Assessment

I look at businesses through one lens: **Will this generate durable, growing cash flows?** Let me assess each product.

---

### 1. LocalGenius (localgenius.company)
**Revenue Grade: A-**
**Investability: STRONG**

**What I Like:**
- **Unit economics are clean.** LTV/CAC of 9.3x at base case churn (per previous board review). Three scenarios, all above the 3:1 minimum. This is fundable.
- **Recurring revenue.** $29/month and $79/month subscriptions create predictable cash flow.
- **Clear ICP.** Restaurants with 1-5 locations. Specific enough to market efficiently, broad enough to scale.
- **Low CAC channels.** Email sequences (Day 3, Day 7, Day 14) are organic, not paid acquisition.
- **Multi-location pricing.** $79/location creates natural expansion revenue without new acquisition cost.

**Revenue Gaps:**
- **No annual pricing.** Monthly-only billing increases churn risk and reduces cash flow predictability. Annual plans with 2-month discount would improve retention AND front-load revenue.
- **Missing middle tier.** $29 → $79 is a 171% jump. A $49 tier would capture "I want more but not everything" customers.
- **No enterprise tier.** Franchises with 20+ locations need volume pricing and dedicated support. This is low-hanging expansion revenue.
- **Zero affiliate/referral program.** Restaurant owners talk to restaurant owners. Word-of-mouth is the cheapest CAC — incentivize it.

**Revenue Opportunity:**
Add annual billing with 20% discount. If 30% of customers switch to annual at current MRR, that's 2.4 months of revenue pulled forward. More importantly, annual customers churn 40% less than monthly.

**Investability Assessment:**
LocalGenius is the portfolio's engine. Clean unit economics, clear market, growing TAM (restaurant tech is expanding). If I were writing a check, this is where it would go. The gap is *proof* — I need to see 6 months of cohort data showing retention.

### 2. Shipyard AI (shipyard.company)
**Revenue Grade: B-**
**Investability: MODERATE (with concerns)**

**What I Like:**
- **High-ticket transactions.** 500K–2M tokens per project = meaningful revenue per engagement.
- **Transparent pricing.** Customers know cost before work begins. Reduces sales friction.
- **100% ship rate.** If true, this is a massive differentiator. Agencies are notorious for overruns.
- **Low marginal cost.** AI does the work. Scaling doesn't require linear headcount growth.

**Revenue Gaps:**
- **No recurring revenue.** "PRD in, production out" is transactional. Customer ships, customer leaves. Where's the subscription?
- **Revisions are afterthought.** "100K per round" is positioned as add-on, not ongoing relationship.
- **No retainer model.** Why isn't there a "$X/month for Y revision rounds" subscription? This is the agency model that actually builds wealth.
- **Pricing is confusing.** "500K–2M tokens" — is that $500 or $5,000? Customers want dollars, not tokens. Convert to dollar ranges in marketing.
- **No visible pipeline.** How many PRDs are in queue? How long is the wait? Capacity constraints could become revenue constraints.

**Revenue Opportunity:**
Launch a "Shipyard Maintenance" subscription: $500/month for 2 revision rounds + priority queue. This converts one-time customers into recurring revenue. Even 10 subscribers at $500/month = $60K/year predictable revenue.

**Investability Assessment:**
Shipyard is a *capability*, not a *business* yet. It can build anything, but it doesn't have durable cash flows. The moment work stops, revenue stops. I'd want to see a subscription layer before considering it investable.

**Verdict:** Not a revenue opportunity, but a valid marketing investment. Continue.

## Warren's Verdict

**LocalGenius is the business. Everything else is either a cost center or a lead source.**

The portfolio is correctly structured — one revenue engine (LocalGenius), one capability shop (Shipyard), two marketing assets (Dash/Pinned), one internal tool (Great Minds). The mistake would be trying to monetize everything.
