## Portfolio Assessment

I write stories that make people come back every week. Products need the same thing — **a reason to return tomorrow**. Let me look at each product's retention mechanics.

---

### 1. LocalGenius (localgenius.company)
**Retention Grade: B+**

**Current Retention Hooks:**
- **Weekly digests.** Every Sunday, restaurant owners get a summary of what happened. This creates a rhythm — a recurring appointment with the product.
- **Review notifications.** When a customer leaves a review, LocalGenius alerts the owner and drafts a response. This is reactive engagement — the product reaches out when something happens.
- **Insight persistence.** The `insight_actions` table tracks what suggestions the owner approved or rejected. Over time, LocalGenius gets smarter. Leaving means losing that learning.
- **Social proof loop.** "Here's what I posted for you this week" creates accountability. The owner sees value delivered even when they didn't open the app.

**What's Missing:**

1. **No streaks or progress visualization.**
   - Where's "You've responded to 47 reviews this month — your best month yet"?
   - Where's "Your Instagram engagement is up 12% since you started"?
   - Numbers alone aren't retention. *Trends* are retention.

2. **No milestone celebrations.**
   - 100 reviews responded? That deserves confetti.
   - First post that got 50 likes? Tell the owner.
   - 6 months as a customer? Send a personalized "look how far you've come" email.

3. **No cliffhangers.**
   - Great TV ends episodes with unresolved tension. Where's the "You have 3 pending suggestions — which will you try this week?"
   - Where's "Your competitor just updated their Google Business Profile — want to see what changed?"

4. **No community.**
   - Restaurant owners are lonely. They don't have peers who understand. A "LocalGenius Owners" Slack or monthly Zoom would create belonging beyond the product.

**Retention Opportunity:**
Add a "This Month vs. Last Month" comparison to the weekly digest. Show: reviews responded (+/-), posts published (+/-), engagement rate (+/-). This creates a story arc — am I winning or losing? What do I do next?

### 2. Shipyard AI (shipyard.company)
**Retention Grade: C**

**Current Retention Hooks:**
- **Revisions.** "100K per round" means customers can come back for updates. But this is positioned as transactional, not relational.
- **Quality memory.** The Great Minds pipeline remembers past projects. Returning customers get context continuity.

**What's Missing:**

1. **No ongoing relationship.**
   - Ship and goodbye. Where's the "How's the site performing?" check-in 30 days after launch?
   - Where's the "We noticed your traffic is up 20% — here's why"?

2. **No notification of relevance.**
   - When a shipped site has issues (downtime, broken links, SEO drop), Shipyard should *tell the customer*. This creates ongoing value beyond the initial build.

3. **No portfolio effect.**
   - If I've shipped 3 projects with Shipyard, where's my "Shipyard Partner" status?
   - Where's the loyalty discount?
   - Where's the "here's what we've built together" dashboard?

4. **No content to return for.**
   - No blog. No case studies. No "how we built X" behind-the-scenes. There's nothing to read, nothing to follow, nothing to share.

**Retention Opportunity:**
Launch a "Post-Launch Pulse" — a monthly automated check on shipped sites (uptime, SEO basics, broken links). Email customers with a health report. This creates ongoing touchpoints and positions Shipyard as a partner, not a vendor.

### 3. Dash (WP Command Bar)
**Retention Grade: B**

**Current Retention Hooks:**
- **Daily utility.** If you use WordPress admin daily, Cmd+K becomes muscle memory. The product retains through habit, not delight.
- **Recent items.** Personalized shortcuts based on usage create switching cost.

**What's Missing:**

1. **No "time saved" feedback.**
   - "You've used Dash 127 times this month" — so what?
   - "You've saved approximately 22 minutes this week" — now I care.

2. **No progressive mastery.**
   - Where's "You've only used search. Did you know about command mode?"
   - Where's "Power user tip: Type > settings to jump directly to WordPress settings"?

3. **No social proof.**
   - "Dash users navigate WordPress 3x faster" — but I'll never see this stat.

**Retention Opportunity:**
Add a monthly email (opt-in): "Your Dash Stats" — commands used, time saved (estimated), power tips you haven't tried yet.

### 4. Pinned (WP Sticky Notes)
**Retention Grade: A-**

**Current Retention Hooks:**
- **Daily touchpoint.** Dashboard widget means every WP admin login shows Pinned notes.
- **Team accountability.** @mentions create social obligation — someone tagged me, I should respond.
- **Note aging.** Visual decay of old notes creates urgency — resolve it or it fades.
- **Historical memory.** Old notes stay visible, creating organizational continuity.

**What's Missing:**

1. **No weekly digest.**
   - "This week on your team: 12 notes created, 8 resolved, 3 overdue" — this creates a story of team productivity.

2. **No "most active" recognition.**
   - Who created the most notes? Who resolved the most? Gentle gamification creates engagement.

3. **No archive review.**
   - "6 months ago today, you pinned: [note]" — nostalgia is a retention hook.

**Retention Opportunity:**
Add a Friday "Week in Pinned" summary: notes created, notes resolved, longest-standing note. This creates a team ritual.

### 5. Great Minds Plugin
**Retention Grade: A**

**Current Retention Hooks:**
- **Retrospectives after every project.** The system learns from itself.
- **Memory store.** Learnings persist across projects.
- **Scoreboard.** 19 projects shipped creates momentum and pride.
- **DO-NOT-REPEAT patterns.** Past failures prevent future failures.

**Assessment:**
Great Minds has the best retention mechanics in the portfolio — for its "users" (the agents and the team). The pipeline *wants* to be used because it gets better with use.

## Top 3 Retention Priorities

**Gap:** Weekly digests show what happened, not the trend
**Fix:** Add "This Month vs. Last Month" comparison showing reviews, posts, engagement
**Impact:** Creates narrative arc — users see themselves progressing
**Effort:** LOW (data exists, just needs visualization)
**Timeline:** 1 week

### Priority 2: Shipyard Post-Launch Pulse (HIGH)
**Gap:** No ongoing relationship after ship
**Fix:** Monthly automated health check email (uptime, SEO, broken links)
**Impact:** Converts transactional relationship to ongoing partnership
**Effort:** MEDIUM (monitoring + email automation)
**Timeline:** 2 weeks

### Priority 3: LocalGenius Milestone Celebrations (MEDIUM)
**Gap:** No celebration of customer wins
**Fix:** Automated celebrations for: 100 reviews, 6-month anniversary, best month ever
**Impact:** Creates emotional connection and shareable moments
**Effort:** LOW (trigger-based emails)
**Timeline:** 1 week

## Shonda's Verdict

**The portfolio delivers value but doesn't create anticipation.**

Good products solve problems. Great products make you wonder what happens next. LocalGenius tells you what it did — it should be hinting at what it *could* do. Shipyard ships and disappears — it should become a trusted advisor who checks in.

**The fix is narrative, not features.** Show progress over time. Celebrate milestones. Create rhythms. Give people a reason to open the app even when they don't need something.

**One thing to fix this week:** Add one line to the LocalGenius weekly digest: "Compared to last month: [+/- reviews] [+/- engagement]." That's it. One line. It changes the email from a report into a story.
