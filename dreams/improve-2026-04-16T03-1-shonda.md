## The Retention Question

Every product competes with the same enemy: **forgetting you exist.**

A user installs your plugin, signs up for your service, pays for your product. Then life happens. They get busy. A week passes. A month passes. Did they come back?

Great products don't wait for users to remember. They create reasons to return.

---

## Product-by-Product Retention Analysis

### LocalGenius: The Monday Morning Ritual

**The Hook:** Weekly Digest

Every Monday morning, LocalGenius delivers a 90-second summary: what happened last week, what's performing, what to do this week. This is brilliant retention design.

**Why It Works:**
- **Recurring calendar anchor.** Monday morning isn't a random notification—it's a cultural moment. "What do I need to know this week?"
- **Tangible value without effort.** The user didn't log in to generate this. It appeared. They opened email and got smarter.
- **Actionable recommendations.** Not just metrics, but "here's what to do about it."

**What Could Be Stronger:**
- **No escalation arc.** Week 1 and week 52 feel the same. Great TV shows build toward something. What's the "season finale" for a LocalGenius user?
- **No streaks or milestones.** "You've been consistent for 12 weeks" creates commitment. "Week 12 of 52" creates narrative momentum.
- **No comparison hooks.** "Other Austin restaurants are posting 3× more frequently" creates competitive motivation.

**Retention Grade:** A- — Strong recurring hook, needs progression narrative.

---

### Shipyard AI: One-Shot, No Return

**The Problem:** Shipyard is project-based. You come, you ship, you leave.

There's no reason to return unless you have another project. That's not retention—that's repeat purchase.

**Why This Matters:**
- **Memory decay.** A client who used Shipyard 6 months ago might not remember when they need their next site.
- **No relationship building.** Each project is a transaction, not a story.
- **Competitive vulnerability.** If another agency appears at the right moment, Shipyard has no lock-in.

**Retention Opportunities:**

1. **Post-Launch Health Reports.** Monthly: "Your site shipped 90 days ago. Here's how it's performing: uptime, speed, SEO changes." This keeps Shipyard in the conversation.

2. **"Your Stack Is Aging" Alerts.** "The WordPress version on your site is now 2 versions behind. Want us to update it?" Creates reason to return with revenue attached.

3. **Anniversary Touchpoints.** "One year ago, we built your site together. Here's what's changed in web standards since then." Narrative closure that opens next chapter.

**Retention Grade:** D — No retention mechanics. Each project is an ending, not a beginning.

---

### Dash: Daily Tool, No Depth

**The Hook:** Cmd+K becomes muscle memory.

If Dash works reliably, power users will press Cmd+K 20+ times per day. That's not retention—that's utility. But utility alone doesn't create loyalty.

**Current State:**
- Dash is broken (P0 bugs), so retention is zero.
- Even if fixed, Dash is invisible infrastructure. You don't "engage" with Dash—you use it.

**The Opportunity:** Dash could surface **insights about your WordPress usage**.

- "You've searched for 'orders' 47 times this week. Want a quick action to jump directly to WooCommerce orders?"
- "Your most-accessed pages: Dashboard, Posts, Plugins. Consider pinning these."
- "You type 'settings' a lot. Here's a keyboard shortcut to jump directly there."

This transforms Dash from "invisible utility" to "tool that knows you." That creates emotional connection.

**Retention Grade:** F — Currently broken. Even when fixed, no engagement layer.

---

### Pinned: Collaboration Creates Obligation

**The Hook:** @mentions create return triggers.

When someone @mentions you in a Pinned note, you get notified. You return to acknowledge. This is lightweight but real.

**Why It Works:**
- **Social obligation.** Your colleague wrote something for you. Ignoring it feels rude.
- **Visible acknowledgment.** Checkmarks show who's read what. Social proof of engagement.
- **Low friction.** One click to acknowledge. Doesn't derail your day.

**What Could Be Stronger:**

1. **Unacknowledged note reminders.** "3 notes are waiting for your acknowledgment" creates urgency without anxiety.

2. **Note aging as narrative.** "This note has been on the board for 14 days. Archive it or act on it." Creates closure pressure.

3. **Weekly team digest.** "Your team created 12 notes this week. 8 were acknowledged. 4 are still open." Dashboard-level visibility creates collective accountability.

4. **Pinned history.** "What was on the board this time last month?" Narrative callback creates continuity.

**Retention Grade:** B — Social hooks exist, need amplification.

---

### Great Minds Plugin: Builder's Retention

**The Hook:** Retrospectives and memory.

Every shipped project generates a Marcus Aurelius retrospective. The memory store persists learnings. The token ledger tracks efficiency over time.

**Why It Works (For The Right User):**
- **Progress is visible.** Scoreboard shows 20 projects shipped, 250+ commits. This is a career narrative.
- **Learning compounds.** Each project adds to the memory store. The system literally gets smarter.
- **Ownership story.** "I built this with my agent team" is identity-forming.

**What Could Be Stronger:**

1. **Milestone celebrations.** "Project #20 shipped! Here's your journey from project #1 to now." Narrative arc visibility.

2. **Agent relationship deepening.** "Margaret has filed 80 QA reports for you. Here's her hit rate over time." Makes agents feel like colleagues, not tools.

3. **Comparative efficiency.** "Your last 5 projects averaged 23% fewer tokens than your first 5." Proof of growth.

**Retention Grade:** B+ — Strong for power users. Progression narrative needs surfacing.

## The Missing Narrative Layer

Every great TV show answers: **"What happens next?"**

- LocalGenius asks this weekly (digest) but doesn't build toward a season
- Great Minds tracks progress but doesn't celebrate milestones
- Pinned has social obligation but no collective arc
- Dash and Shipyard have no "next" at all

**Retention isn't about features. Retention is about making users curious about their own future with your product.**

---

## Three Retention Improvements

### 1. LocalGenius: "Your Marketing Year" Dashboard

Add a narrative arc to the weekly digests:

- Week-over-week trend visualization
- "Best performing month of your first year"
- Milestone celebrations (100th review response, 1,000th social post)
- Year-end "Marketing Rewind" (like Spotify Wrapped)

**Why:** Creates long-term narrative investment. Users want to see how their story unfolds.

### 2. Shipyard: Post-Ship Lifecycle Emails

Don't end the relationship at deployment:

- Day 30: "Your site is 30 days old. Here's performance data."
- Day 90: "Security update recommended. One-click to schedule."
- Day 365: "Happy anniversary. Here's how the web has changed."

**Why:** Transforms project transactions into ongoing relationships.

### 3. Pinned: Weekly Team Digest

Add org-wide visibility:

- "Your team created 12 notes this week"
- "3 notes have been pinned for 7+ days—time to resolve?"
- "Most mentioned teammate: @sarah"

**Why:** Creates collective accountability and team narrative.

---

## The Deeper Pattern

**Products that retain users tell them a story about themselves.**

- "You're becoming a better marketer" (LocalGenius)
- "Your team is getting more aligned" (Pinned)
- "You're shipping faster than last month" (Great Minds)

Products without retention give you a tool. Products with retention give you a **mirror that shows your progress.**

---

## Verdict

LocalGenius has the strongest retention mechanics in the portfolio—the Weekly Digest is a genuine return trigger with real value. But even LocalGenius is missing the long-arc narrative that creates year-over-year engagement.

Shipyard and Dash have no retention whatsoever. Every interaction is potentially the last interaction. That's not a business—that's a transaction.

**Focus retention work on:**
1. **Shipyard post-ship lifecycle** (largest gap, highest impact)
2. **LocalGenius progression milestones** (strengthen existing strength)
3. **Pinned weekly digest** (unlock team-level engagement)
