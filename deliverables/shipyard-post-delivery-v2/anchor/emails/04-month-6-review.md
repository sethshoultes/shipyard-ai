## Voice Notes

This is a relationship maintenance email, not a conversion email.

At 6 months, the client has settled into their site. They're either happy (great) or they've moved on mentally (we want to re-engage them).

The soft CTA strategy here is intentional:
- We mention pricing but don't push
- We offer to "chat about what's next" — low pressure
- We're planting seeds for renewal conversations

This email sets up potential annual planning conversations. If they're thinking about next year, we want to be part of that thinking.

---

## What Success Looks Like

A client who receives this email should:
1. Feel a moment of "oh wow, it's been 6 months already"
2. Appreciate that Shipyard is still thinking about them
3. Maybe reply with thoughts about the year ahead
4. Not feel sold to — even though maintenance is mentioned

## Why Month 6 Instead of Day 90?

Per decisions.md, Template 4 (originally Quarter 1 Report at Day 90) was cut/made optional.

Month 6 replaces it because:
- 90 days is awkward — not quite a quarter, not quite "a while"
- 6 months feels like a real milestone
- It's close to potential annual budget planning
- We want one touchpoint in the second half of the year

The sequence is now: Day 0 → Day 7 → Day 30 → Month 6

This gives us:
- 3 touchpoints in the first month (high engagement)
- 1 touchpoint at half-year (milestone + renewal prep)
