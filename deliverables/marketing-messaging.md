You write a PRD. We build and deploy a production-ready Emdash site. No meetings, no revisions, no waiting. You get a finished product with the quality of a senior engineering team, at a fraction of the cost of a traditional dev shop — and we deliver it in weeks, not months.

## Value Propositions

- **Speed without sacrifice.** PRD to live site in weeks. We're not cutting corners — we're cutting meetings. Every site passes QA and accessibility standards before it ships.

- **Predictable cost, zero scope creep.** Token-based pricing means you know exactly what you're getting and what it costs. No surprise invoices. Changes after launch? That's a separate job with transparent pricing.

- **Quality that rivals senior teams.** Built by an autonomous AI agency optimized for shipping. You get the decisions of architects, the rigor of QA, the eye for detail of designers — compressed into a pipeline that doesn't sleep.

- **Emdash-first by design.** We don't retrofit generic templates. We build for Emdash primitives, leverage its platform, and deliver sites that sing on the platform they're built for.

## Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "AI-built sites feel generic and cheap."
**Response:** "The site is built by AI. The taste is human. We don't use templates — we design for your business. You'll have access to every decision we made. If you don't like it, you've got revision tokens to change it. But most clients are surprised by the quality."

### Objection 2: "We need customization. Your process sounds rigid."
**Response:** "The PRD is your canvas. Every decision you put in the PRD gets built. The process isn't rigid — it's focused. We don't spec a site in meetings. You spec it once in writing, we build it, you deploy it. Changes? That's revision tokens, same price model, same speed."

### Objection 3: "What if we want to make changes after launch?"
**Response:** "Changes after launch are revisions. They cost 100K tokens per round. That's about $100-500 depending on scope. You request changes in writing, we build them, you deploy. Same pipeline. Same quality bar. No endless email threads."

### Objection 4: "How do I know the site will actually work? What's the guarantee?"
**Response:** "Every site we build passes automated testing, accessibility checks, and manual QA before it ships. You get a QA report. If something breaks in production, that's on us — we'll fix it. But that doesn't happen because we don't ship broken code."

### Objection 5: "Can't I just hire a freelancer cheaper?"
**Response:** "A freelancer might cost less per hour. But you'll spend 3-6 months in Slack, waiting for status updates, explaining requirements, asking for revisions. We charge flat tokens, deliver in 4-6 weeks, and never miss a deadline. Total cost of ownership? We're a steal."

## Words We Use

**Brand language that reinforces who we are:**

- **Ship** — We ship. We don't "launch" or "roll out." Shipping is intentional, fast, and final.
- **Deploy** — Sites go live via git push. Production is real. Staging is practice.
- **PRD** — The contract. If it's not in the PRD, it doesn't get built.
- **Pipeline** — Our competitive advantage. The speed and consistency of the system.
- **Tokens** — Our unit of work. Transparent. Measurable. Fair.
- **QA** — Non-negotiable. Not optional. Every ship passes QA.
- **Revision** — Changes after launch. Explicit. Priced. Separate job.
- **Built by Emdash** — We're not adapting a framework. We're leveraging Emdash.
- **Production-ready** — Not almost done. Not "just needs polish." Done.
- **Autonomous** — We don't wait for you. We don't need supervision.

---

## Words We Never Use

**Terms that undermine our brand:**

- **"AI-powered"** — Jargon. Vague. Everyone says it. We say "built by AI."
- **"Leverage"** — Corporate speak. We use and build, not leverage.
- **"Solution"** — Passive. We build sites, not solutions.
- **"Synergy"** — Never.
- **"Cutting-edge"** — Lazy. Show, don't claim.
- **"Seamless"** — Overused. Everything is just seamless now.
- **"Scalable"** — Not our pitch. We're not building infrastructure for millions.
- **"Best-in-class"** — Claim it and you look like everyone else. Show it and you don't need to say it.
- **"Innovative"** — See above.
- **"Strategic partner"** — We're a vendor. You're a client. Let's be honest.
- **"Eco-system"** — Gross.

By the time it ships, your market has moved on. We quote flat tokens, ship in 4-6 weeks, and cost a fraction. You could hire three Shipyard projects for the price of one agency engagement."

"A freelancer charges $50/hour and takes 200 hours. That's $10K and 5-6 months in your calendar. You're checking in weekly, you're blocked on decisions, you're watching the project drag. Shipyard is $1K-$10K flat, 4-6 weeks, and you move on with your business. No bottlenecks. No excuses."

**The pitch:** We're not competing on price — we're competing on total cost of ownership. Cheap + slow costs more than fast + transparent.

## Appendix: Voice & Tone Guidelines

- **Direct.** Short sentences. No run-ons. No hedging.
- **Confident.** We know what we do. We say it plainly.
- **No jargon.** If a business owner from 1985 wouldn't understand it, we don't say it.
- **Builder's voice.** We talk like engineers, not marketers. We show, we don't claim. We say "we shipped" not "we're proud to announce."
- **Honest.** We acknowledge trade-offs. We don't hide limitations. We say "this isn't for everyone" when it isn't.
- **Constraint-aware.** We talk about tokens, budgets, timelines as features, not problems. Constraints breed creativity.
